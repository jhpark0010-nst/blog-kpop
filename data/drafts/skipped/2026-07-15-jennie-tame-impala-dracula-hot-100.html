BLACKPINK's Jennie and Australian artist Tame Impala have set a new personal best on the Billboard Hot 100 with their collaboration 'Dracula.' According to Billboard on July 14 (local time), 'Dracula' climbed three spots from the previous week to reach No. 5, marking the song's highest chart position to date.

The track was originally released as a Tame Impala solo song in October of last year. A remix featuring Jennie was then unveiled this past February, and the new version gained popularity on short-form video platforms, fueling a chart resurgence.

The HYBE label girl group collaboration track 'Iconic By Mistake' by LE SSERAFIM, ILLIT, and KATSEYE dropped eleven spots from the previous week to No. 60, marking its fourth consecutive week on the chart. BTS's 'SWIM' charted at No. 84, extending its run to 16 consecutive weeks.

On the Billboard 200 album chart, ATEEZ's fourteenth mini-album 'GOLDEN HOUR : Part.5' fell eighteen spots from its No. 1 position the previous week to land at No. 19 this week.

Also on the Billboard 200, BTS's 'ARIRANG' came in at No. 25, the original soundtrack album for the Netflix animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters' placed at No. 29, CORTIS's 'GREENGREEN' charted at No. 82, and KATSEYE's 'Beautiful Chaos' landed at No. 141. SEVENTEEN's new unit V8 (THE8 and Vernon) debuted on the chart for the first time with their first mini-album 'V8' at No. 128.
