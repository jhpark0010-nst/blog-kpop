BTS has achieved another landmark record in London, UK. On July 6 and 7 (local time), BTS held their world tour 'BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG IN LONDON' at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in London, performing to approximately 130,000 fans across the two nights. All shows sold out the moment tickets went on sale, and according to concert organizer Live Nation, the performances set the record for the highest per-show audience in the history of Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, which opened in April 2019.

BTS continues to mark meaningful milestones in Korean music history every time they visit London. In June 2019, they became the first Korean artists to hold a solo concert at the iconic Wembley Stadium, leaving a significant mark on the global concert scene. Returning to London approximately seven years later, BTS once again made their presence felt by fully conquering Tottenham Hotspur Stadium.

Even before the concerts began, excitement among fans spread throughout London. Tottenham Hotspur Stadium also highlighted BTS's visit across its official channels. The venue introduced BTS as 'Pop Royalty,' and the Tottenham Hotspur official Korean-language account posted a video of 'SWIM' — the title track from BTS's fifth studio album ARIRANG — echoing grandly through the stadium, welcoming fans who had gathered from around the world.

Over the two nights, BTS delivered flawless live performances and explosive stage presence that kept the audience fully captivated. They opened with 'Hooligan,' the first track from their new album ARIRANG, then performed new songs including 'NORMAL' and 'Body to Body,' followed by global mega-hits such as 'IDOL' and 'FIRE,' setting the entire venue ablaze. The members shared their feelings, saying: 'We get to feel the emotion of Wembley Stadium from seven years ago once again. Thanks to your passion, we had a truly wonderful time today.' They added: 'Many memories of past performances came back to us, and we were once again surprised to find that London is such a passionate city.'

Having written yet another chapter of history in London, BTS will next move to Allianz Arena in Munich, Germany, performing on July 11 and 12 to continue the heat of their European tour. They are set to become the first Korean artists to perform at Allianz Arena, adding yet another unprecedented record to their name.
