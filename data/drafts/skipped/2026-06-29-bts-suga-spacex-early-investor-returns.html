BTS member Suga has reportedly been an early investor in U.S. aerospace company SpaceX. On June 29, Korean outlet Edaily reported that Suga invested in SpaceX while the company was still privately held, making him one of its early backers.

SpaceX's valuation grew from approximately $46 billion (around 71.2 trillion KRW) in 2020 to roughly $100 billion (around 155 trillion KRW) by 2021, following additional rounds of investment.

SpaceX made its debut on the New York Stock Exchange on June 12, with an IPO price of $135 per share (approximately 210,000 KRW). At listing, the company's market capitalization reached approximately $1.77 trillion (around 2,741 trillion KRW).

According to the report, Suga is believed to have invested in SpaceX before 2022. Based on that timeline, his returns are conservatively estimated at around 20 times his initial investment. If the investment was made around 2020, returns could be as high as approximately 40 times.

Meanwhile, Suga debuted with BTS in 2013. In 2023, he released his first solo studio album 'D-DAY' and began his alternative military service as a social service worker the same year. He was discharged in June of last year.
