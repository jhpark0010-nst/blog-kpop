BLACKPINK's Jennie and Australian artist Tame Impala have held their peak position on the Billboard main singles chart, the Hot 100. According to Billboard on July 21 (local time), their collaboration track 'Dracula' charted at No. 5 for the second consecutive week, matching its position from the previous week.

The song was originally released in October of last year as a Tame Impala solo track. A remix version featuring Jennie was then unveiled in February of this year, gaining popularity on short-form video platforms and climbing back up the chart.

Riding the momentum, Jennie is set to release a new single, 'Less than a Lover,' on July 24 at 1 p.m. The summer song is one that Jennie participated in writing the lyrics for and directing the music video.

'Iconic By Mistake,' a collaboration track by HYBE label girl groups LE SSERAFIM, ILLIT, and KATSEYE, charted at No. 71, dropping eleven spots from the previous week but marking its fifth consecutive week on the chart. BTS's 'SWIM' entered at No. 100, extending its run to 17 consecutive weeks on the chart.

On the Billboard main album chart, the Billboard 200, TOMORROW X TOGETHER member Yeonjun's second solo mini-album 'NO LABELS: PART 02' debuted at No. 16. Also on the Billboard 200, BTS's 'ARIRANG' charted at No. 25, the original soundtrack album for the Netflix animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters' at No. 31, and ATEEZ's 'GOLDEN HOUR: Part.5' at No. 54.

CORTIS's 'GREENGREEN' came in at No. 132, and KATSEYE's 'Beautiful Chaos' was counted at No. 138.
