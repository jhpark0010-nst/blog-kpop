BTS released their new single 'Come Over' at 1 p.m. on June 12, 2026, according to their agency Big Hit Music. The release coincides with the group's Busan concerts, the latest stop on their ARIRANG world tour.

'Come Over' was previously only available on the deluxe vinyl (LP) edition of BTS's fifth studio album ARIRANG, which was released on April 3. The track is now receiving an official digital release as part of the 2026 BTS FESTA event, held annually around June 13, the group's debut anniversary.

The song features production by member SUGA, with RM and j-hope also credited. It blends a stadium anthem sound with pop, combining spacious synthesizer textures and resonant vocals to create a grand, sweeping atmosphere.

In 'Come Over,' BTS sing about always finding their way back to 'you' even in moments of feeling lost. According to Big Hit Music, the honest confession at the end of that wandering is ultimately a message directed at ARMY, the group's fandom, who have stood by the members for so long.

Also released on June 12 is a new LP edition of ARIRANG titled the '613 Limited Edition Picture Disc Vinyl.' This vinyl includes all existing ARIRANG tracks plus bonus tracks — 'Voice Message: Love Song' and a Korean-language version of 'NORMAL' — for a total of 16 songs. During the 2026 BTS FESTA period celebrating the group's 13th debut anniversary, BTS also unveiled a range of content including a family photo, a 'Hooligan' performance video, and new episodes of 'Run BTS 2.0.'

BTS is holding their ARIRANG world tour concerts in Busan on June 12 and 13 at Asiad Main Stadium. June 13 is the group's debut anniversary, and Busan is the hometown of members Jungkook and Jimin, making the shows especially meaningful for both the members and fans. Additionally, BTS will release the music video for their fifth album track 'Merry Go Round' via Spotify on June 19 at 6 p.m.
