As a copyright infringement lawsuit filed by foreign songwriters over NewJeans' hit song 'How Sweet' has been confirmed, the group's label ADOR has issued an official statement on July 10.

Dispatch reported on July 10 that it had obtained a complaint filed with the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California, alleging that NewJeans' 'How Sweet' infringed on the copyright of 'One of a Kind,' a song created by American songwriter Audrey Amakostre and three other collaborators. According to the report, four American producer-songwriters filed a formal copyright infringement lawsuit against HYBE, ADOR, NewJeans members, as well as Bana — considered a key partner of Min Hee-jin's music circle — and songwriter 250, among others.

In response, an ADOR representative told MyDaily on July 10: 'The track in question is a song that was sourced through Bana, which was selected by then-CEO Min Hee-jin. We are currently reviewing internal documents to determine whether Min properly conducted a similarity review of the track at the time.'

According to the complaint, Bana — which functioned as an outsourced production company — delivered the instrumental track for 'How Sweet' to U.S. publishing company Pulse Music and commissioned topline writing. Audrey Amakostre and three other American musicians completed and submitted the topline, but Bana reportedly declined it, stating they would 'ultimately not be adopting it.' However, the plaintiffs claim that their work was used as-is in NewJeans' subsequently released song 'How Sweet,' which came out approximately four months later.

The credits on the released track list Min Hee-jin as executive producer and Kim Ki-hyun, head of Bana, as A&R director. In the complaint, the American musicians stated: 'Min Hee-jin has said, "Kim Ki-hyun is NewJeans. He produced all the songs."' They further argued: 'Since Kim Ki-hyun personally received our work, he cannot claim to be unaware of the song's existence.'
