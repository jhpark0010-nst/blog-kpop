(G)I-DLE, NMIXX, and CORTIS have been selected for Forbes' '30 Under 30 Asia 2026' list, the magazine's annual ranking of influential figures under 30 across Asia and the Pacific. The three K-pop acts were named in the Entertainment & Sports category, according to the music industry on May 29.

The Forbes '30 Under 30 Asia' list is published each year and highlights individuals who have demonstrated industry influence across Asia and the Pacific region.

(G)I-DLE and NMIXX gained recognition this year through their respective new releases — (G)I-DLE with their new single 'Mono,' and NMIXX with their new mini-album 'Heavy Serenade.'

CORTIS earned their spot after their single 'REDRED' reached No. 1 on Melon's Top 100 chart. The album containing the track, 'GREENGREEN,' sold 2.31 million copies in its first week and debuted at No. 3 on the Billboard 200, the main album chart in the United States.

Choreographer Lee Jung was also included on the list. Lee Jung created the performance for 'Soda Pop,' the original soundtrack from the Netflix animated series 'K-Pop Demon Hunters,' which became a global hit.
