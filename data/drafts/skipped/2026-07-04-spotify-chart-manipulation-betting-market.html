As online prediction betting platforms such as Polymarket and Kalshi have grown rapidly, incidents of music chart manipulation aimed at winning payouts have begun to emerge. According to CBS News and the Financial Times on July 3 (local time), Malcolm Todd's 2024 song 'Earrings' climbed quickly up Spotify's US chart, eventually reaching number one.

The chart rise was driven by a 70% surge in streaming counts between June 28 and 29. However, a Spotify investigation found that the spike was the result of so-called 'bots' — automated programs that repeatedly stream a track. The platform announced it would remove 500,000 streams from the song's count and adjust its ranking from number one down to number four.

The manipulation occurred during a period when suspicious bets were being placed on Kalshi regarding music chart positions. Bets had been placed on whether 'Earrings' would reach number one on Spotify's US chart before the end of June. The odds had initially been estimated at just 2.5%, but the artificially inflated ranking allowed those who placed bets to earn 20 times their investment.

The issue became public after Kalshi trader Caleb Davis posted about the suspicious betting outcome on X (formerly Twitter) on June 30. Spotify's subsequent investigation confirmed that manipulation had indeed taken place. Although Spotify corrected the chart rankings, the betting participants are reported to have already divided their winnings.

As the user base of online betting platforms has grown, problems such as insider trading and manipulation have surfaced alongside it. Previously, a soldier involved in the operation to arrest Venezuelan President Nicolás Maduro placed a bet on the mission's success and earned $400,000 in profit. Separately, an anomaly was detected in a Paris temperature gauge while bets were being placed on French temperatures.

The U.S. Commodity Futures Trading Commission (CFTC) released a draft regulation last month aimed at strengthening oversight of prediction markets, but criticism that the measures are insufficient continues to mount.
