On June 13, 2026 — BTS's 13th debut anniversary — fans from across the globe packed the area around Busan Asiad Main Stadium, where the group's world tour 'ARIRANG' was set to take place. Despite temperatures approaching 30°C, ARMY members gathered in large numbers from midday onward, their faces bright with excitement as they made their way to the venue in small groups after securing tickets through fierce competition.

Notably, many international fans arrived dressed in hanbok, matching the Korean-inspired concept of the 'ARIRANG' album and tour. Christine (29), who came from the Philippines, wore a white jeogori paired with a red skirt embroidered in gold. A clinical laboratory science major, she said she got through the grueling schedule of her university years by listening to BTS. 'My schedule in college was very demanding, and the pressure grew as graduation approached. I even felt a loneliness, as if I was falling behind on my own,' she said. 'Around that time, BTS's 'Dynamite' and 'Boy With Luv' caught my attention, and those songs gave me the strength to keep moving forward.'

She added, 'BTS's music is like a translator. It connects people from different countries who speak different languages through music. All of our hearts are linked in purple.'

BTS was expected to meet more than 100,000 ARMY members over two days at Busan Asiad Main Stadium. On the previous day, members gifted all audience members with sky-blue parasols and transparent bags, drawing a warm response. On this day, fans used those gifted parasols to shield themselves from the sun while waiting to enter. Fans wore 'ARIRANG' concert goods T-shirts, decorated their bags with keyrings and photocards, exchanged photocards with one another, and many carried taeguk-patterned fans — symbolic of Korea — to beat the heat. Some fans, dressed in their own traditional national costumes, were also spotted filming challenge videos for 'Hooligan,' a new track from the fifth album.

Debi (30), who came from Indonesia, attended the concert in a light purple hanbok representing BTS. 'BTS declared a new era, a '2.0,' with the 'ARIRANG' album, and I can genuinely feel their change. They've become even more sexy and handsome after coming back from the military,' she said. 'In the past, when I lost my father, it was an incredibly difficult time, and I found comfort listening to BTS's 'Butterfly'.' She then addressed the members directly: 'I sincerely congratulate you on your 13th anniversary. I hope all the members stay healthy and well.'

Busan was transformed into a massive festival venue on the occasion of BTS's concert, with the offline event 'BTS THE CITY ARIRANG-BUSAN' held at various landmarks across the city. Photo zones for the 'ARIRANG' world tour were set up from Busan Station, and the walls of Jonghap Stadium Station near the venue were covered with photos of the members — particularly Jimin and Jungkook, both of whom are from Busan — drawing long lines of fans eager to take commemorative photos. BTS launched the 'ARIRANG' world tour in April at Goyang Stadium, with 86 shows across 34 cities worldwide, making it the largest stadium-scale world tour in K-pop history. The group previously held their pre-enlistment farewell concert 'Yet To Come in BUSAN' at the same venue in October 2022.

Professor Kim Jeong-seop of Sungshin Women's University said, 'We are in an era where the tourism industry is expected to breathe new vitality into regional areas, and tourism ultimately requires attractions and various things to enjoy. BTS's large-scale concert will be a good example of fulfilling those needs.' Lee Ji-young, a research professor at Hankuk University of Foreign Studies' Semiosis Research Center, also noted, 'Jimin and Jungkook's hometown is Busan. The fact that they are holding a concert in Busan on their debut anniversary is in line with the message of the 'ARIRANG' album — that they will not forget their roots.'
