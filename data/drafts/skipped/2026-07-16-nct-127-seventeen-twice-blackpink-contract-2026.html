As K-pop's contract renewal season continues, boy groups and girl groups are making starkly contrasting choices. SM Entertainment officially announced on July 15 that it has renewed contracts with all seven NCT 127 members — Johnny, Taeyong, Yuta, Doyoung, Jaehyun, Jungwoo, and Haechan. The agency expressed gratitude to fans for their ongoing support and stated it will 'provide full backing for NCT 127's activities and remain a steadfast partner going forward.'

NCT 127 debuted in July 2016 and is celebrating its 10th anniversary this year. However, the group underwent a change in April when member Mark's contract ended and he departed, leaving the group as a seven-member unit.

Following NCT 127, SEVENTEEN also shared positive news. Pledis Entertainment announced on July 13 that it has agreed to a second contract renewal with all 13 SEVENTEEN members. SEVENTEEN, who debuted in 2015, had previously signed an early first renewal in 2021. The agency noted that 'all members not currently on military service have already completed signing, and the remaining members fulfilling their military duties will complete their contracts sequentially as their service schedules allow.' SEVENTEEN also expressed their resolve, stating they 'gathered with the belief that there is nothing the 13 of them cannot achieve together, and have decided to continue as a team.'

The atmosphere on the girl group side is quite different. TWICE is currently facing their second renewal period, but ongoing reports about individual members' future plans have been emerging. According to an exclusive report by News1 on July 14, member Tzuyu may choose a new agency rather than re-signing with JYP Entertainment. JYP responded by saying 'renewal discussions are currently underway.' In June, it was revealed that member Jeongyeon had met with Baro Entertainment — the agency of her older sister, actress Gong Seung-yeon — fueling transfer speculation. Baro acknowledged the meeting took place but said 'nothing has been confirmed yet.' TWICE debuted in 2015, and all nine members renewed with JYP in 2022, making it a point of interest whether a full-group renewal will follow this time as well.

BLACKPINK went through a similar crossroads earlier. Debuting in 2016, BLACKPINK signed an exclusive contract with YG Entertainment in December of last year, but only for group activities. Individual activity renewals were not finalized, leading Jisoo, Jennie, and Lisa to each establish their own one-person agencies, while Rosé joined The Black Label, the label run by producer Teddy, with whom she had long collaborated.

Even so, the group's power as a unit remained strong. Through their world tour 'DEADLINE,' which ran from May of last year through January of this year, BLACKPINK filled major venues around the world — starting with Goyang Sports Complex in Korea, followed by SoFi Stadium in the United States, Stade de France in France, Tokyo Dome in Japan, and Wembley Stadium in the United Kingdom — becoming the first K-pop girl group to perform at Wembley. As the same renewal season plays out, boy groups and girl groups continue to make opposing choices, and the industry is watching closely to see what balance each will strike between maintaining a full lineup and pursuing individual creative freedom.
