TWICE has kicked off a three-day encore run of their world tour 'THIS IS FOR' at the KSPO Dome (formerly the Gymnastics Arena) in Seoul Olympic Park, starting July 10, 2026. Their agency JYP Entertainment made the announcement, marking the grand finale of a tour that stands as the group's largest ever.

TWICE launched the tour in Incheon in July 2025 and went on to meet fans across 44 regions worldwide over 81 shows, visiting cities including Macau, Kaohsiung, Hong Kong, Taipei, Boston, and Vancouver. The entire tour was staged on a 360-degree open-style platform, allowing more audience members to attend per show than a conventional straight-stage setup.

During the tour, TWICE became the first K-pop group and the first overseas act ever to perform at Japan's National Olympic Stadium in Tokyo, drawing 80,000 fans per night over three nights for a total of 240,000 attendees. They also performed at all four of Japan's major domes — Kyocera Dome Osaka, Vantelin Dome Nagoya, PayPay Dome Fukuoka, and Tokyo Dome — drawing a combined audience of 640,000 in Japan alone.

TWICE debuted in October 2015 and has been expanding the scale of their tours since their first in 2017. Across six previous tours, they have held a total of 188 shows in 59 regions worldwide — the most of any K-pop girl group. The group is known for hit songs including 'Cheer Up', 'TT', 'LIKEY', and 'What is Love?', and their thirteenth mini-album 'With YOU-th' reached No. 1 on the Billboard 200 in 2024.

JYP Entertainment said, 'At this concert, which puts the finishing touch on a grand tour spanning approximately one year, TWICE will deliver the greatest stage encompassing the past 11 years.'
