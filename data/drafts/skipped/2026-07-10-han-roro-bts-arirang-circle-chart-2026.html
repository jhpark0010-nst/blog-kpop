Singer-songwriter Han Roro's track 'I Will Fall in Love' was the most-streamed song in the Korean music industry in the first half of 2026, while BTS's album 'Arirang' (ARIRANG) ranked first in album sales. The Korea Music Content Industry Association, which operates the popular music chart Circle Chart, announced its first-half chart recap on July 10.

Han Roro's 'I Will Fall in Love,' a track from her first mini-album, claimed the No. 1 spot on both the digital chart and the streaming chart for the first half of the year. Originally released in August 2023, the song spread by word of mouth among the MZ generation and became Han Roro's signature track. It has consistently appeared in the weekly digital chart's Top 10 since late November of last year (Week 48), cementing its status as the defining song of the first half of 2026.

BTS's fifth full-length album 'Arirang' topped the album chart with first-half sales of 4,224,016 copies. 'Arirang' is the album BTS released as a complete group for the first time in three years and nine months, addressing the diverse and universal emotions they encountered through their identity and life experiences.

The song 'Golden,' performed by the fictional girl group HUNTR/X from the Netflix animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters' as part of the original soundtrack, reached No. 1 on the Global K-pop Chart. The track held the top position on that chart for 38 consecutive weeks.

TOMORROW X TOGETHER topped the download chart with 'Stick With You,' the title track of their eighth mini-album. Sibling duo AKMU claimed the top spot on the V Coloring chart with 'Rumored Paradise,' a track from their fourth full-length album.
