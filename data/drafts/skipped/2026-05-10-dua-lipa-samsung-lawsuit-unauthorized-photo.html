Global pop star Dua Lipa has filed a $15 million (approximately 22 billion Korean won) lawsuit against Samsung Electronics, alleging unauthorized use of her photo. U.S. entertainment trade outlet Variety reported on May 9 (local time) that the suit has been submitted to a federal court.

Lipa's attorneys filed the complaint with the U.S. District Court for the Central District of California, stating: 'We bring claims of copyright infringement, trademark infringement, and right of publicity violations against Samsung for its large-scale, ongoing, and unlawful commercial exploitation of Lipa's valuable image and likeness on TV packaging boxes without her permission.'

According to the complaint, Samsung Electronics began using Lipa's photo on the exterior of TV packaging boxes last year. Lipa's team claims that after becoming aware of this, they demanded Samsung stop using the photo, but Samsung refused.

The photo in question was taken backstage at the Austin City Limits Festival in 2024, and Lipa's team states that the copyright to the image is owned by Lipa herself.

Lipa's legal team emphasized in the complaint: 'Her face was prominently used in a large-scale consumer goods marketing campaign without her consent, prior consultation, or any say or control. Lipa did not authorize this.'

Lipa, who is originally from the United Kingdom, debuted in 2015 with the single 'New Love' and gained popularity when her first studio album 'Dua Lipa' reached number 3 on the UK Albums Chart in 2017. She has won three Grammy Awards since her debut. Variety noted that it reached out to Samsung for comment but did not receive a response.
