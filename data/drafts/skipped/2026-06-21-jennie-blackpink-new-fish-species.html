A newly discovered fish species has been officially registered in academia under the name inspired by BLACKPINK's Jennie. According to research published in the international academic journal Zoosystematics and Evolution, a team of researchers from Sun Yat-sen University in China discovered a new fish species during a field survey in the mangrove wetlands of Hengqin Island (Hengqindao) in Guangdong Province, and named it Brachygobius jennie — commonly known as Jennie's bumblebee goby.

This discovery is also the first confirmed case of a bumblebee goby genus fish in China. The lead author and graduate student Tian Jiangyan, who collected 31 specimens during the survey, recalled the moment of discovery: 'While conducting a field survey in the Pearl River Estuary wetlands, I noticed small fish living near the bottom of shallow water. At first I thought they were juvenile fish, but their body markings did not match any known species in the area.'

The species was confirmed as new based on its distinctive pattern — four narrow stripes behind the head — along with genetic differences. The Jennie goby does not exceed 9 millimeters in length as an adult, making it the smallest within its genus and potentially one of the smallest fish in the world.

As for why the researchers named this tiny, remarkable new species after a K-pop star, Tian Jiangyan explained via science news platform EurekAlert: 'Listening to Jennie's songs was a constant source of inspiration throughout my studies.' He added: 'Naming this fish species after her is my own way of acknowledging the positive influence she has had on my research.' While it is not uncommon in the scientific community to name new species after admired or inspiring figures, this naming has drawn attention as an interesting case of a K-pop artist's positive influence reaching the academic world.
