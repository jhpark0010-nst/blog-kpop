BTS's title track 'SWIM' from their fifth album 'Arirang' has been accused of plagiarism by unknown American songwriters. Their label Big Hit Music denied the claims on July 10, calling them 'one-sided assertions' and vowing to respond firmly.

According to Billboard's report on July 9 (local time), three songwriters — Steve Cooper, John Sandler, and Graelyn Johnson — filed a lawsuit the previous day alleging substantial similarities between BTS's 'SWIM' and their own demo track of the same name.

The complaint names HYBE, HYBE America, and Big Hit Music as defendants, along with the songwriting team behind 'SWIM,' including Ryan Tedder, a former member of the band OneRepublic. Although RM is also listed as one of the song's co-writers, Billboard noted that Cooper and the other plaintiffs did not name BTS or its individual members as defendants.

Regarding the question of access — a key issue in copyright infringement cases — the plaintiffs claim they sent their demo to various industry contacts, including executives at an artist publishing group, starting in March of last year, and that some of those contacts also shared the demo with certain co-writers of 'SWIM.'

Musicologist Alexander Stewart, commissioned by the plaintiffs to conduct an analysis, stated that a comparison of BTS's 'SWIM' and the demo revealed 'similarities ranging from the hook referencing the title to distinctive harmonic, textural, rhythmic, and lyrical elements.' He concluded that 'from an expert standpoint, it is an inescapable conclusion that [SWIM] is not an original creation by BTS but a copied work.' Stewart has previously been involved in plagiarism cases involving Ed Sheeran's 'Thinking Out Loud' and Led Zeppelin's 'Stairway to Heaven,' both of which were ultimately dismissed.

Big Hit Music responded on July 10, stating: 'This lawsuit is nothing more than a one-sided claim by the plaintiffs. We clearly state that SWIM is an independently created work.' The label added that it plans to 'respond firmly through all future legal proceedings.'
