A series of outfit controversies has recently swept the K-pop music scene. Mark (formerly of NCT), TWICE's Chaeyoung, and BIGBANG's G-Dragon each faced public criticism after wearing T-shirts or stage outfits containing symbols or phrases tied to racism and Nazi imagery, leading to apologies and explanations from the artists and their agencies.

Mark drew significant backlash after he was spotted wearing a garment featuring a Confederate flag design — a symbol widely associated with white supremacy and racial discrimination. The Confederate flag is the banner of the union formed by 11 Southern U.S. states that seceded from the Union in 1861 while upholding slavery. On June 24, his agency Upper Room issued an apology, stating, 'We apologize for the discomfort and disappointment caused by the vintage T-shirt.' The agency added, 'The outfit was chosen simply because it was vintage,' and explained that once the historical significance and sensitivity of the image were recognized, steps were taken to prevent it from appearing in official content. 'Despite our intentions, we should have exercised greater caution,' the agency acknowledged, adding, 'We take full responsibility for this mistake.'

Chaeyoung also previously apologized over an outfit controversy. In 2023, she posted a photo taken at a restaurant on her personal account. The issue was the T-shirt she was wearing, which featured a swastika — a symbol of Nazi Germany. Following the backlash, Chaeyoung said, 'I sincerely apologize,' and explained, 'I failed to properly recognize the swastika on the clothing I was wearing.' She added, 'I should have looked more carefully, and I am truly sorry for causing concern to so many people. I will make sure this kind of situation does not happen again.'

G-Dragon was also not free from outfit-related controversy. Last month, he appeared on stage at 'K Spark in Macau,' held at an outdoor performance venue in Macau, wearing a T-shirt with a Dutch-language phrase. Among the words on the outfit was 'NEGER,' which was pointed out as a racial slur historically used to refer to Black people in Dutch. As the controversy grew, his agency Galaxy Corporation issued a deep apology, stating, 'We sincerely apologize for the fact that the stage outfit contained a phrase that does not align with social and cultural context.'

The agency further stated, 'This incident has reminded us once again of the importance of cultural sensitivity and prior review,' and added, 'We will approach future activities more carefully, with respect for fans from diverse cultural backgrounds. We once again sincerely apologize.'

As more K-pop artists build global fanbases, these incidents have highlighted the growing importance of thoroughly vetting costumes and props before they appear in public.
