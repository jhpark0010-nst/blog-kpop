NewJeans marked their 4th debut anniversary on July 22 with a surprise release of photos and a special video featuring all four current members — Hanni, Haerin, Hyein, and Minji — posted without prior notice on the group's official channel by ADOR. The release has drawn significant attention as it raises the possibility of the group resuming full activities.

This marks the first time NewJeans has released new official group content in approximately 1 year and 4 months. The group had suspended activities following their performance at ComplexCon Hong Kong in March of last year, making the sudden appearance of a full four-member release a major moment for fans.

NewJeans had previously clashed with ADOR after the dismissal of former CEO Min Hee-jin, announcing the termination of their exclusive contracts at a press conference in November 2024. However, courts ruled in ADOR's favor in both injunction and main lawsuit proceedings, effectively blocking the members from pursuing independent activities. The group then declared a halt to activities after the ComplexCon Hong Kong show.

Subsequently, Haerin and Hyein returned to ADOR in November of last year, with Hanni rejoining in December. Meanwhile, Danielle remains in an ongoing legal dispute after ADOR notified her of contract termination in December of last year, citing her as bearing 'decisive responsibility for the team's departure.'

As all members currently with ADOR appeared in the new video, anticipation for future group activities has grown. However, ADOR stated, 'This video is commemorative content created to celebrate NewJeans' 4th debut anniversary and enjoy the occasion with fans,' adding, 'Specific activity plans and details will be announced after all discussions have been concluded.'

NewJeans debuted on July 22, 2022, and established themselves as a leading K-pop girl group with back-to-back hits including 'Attention,' 'Hype Boy,' 'Ditto,' 'OMG,' and 'Super Shy.' In 2023, their second mini-album 'Get Up' reached No. 1 on the Billboard 200, making them only the second K-pop girl group to achieve that feat. They have not released new music since the Japanese single 'Supernatural' in June 2024. With the four-member content now public, attention continues to build over whether NewJeans will move toward a full-scale activity resumption.
