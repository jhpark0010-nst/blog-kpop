G-Dragon has been named the promotional ambassador for the UNESCO World Heritage Committee, which will be held in Korea for the first time. The Cultural Heritage Administration announced the appointment on July 3, describing G-Dragon as 'an artist who has wielded global influence not only in K-pop but across culture and the arts as a whole.'

The Cultural Heritage Administration explained that G-Dragon's worldwide reach and consistent public-interest activities make him the ideal partner to spread awareness of the true value of World Heritage. Through this appointment, G-Dragon will officially begin his activities as ambassador for the 48th UNESCO World Heritage Committee, which opens in Busan on July 19. He is set to participate in producing promotional videos for a citizen engagement campaign on World Heritage protection and to take part directly in various on-site events, delivering the core message of 'peace through culture and participation' to audiences at home and abroad.

G-Dragon has been serving as honorary chairperson of the Juspeace Foundation, a public-interest legal entity he established in 2024 through a donation of his copyrights, leading efforts to resolve social issues through art and to spread a culture of public benefit. An official from the Cultural Heritage Administration expressed anticipation, saying that 'the values of G-Dragon and the Juspeace Foundation — that small participation changes the world and creates peace — align with the spirit of international cooperation for peace that UNESCO pursues.'

To mark the first-ever hosting of the World Heritage Committee in Korea, the Juspeace Foundation will launch an urban campaign called 'Heritage in Peace' on July 10 in cooperation with UNESCO, enabling citizens to join in protecting World Heritage. The campaign was planned to raise awareness of World Heritage and encourage donations to the UNESCO World Heritage Fund. Starting with the participation of Busan citizens and local communities, the goal is to establish a new model of engagement in which citizens, companies, and cities protect World Heritage together. All donations raised through the campaign will be delivered in full to the UNESCO World Heritage Fund and used to safely protect World Heritage from various threats including war, the climate crisis, and natural disasters.

G-Dragon's side conveyed his feelings upon the appointment, saying, 'I feel it is very meaningful to take on the role of ambassador for the UNESCO World Heritage Committee, which is being held in Korea for the first time.' He added, 'As World Heritage is a shared asset that all of humanity must protect together, I want to deliver a message of peace that many people can relate to and act on together.'

An official from the Cultural Heritage Administration also said, 'We expect that G-Dragon and the Juspeace Foundation joining us will further expand domestic and international interest in the World Heritage Committee and citizen participation.'
