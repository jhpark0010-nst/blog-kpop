TWICE member Tzuyu is reportedly preparing to launch an independent career in Taiwan ahead of the expiration of her personal exclusive contract with JYP Entertainment. According to a July 15 report by Taiwanese media outlet China Times, multiple sources within the local entertainment industry claimed that Tzuyu is unlikely to renew her individual contract with JYP.

Those same sources stated that she appears set to pursue solo activities based out of a management company established in Taiwan by her parents. They revealed that Tzuyu's parents founded 'Yuhai Entertainment' in Taiwan in November of last year.

The company has registered 22 business categories, including performance planning, music production and distribution, advertising, and talent management. The registered address of the company was confirmed to be the same location as a cafe operated by Tzuyu's mother. Local industry insiders believe the company was established with Tzuyu's future independent activities in mind.

Specific circumstances supporting the solo activity reports have also been raised. At a beer rock festival held in Kaohsiung, Taiwan earlier this month, Tzuyu performed a solo stage and is said to have carried out her schedule without a JYP-affiliated manager present. It was also reported that she participated in the event under her real name, 'Chou Tzuyu,' rather than as 'TWICE Tzuyu.'

Additionally, it has been reported that during Tzuyu's visit to Taiwan last year, she met Chen Zeshan, a prominent music producer and former head of Warner Music Greater China. Chen Zeshan was also said to have provided on-site support at a recent event, fueling speculation that he will take on a supporting role in Tzuyu's future activities in the Chinese-speaking market.

However, in an official statement on July 14, JYP responded: 'We are currently in a period of discussing contract renewals with TWICE members.' The agency added, 'We will provide official notice as soon as specific matters are confirmed.'
