BTS's fifth full-length album 'ARIRANG' has re-entered the Top 10 of the Billboard 200, the main U.S. album chart. According to Billboard on June 23 (local time), 'ARIRANG' climbed to No. 10 on the Billboard 200, rising one spot from the previous week.

'ARIRANG', released in March, made history as the first K-pop album to spend three consecutive weeks at No. 1 on the Billboard 200. BTS has now remained on both the main Billboard album and singles charts for 13 consecutive weeks.

The album's title track 'SWIM' charted at No. 55 on the Hot 100 singles chart. The surprise release 'Come Over', dropped on June 12, debuted at No. 69 on the same chart.

On the Billboard 200, BOYNEXTDOOR's first full-length album 'HOME' reached No. 16, a new personal best for the group. The original soundtrack album for the Netflix animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters' came in at No. 27. CORTIS's 'GREENGREEN' entered at No. 61, while KATSEYE placed at No. 112 with 'Beautiful Chaos' and No. 173 with 'SIS'.

On the Hot 100, Jennie's collaboration with Tame Impala, 'Dracula', entered at No. 18. The collaborative single 'ICONIC BY MISTAKE' by HYBE girl groups LE SSERAFIM, ILLIT, and KATSEYE debuted at No. 38. KATSEYE's 'Pinky Up' also charted at No. 94.
