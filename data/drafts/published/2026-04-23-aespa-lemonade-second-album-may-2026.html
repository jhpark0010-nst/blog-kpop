aespa is set to release their second full-length album, 'LEMONADE,' on May 29, 2026. SM Entertainment announced the news on April 20, marking the group's first full album in approximately two years since 'Armageddon,' which came out in 2024. Fans have been eagerly anticipating the group's next major project since that debut full-length release.

The new album will feature 10 tracks spanning a variety of genres. According to SM Entertainment, 'LEMONADE' showcases a more mature side of aespa, reflecting stronger storytelling and musical growth. The label described the record as a chance for listeners to experience a more developed version of the group's signature sound and artistic identity.

In other K-pop news, indie band Soran will drop a new single titled 'Delivery' on April 24 at 6 p.m. KST. The track is a city-pop song that captures the feeling of wanting to spend time with someone special. Soran is also scheduled to perform at 'Beautiful Mint Life 2026,' taking place on May 30 at Seoul Culture Tank (Seoul Mapo-gu).

Additionally, Lee Ji-hoon — runner-up on the SBS program 'Our Ballad' (우리들의 발라드) — will release a new song called 'A Good Person' on April 20 at 6 p.m. KST. The track was written, composed, and produced by Yoon Jong-shin, and carries a message of comfort for those who have lost their sense of self-worth. Actor Yoo Seung-mok, who shares the same agency SM C&C as Lee Ji-hoon, appears in the music video.
