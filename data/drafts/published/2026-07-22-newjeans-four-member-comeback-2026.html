NewJeans' long-awaited return is slowly taking shape. On July 22 — the group's 4th debut anniversary — NewJeans released a special film titled '2026 Summer of NewJeans' on their official YouTube channel, featuring all four members: Minji, Hanni, Haerin, and Hyein. This marks the first time NewJeans has released new team content in approximately one year and four months, since their Hong Kong concert in March of last year.

In the video, the four members are seen smiling brightly, pushing through tall grass, and holding hands — showcasing their signature fresh and warm chemistry. Originally a five-member group, NewJeans had attempted to promote independently following a contract dispute with their agency ADOR, but suspended activities after a court upheld ADOR's injunction request. After Haerin, Hyein, and Hanni each returned to ADOR in turn, Minji — who had been in ongoing negotiations — also appeared in this 4th anniversary content, leading the music industry to view a four-member NewJeans comeback as essentially imminent. Ousted member Danielle has been notified of contract termination by ADOR and is currently involved in a large-scale damages lawsuit.

Minji's return and the resulting four-member restructuring is, objectively speaking, a more advantageous choice compared to a three-member configuration. With a full five-member lineup now effectively impossible due to Danielle's departure, a four-member setup preserves the group's original form as much as possible and offers greater musical and visual cohesion than a trio. Additionally, since each member holds a distinct individual fanbase, Minji's inclusion is expected to serve as a momentum for reuniting the previously divided fandom.

Nevertheless, deep shadows still loom over the road to their comeback. While NewJeans' brand power and music streaming strength remain intact, the K-pop landscape has shifted rapidly during the more than one year the group's activities were frozen. The time consumed by legal battles and public disputes has taken away what should have been the most brilliant period of their idol careers.

Above all, the biggest challenge NewJeans must overcome is image rehabilitation. Since their 2022 debut, NewJeans rose to the top with back-to-back hits including 'Attention,' 'Hype Boy,' and 'Ditto,' reaching such a peak in public favorability that they were often called a group with no antis. However, their image — once synonymous with fresh-faced, pure teenage energy — took a significant hit through the agency dispute and legal battles that shook the entire pop culture world.

ADOR stated, 'Specific activity plans and methods will be announced after discussions are fully concluded,' maintaining a cautious stance. All eyes in the music industry are now on the four-member NewJeans as they prepare to stand before fans again after a long hiatus — and on whether they can win back the hearts of a public that has partly turned away, and reclaim the throne they once held.
