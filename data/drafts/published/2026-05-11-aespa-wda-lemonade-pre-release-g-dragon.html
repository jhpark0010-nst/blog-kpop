aespa have unveiled 'WDA (Whole Different Animal),' the pre-release single from their second full-length album LEMONADE, across major music platforms on May 11 at 6 PM KST. The track is a hip-hop-based dance song featuring a grand synth bass and a heavy hook that delivers an overwhelming impact, with lyrics capturing aespa's growth and signaling a new chapter in their universe.

G-Dragon participated in the song as a featured artist and also contributed rap-making for his own part, completing a powerful synergy with aespa. The collaboration has generated significant anticipation for a strong response.

The music video for 'WDA,' released simultaneously through SM Entertainment's official channels, depicts a world where the boundary between reality and the digital realm has grown increasingly blurred, making it impossible to prove what is 'real.' Figures who wear aespa's faces but are clearly not aespa appear amid this chaos and conflict. The video ultimately shows aespa breaking through the cracks — referred to as 'Complaexity' — and proving their own existence once again.

Since their debut, aespa have built a narrative in which their real selves and digital selves coexist, with ae-aespa (AI-aespa) serving as a core pillar of their universe. This music video was conceived to naturally raise the question of 'what is real' through direction that captures the unsettling sensation of an era where familiar images are endlessly copied and consumed, and the line between original and copy, reality and virtual, has become blurred.

aespa also held a 'WDA' countdown live through each platform's official channel starting at 5 PM KST — one hour before the single's release — talking about the new track and communicating with fans. The event was designed to amplify anticipation for the second full-album comeback.

aespa's second full-length album LEMONADE will be released in full as a digital release on May 29 at 1 PM KST, and will also be issued as a physical album.
