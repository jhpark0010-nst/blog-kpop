BTS leader RM shared candid thoughts about the group's fifth studio album 'Arirang' (ARIRANG) during a Weverse Live session on May 28. The broadcast drew more than 3.7 million viewers despite airing on a weekday afternoon. RM reflected on the album's creation, calling it one of the most challenging experiences of his career.

'No matter which producer had come in, making an album for BTS members who had just been discharged from the military would have been the hardest thing in the world,' RM said. 'The fact that the album and tour came together without a single person dropping out is a miracle in itself.' He also acknowledged mixed reactions to the title 'Arirang,' saying he had anticipated that the name alone would divide opinions, but emphasized that the group had kept their promise by returning together and releasing a full album.

RM reflected on a difficult period during the album's production. 'Around the time Jin was discharged, everyone's mental state wasn't great,' he recalled. 'We gathered then and made up our minds to repay the wait.' After all members completed their military service, the group traveled to Los Angeles for a songwriting camp. RM noted that a lack of a strong central direction made the process harder: 'We had done so many genres that each of us had different goals. The members, the fans, and the related departments all had different ideas. This was an album made without a set path, which made it even more difficult.'

On the title track 'SWIM,' RM said it was not originally intended to be the lead single. 'I like this song. Many people were probably disappointed — they might have thought it was bland and slow compared to past BTS songs,' he said. 'But no better title track than 'SWIM' came out of the sessions. Considering factors like radio play, 'SWIM' was the only song with lasting power. I wanted to make it a meaningful track. This song feels like BTS entering their 30s — more mature, more grown-up. I'd want to hear it even from the grave.'

Regarding the traditional Korean folk song arirang incorporated into the track 'Body to Body,' RM acknowledged the divided reactions. 'I listened to it three times before it clicked. We found it unfamiliar at first too,' he said. 'But when I thought about the live shows — the image of so many international fans singing along to that Korean folk melody as if they had been waiting for it — I felt that was something you simply cannot manufacture.' RM also mentioned his fondness for album track 'No.29,' which features the sound of the Emille Bell (Seongdeok Daewang Sinjong), adding that a scheduling conflict prevented him from actually striking the bell and that he hopes to do so someday and perform the track at a concert.

RM closed the session by speaking about BTS's identity going forward. 'BTS wants to continue being a team that delivers stories — whether personal or universal,' he said. 'We want to protect that sense of romance.' He also highlighted the album's achievement of having every track chart, noting that it is rare for an idol group's album to have many songs gain widespread attention.
