BTS's fifth studio album 'ARIRANG' has continued its strong run on the U.S. Billboard main album chart, the Billboard 200, recording a top 5 position for six consecutive weeks.

Billboard announced in a chart preview article on May 3 (local time) that 'ARIRANG' ranked at No. 5 with 56,000 album units, dropping one spot from the previous week.

The Billboard 200 ranks albums based on total album units, which combine physical and digital album sales, streaming equivalent albums (SEA), and track equivalent albums (TEA) converted from digital song downloads.

Released in March, 'ARIRANG' became the first K-pop album to reach No. 1 for three consecutive weeks on this chart, and has since maintained its position in the upper rankings at No. 3, No. 4, and now No. 5.

Meanwhile, Michael Jackson's classic album 'Thriller,' originally released in 1982, re-entered the Billboard 200 at No. 7, boosted by the North American release of the biographical film 'Michael.' 'Thriller,' which held the No. 1 spot for an impressive 37 weeks from 1983 to 1984, recorded 45,000 album units during this chart's tracking period — a 425% increase from the previous week.
