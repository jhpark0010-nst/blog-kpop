G-Dragon's agency Galaxy Corporation issued a formal apology on May 4, 2026, after a T-shirt the artist wore during a performance was found to contain a racially offensive phrase targeting Black people. The incident drew widespread criticism from global fans.

According to sources in the K-pop industry, G-Dragon appeared at the K-SPARK (K-스파크) concert held in Macau on May 2. The T-shirt he wore at the event became the subject of controversy.

The T-shirt contained a phrase in Dutch that carried racially discriminatory connotations.

As criticism from global fans continued to mount, Galaxy Corporation released an official statement: 'We sincerely apologize for the inclusion of a phrase that is inappropriate in its social and cultural context.'

Galaxy Corporation added, 'Through this incident, we have once again recognized the importance of more careful cultural sensitivity and responsible review. We will examine and improve our overall internal review and verification procedures, including styling, and operate under more cautious standards going forward.'
