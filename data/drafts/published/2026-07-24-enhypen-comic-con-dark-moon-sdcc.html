ENHYPEN made an unprecedented appearance at San Diego Comic-Con (SDCC) on July 23 (local time), joining the event as panelists at the San Diego Convention Center in California. It marked the first time a K-pop idol group has participated in one of America's two major comic conventions, which has traditionally centered on comics, graphic novels, and animation.

The appearance was made possible through 'Dark Moon,' an original story created in collaboration between HYBE and ENHYPEN, built around the concept of vampire boys. The webtoon 'Dark Moon: The Blood Altar' has accumulated over 200 million views, and an anime, web novel, and follow-up webtoon are also in development.

Because the story expands on the group's own worldview and characters — with each member having a corresponding character whose personality and even tastes are often drawn from the real members — fans enjoy moving between the music and the webtoon. ENHYPEN's Jungwon said at the panel talk, 'Our personalities are so much reflected in the Dark Moon story that we sometimes say the characters are even more like us than we are.'

Fans who gathered at the Dark Moon booth at SDCC said they love both the group and the webtoon. A fan named Hanna, who dressed in the school uniform worn by characters in 'Dark Moon' — even hand-coloring her own necktie — said, 'I am an ENGENE (ENHYPEN's fandom name) and I come to Comic-Con every year, but I came specifically because ENHYPEN was going to be here. Of course I've read all the webtoon too.' A teenage fan who attended with her father added, 'I also went to the ENHYPEN world tour held in San Diego two days ago,' and said she was happy that both the tour and the Comic-Con event took place in San Diego so she did not have to travel to Los Angeles.

Even visitors who were not ENHYPEN fans recognized 'Dark Moon.' Some passersby noticed the 'Dark Moon' animation playing on the booth's front display and exclaimed, 'Oh, it's a K-pop group comic,' stopping to take photos. Park Tae-ho, Head of Business at HYBE Next Entertainment Business Division, said of the group's first Comic-Con participation: 'Honestly, I was quite nervous,' but added, 'After the preview night and the panel talk, I think it was absolutely the right call to come. More people are interested in Dark Moon than I expected.'

HYBE has introduced various story IPs through collaborations with groups including BTS and LE SSERAFIM, but 'Dark Moon' is drawing overwhelming popularity among them. The familiar vampire setting is seen as a key factor. At the panel talk, the 2008 film 'Twilight' and 'Dark Moon' were connected through their shared vampire theme. Director Catherine Hardwicke, who directed 'Twilight,' and actors Peter Facinelli and Ashley Greene, who played Carlisle and Alice respectively, attended and spoke about the appeal of the vampire concept. ENHYPEN members said they all grew up watching 'Twilight' and credited the film with opening the public's eyes to vampires. Member Sunghoon added, 'In our music videos, we suddenly transform into bats and throw swords, but because people already know we are vampires, we don't have to explain each thing one by one — that is a really huge advantage.'
