NCT's Mark has come under heavy criticism from fans both in South Korea and internationally after being photographed wearing a vintage T-shirt featuring a Confederate flag pattern. On June 23, Mark's solo management agency Upper Room issued an official statement via social media, offering a sincere apology: 'We sincerely apologize for the concern, discomfort, and disappointment caused by the vintage T-shirt seen in recently released photos.'

The symbol at the center of the controversy is the Confederate flag printed on the front of the vintage T-shirt Mark was wearing. The Confederate flag was the official flag of the Confederate government, which seceded from the Union during the American Civil War in order to preserve slavery. Entering the 20th century, it became widely regarded as a symbol of white supremacy, racism, and far-right extremism. Some U.S. states and institutions have legally banned the display of the flag or the sale of merchandise bearing its imagery.

The backlash was particularly pointed given that Mark is a Canadian national — a North American artist — making his apparent oversight of the symbol's historical sensitivity a focal point of criticism within and beyond the K-pop industry.

In its statement, Upper Room explained: 'The outfit was chosen simply as a vintage clothing item. However, upon recognizing the historical significance and sensitivity of the symbol displayed on the T-shirt, we took measures to ensure that the symbol would not be exposed in official content.'

The agency continued: 'Regardless of intent, we acknowledge that this matter should have been handled with greater care and caution. We take full responsibility for this oversight.' It also stated: 'Upper Room and the artist firmly oppose and do not tolerate racism, hatred, discrimination, or any form of bigotry.'

Upper Room concluded by saying: 'We are deeply aware of the seriousness of the concerns raised and sincerely regret the pain and discomfort this incident has caused. We will strengthen our internal review procedures for outfit selection and content approval to prevent a recurrence. We sincerely apologize to everyone who was hurt, offended, or disappointed by this incident.'
