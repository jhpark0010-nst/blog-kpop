Stray Kids member Seungmin has been diagnosed with an ankle stress fracture, his agency JYP Entertainment confirmed on July 2 via official channels. According to the statement, Seungmin is focusing on treatment and rest following medical advice, and will be participating in activities only minimally, including being unable to appear in some upcoming content releases.

JYP Entertainment added that 'Seungmin showed a strong will to continue activities, but after sufficient discussion, this decision was made so that he can return to fans in good health as soon as possible.' The agency also noted that it will continue to monitor the artist's condition and recovery status regarding future scheduled official events.

Seungmin had previously been unable to take the stage at the major U.S. festival 'The Governors Ball Music Festival' on June 6 due to the same injury.

This string of idol injuries has been a cause for concern across the K-pop industry. Recently, LE SSERAFIM leader Kim Chaewon had paused activities ahead of a comeback due to neck pain before announcing her return to health. Shortly after, TWS member Youngjae was reported to have sustained a left thigh muscle injury during concert rehearsals. Youngjae strongly wished to perform and completed the group's Asia tour Seoul concert schedule with modified choreography.

The news of Seungmin's injury is especially unfortunate as Stray Kids are on the verge of a major comeback and large-scale world tour. The group achieved a historic milestone with their mini album 'ATE,' spending 100 weeks on the Billboard World Albums chart — a first for a fourth-generation K-pop boy group — and surpassing 1 billion cumulative streams on Spotify.

Stray Kids are set to kick off their new world tour 'RUN IT' at KSPO DOME starting July 25, with all seats already sold out. On August 7, they will release their new mini album 'THIS & THAT,' marking their first full album activities of 2026. JYP Entertainment emphasized that it 'will do its best to help the artist recover his health as quickly as possible.'
