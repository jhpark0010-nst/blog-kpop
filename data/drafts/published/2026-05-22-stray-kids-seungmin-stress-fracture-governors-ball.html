Stray Kids member Seungmin has been diagnosed with a stress fracture in his left ankle and will be unable to perform at The Governors Ball Music Festival in New York on June 6 (local time). Agency JYP Entertainment released an official statement on May 21 through fan community channels, announcing the injury and confirming that the group will perform as a seven-member unit.

According to the agency, Seungmin first experienced pain in his left ankle in March and visited a hospital, where doctors noted the possibility of a stress fracture. He has been receiving ongoing treatment since then. Following additional examinations, a stress fracture in his left ankle was officially confirmed. He is currently undergoing further detailed examinations regarding related symptoms and potential associated conditions, while focusing on treatment and recovery.

JYP stated that 'Seungmin is currently resting in his daily life and avoiding strenuous physical activity.' The agency added that 'in order to focus on a full recovery and stable treatment, he will be minimizing long-duration flights and travel in confined spaces, taking sufficient rest, and continuing to monitor his recovery progress.'

As a result, Seungmin will not be able to take the stage at The Governors Ball Music Festival, a large-scale outdoor music festival held annually at Flushing Meadows Corona Park in New York. Stray Kids had been invited as headliners for the June 6 performance, drawing significant attention from the global music scene. Due to the worsening of Seungmin's injury, the New York performance will proceed with the remaining seven members.

The agency expressed its apologies, saying 'We are sorry for delivering this sudden news to fans who have been looking forward to the performance.' JYP also pledged that 'we will continue to prioritize our artist's health above all else and do our utmost to ensure he receives proactive and professional treatment going forward.'
