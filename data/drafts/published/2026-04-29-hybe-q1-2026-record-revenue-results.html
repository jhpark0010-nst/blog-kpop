HYBE posted its best-ever first-quarter results in Q1 2026, with consolidated revenue reaching 698.3 billion KRW — close to the 700 billion KRW mark — despite the period traditionally being an off-season for the entertainment industry. This represents a 40% increase compared to the same quarter last year (500.6 billion KRW). The performance was driven by the impact of the BTS comeback and the rapid growth of newer artists.

Direct participation revenue (physical albums, concerts, advertising, etc.) grew 25% year-on-year to 403.7 billion KRW. Album sales revenue in particular surged 99% year-on-year to 271.5 billion KRW — a scale comparable to Q4 2023 (276.2 billion KRW), which had seen landmark results including SEVENTEEN's mini album 11 'SEVENTEENTH HEAVEN' topping K-pop first-week sales records and Jungkook's album 'GOLDEN' reaching No. 1 in first-week sales among solo artists.

At the center of the strong album sales was BTS's fifth full-length album 'ARIRANG.' The album sold 3.98 million copies on its first day alone. According to global music data analytics firm Luminate, the ARIRANG LP also recorded 208,000 copies in weekly sales, the highest weekly sales figure for a group since tracking began in 1991. ARIRANG became the first album by a Korean artist to spend three consecutive weeks at No. 1 on the Billboard 200. The title track 'SWIM' reached No. 1 on the Billboard Hot 100, and all 13 tracks on the album charted.

ENHYPEN achieved their fourth double million-seller with mini album 7 'THE SIN : VANISH.' Newer artists also stood out. KATSEYE recently surpassed 32 million monthly Spotify listeners, the highest figure among all girl groups worldwide. Back-catalog sales rose alongside the influx of new fans. The combined cumulative sales of KATSEYE's first EP 'SIS (Soft Is Strong),' released in August 2024, and their second EP 'BEAUTIFUL CHAOS,' released in June 2025, have reached 1 million copies. CORTIS's debut album 'COLOR OUTSIDE THE LINES' continued to sell steadily beyond its initial release period, setting a new K-pop group debut album sales record. Released in September of last year, the album recorded approximately 440,000 first-week sales, surpassed 1 million copies in November of the same year, and reached cumulative sales of 2 million copies as of February this year.

Indirect participation revenue (MD and licensing, content, fan club, etc.) reached 294.7 billion KRW, up 66% year-on-year. MD and licensing and fan club segment revenues grew 29% and 69% respectively compared to the prior year. The MD and licensing segment benefited from BTS tour-related merchandise including light sticks, as well as character goods based on HYBE Music Group artists. Fan club segment revenue showed sharp growth driven by pre-sale demand for BTS world tour concerts. Weverse, the global superfan platform that returned to annual profitability last year, also recorded its highest-ever active user metrics and profitability growth since its service launch in Q1 this year. Monthly average active users in Q1 grew 20% quarter-on-quarter to 13.37 million, a new all-time high.

HYBE's adjusted operating profit for Q1 2026, reflecting actual business performance, stood at 58.5 billion KRW, with an adjusted operating margin of 8.4%. The pre-adjustment operating profit showed a deficit, as 255 billion KRW donated in shares by the largest shareholder for employee performance bonuses was recognized as an expense under accounting standards. This is a one-time cost that must be recognized under accounting rules despite involving no outflow of the company's net assets. From Q2 onward, multiple HYBE Music Group artists including TXT, LE SSERAFIM, TWS, ILLIT, and CORTIS are expected to resume activities with new album releases, and BTS world tour-related results will be reflected, with both revenue and profit anticipated to grow further.
