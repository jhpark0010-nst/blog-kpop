Actress Jung Ho-yeon sat down with MyDaily on July 8 at a cafe in Jongno-gu, Seoul, to discuss the film 'Hope' — sharing everything from her first encounter with director Na Hong-jin to the casting process. 'Hope' marks Jung Ho-yeon's screen debut and was invited to the Competition section of the 79th Cannes International Film Festival held in May.

'Hope' tells the story of Bum-seok (played by Hwang Jung-min), the head of the Hopo Port branch office located in the Demilitarized Zone, who hears from local young men that a tiger has appeared, throwing the entire village into a state of emergency as he faces an unbelievable reality. Jung Ho-yeon plays Seong-ae, a police officer at the Hopo Port branch, and received praise for pulling off highly demanding action sequences.

Asked about her feelings on the film's release day, Jung Ho-yeon said, 'I feel a great sense of excitement. I want to meet the audience quickly and hear their feedback.' She described her first meeting with director Na Hong-jin as feeling 'like I could fly through the sky.' 'Hope' is Na Hong-jin's first film in 10 years since 'The Wailing' in 2016.

She continued, 'At the time, I was already thinking about what project to take on next when the director reached out first and asked to meet. It was a simple meeting request, but my mindset was that I was going in for an audition. On the way there, I imagined a lot about how I wanted to come across and whether he would think well of me.' She also recalled, 'When I met the director, his gaze was intense and he didn't even blink. He was sitting to my right, and I thought to myself that I shouldn't put on any kind of act.'

Jung Ho-yeon said she showed director Na Hong-jin her true self, recounting what happened next: 'The director said, "Since Jung is coming into Chungmuro (the Korean film industry), shouldn't I treat her to a bowl of jajangmyeon?" He bought me delicious jajangmyeon nearby.' She added, 'I had so much fun at that meeting and thought it was over, so I was completely surprised when the director recommended me to the production company. I had no expectation of receiving the script that day, so I was overjoyed.'

She went on to say, 'It felt more precious than any treasure in the world,' and laughed as she recalled, 'I held it close to my chest the entire way home, and as soon as I arrived, I wrote my name under the title "Hope." I desperately wanted this project to become mine.'
