Kim Bujang, an action drama about a father searching for his missing daughter, has topped Netflix's global non-English shows chart. According to Netflix's official site Tudum, the series recorded 10.5 million views (measured as total watch time divided by runtime) for the week of June 29 to July 5, 2026, ranking first among all non-English shows worldwide.

Kim Bujang made its debut on June 26 and shot straight to third place in the non-English shows category within just three days. In its second week of availability, it climbed to the top spot. The series reached number one in 11 countries — including South Korea, Singapore, Thailand, and Peru — and entered the top 10 in 79 countries.

Starring So Ji-sub, Kim Bujang is based on a webtoon of the same name. It follows a former special agent who has been living quietly while hiding his abilities, but reveals his true identity and fights to find his kidnapped daughter. The show has resonated with audiences for its portrayal of a father's deep love and its fast-paced, satisfying action sequences.

Kim Bujang first aired as an SBS Friday-Saturday drama before becoming available on Netflix. By its fourth broadcast episode, the show had recorded a 21.6% viewership rating — the highest rating of any drama aired in Korea this year. This places it third all-time among SBS Friday-Saturday dramas, behind 'Penthouse 2' (29.2%) and 'Hot Blooded Priest' (22%).

Chamgyoyuk (True Education), which had held the number one spot in the non-English shows category for four consecutive weeks, dropped to second place with 4.7 million views, entering the top 10 in 54 countries. Starring Kim Mu-yeol, the series examines the realities of Korea's public education system. Man Kkeut Jul Sonyeon (The Last Row Boy), starring Choi Min-sik and Choi Hyun-wook, rose three places from eighth to fifth with 2.6 million views, entering the top 10 in 21 countries. Based on a Spanish play, it tells the story of a failed writer and literature professor who becomes obsessed with a genius student sitting in the back row of his classroom.

Gas Ningen, directed and written by Yeon Sang-ho of Train to Busan and Guntche fame, ranked seventh with 2 million views and entered the top 10 in 6 countries. It is a Netflix Japan original series expanding on the 1960 tokusatsu film Gas Ningen Dai Ichigo by director Honda Ishiro, following a 'gas human' who can transform their body into gas to commit crimes, and those who pursue them. In the non-English films category, Namphyeondeul (The Husbands), starring Gong Myung and Jin Seon-kyu, came in second with 1.7 million views across 10 countries. In the English-language films category, K-Pop Demon Hunters maintained its top 10 position for the 55th consecutive week, holding sixth place with 3.5 million views.
