NewJeans appears to be gearing up for a comeback, with fans and media now focused on when exactly the group will return to the stage. Label ADOR confirmed on April 27 that 'the Copenhagen visit was part of a pre-production process to capture a new musical narrative for NewJeans,' responding to a document circulating in online communities that appeared to show a recording studio schedule in Copenhagen, Denmark.

The document revealed that a studio had been booked under ADOR's name from April 13 to 16. Some speculated that NewJeans had visited the local studio to work on a new album. With the label effectively confirming this, the possibility of NewJeans resuming activities is gaining weight.

However, the member lineup remains an uncertain variable. Three members — Hanni, Haerin, and Hyein — are reported to have been part of the Copenhagen trip, while attention is focused on whether Minji, whose future with the group is currently under discussion, will participate in activities. ADOR released an official statement saying, 'We are preparing activities in accordance with each member's condition and circumstances, and will inform fans of future plans at the appropriate time.' Ultimately, the choices each member makes going forward are expected to be the key factor in NewJeans' activities.

Since their debut in 2022, NewJeans established themselves as a leading fourth-generation girl group with a string of hit songs including 'Attention,' 'Ditto,' 'OMG,' 'Super Shy,' 'ETA,' and 'How Sweet.' They also demonstrated a remarkable global presence, with multiple songs simultaneously charting on the Billboard Hot 100 and extended runs on the Billboard 200.

However, group activities came to a halt in November 2024 when the members notified ADOR of contract termination, claiming violations of management obligations. The dispute escalated into a legal battle, and after ADOR lost the first-instance ruling in October of last year in a lawsuit seeking confirmation that the exclusive contracts remained valid, group activities have effectively been suspended for an extended period.

During NewJeans' absence, competition in the K-pop scene has intensified, with fourth-generation leaders aespa, IVE, and NMIXX, as well as fifth-generation girl groups ILLIT, Kiki, and Hearts2Hearts making significant advances. Attention is now focused on whether NewJeans can overcome this hiatus and return to the stage — and which members will be there when they do.
