The path forward for NewJeans, long obscured in uncertainty, is slowly coming into focus. On May 7, a birthday congratulatory post for member Minji appeared on NewJeans' official social media, becoming a decisive indicator that a reconciliation with their agency ADOR and an imminent comeback are on the horizon. ADOR also released an official statement regarding Minji's situation, saying, 'We are continuing internal discussions regarding future activities, and overall, negotiations are proceeding in a positive direction,' lending further weight to the comeback signal.

The most noteworthy point is the change of heart from Minji, who had previously been categorized as a close ally of former ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin. Minji had maintained a sharp stance in the exclusive contract dispute with ADOR, standing firmly in Min Hee-jin's corner. However, Minji recently made a surprise visit to a fan-organized birthday cafe, delivering a handwritten letter, homemade cookies, and photo booth pictures — and the fact that ADOR supported the entire event through its official account signals a rapid restoration of trust between the two sides.

This is interpreted as Minji's pragmatic choice to prioritize her future as a NewJeans member and her promises to fans over emotional conflict. The judgment at play is that securing continuity of activities within ADOR's management system is the best course of action.

As discussions around Minji's return trend positively, the likelihood of NewJeans reorganizing into a four-member unit — Minji, Hanni, Haerin, and Hyein — has grown considerably. Unlike the three members who have already confirmed their return, Danielle received a notice of termination from ADOR and is now navigating a difficult path involving a massive penalty and damages lawsuit worth 43.1 billion won.

While seeing NewJeans as a five-member group has become unlikely, a four-member NewJeans with leader Minji on board has gained strong momentum to maintain the team's identity and return to full activity. Attention is also turning to how this newly reborn four-member NewJeans will operate. Hanni, Haerin, and Hyein — who have already confirmed their return — visited Copenhagen, Denmark last month and completed a pre-production process to capture a new musical narrative. Reports of studio bookings and filming sightings at local venues hinted that NewJeans' comeback to the music scene is not far off. Although Minji did not accompany them on that trip, the possibility of her joining remains flexibly open depending on each member's condition and an optimized schedule, making anticipation for the four-member activities higher than ever.

Just like the cookies Minji baked with care, NewJeans is preparing to stand before the public once again with sincerity. All eyes are on what new records they will set in the music industry as they rise again after enduring a period of intense growing pains.
