Netflix has announced a world tour inspired by its animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters,' the two-time Oscar winner. On May 14, Netflix revealed through its official site Tudum that it is developing and producing a global tour inspired by the film in partnership with AEG Presents.

Netflix stated, 'AEG and Netflix are bringing the magic of Honmun to a venue near you. You will be able to feel the golden energy in real life.' It added, 'This will be a live experience that brings elements of the two-time Oscar-winning K-Pop Demon Hunters to life in a surprising way.'

According to outlets including Deadline, Netflix officially announced the tour at its Upfronts event held in New York. At the event, Netflix's Chief Advertising Officer Amy Reinhard said, 'No title has captured the attention of the world quite like K-Pop Demon Hunters. Even now, K-Pop Demon Hunters remains the most popular film on Netflix.' She added, 'This tour will be designed so that fans can experience the characters and music they love up close.'

The cast members who will actually appear on stage have not been disclosed. There is interest in whether performers who voiced the girl group Huntrix in the film — including Jae, Audrey Nuna, and Rei Ami — as well as the demon boy group Sazaboys, will be confirmed for the tour. Tour dates and cities are set to be announced later this year.

'K-Pop Demon Hunters' is an animated film following the story of Huntrix, a girl group that protects the human world from evil spirits. It won the Animation award at the 83rd Golden Globe Awards last year and the Best Animated Feature Film award at the Academy Awards this past March.

The original soundtrack 'Golden' won the Golden Globe and Academy Award for Best Original Song, as well as the Grammy Award for Best Song Written for Visual Media. Since its release, the film has held a spot in the Top 10 most-watched English-language films on Netflix worldwide for a cumulative 47 weeks, continuing its cultural phenomenon status.
