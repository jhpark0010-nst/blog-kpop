Kim Junsu's Bangkok concert, scheduled for July 25, 2026 as part of his Asia tour 'GRAVITY,' has been canceled. His agency, Palm Tree Island, announced the cancellation on June 10, stating that the Bangkok stop of the tour has been unavoidably called off.

Palm Tree Island explained the reason in a follow-up statement: 'During the preparation process, a communication error between the local organizer and operator resulted in a venue being contracted that was different from the one agreed upon and approved in advance with us, making the cancellation unavoidable.' The agency added, 'We sincerely apologize to the fans who have been waiting for the concert for the sudden news and any disappointment and inconvenience caused.'

Kim Junsu is set to kick off his Asia tour 'GRAVITY' on June 12 at KSPO DOME in Seoul Olympic Park (formerly the Gymnastics Arena). The tour had been planned to cover 9 cities across 8 countries — Macau, Taipei, Malaysia, Tokyo, Osaka, Hong Kong, and Singapore — but with Bangkok, the final stop, now canceled, he will be unable to meet local fans there.

Palm Tree Island released an official notice stating: 'The 2026 XIA 6TH ASIA TOUR CONCERT 'GRAVITY' IN BANGKOK, scheduled for July 25, 2026, has been unavoidably canceled. A communication error between the local organizer and operator led to a different venue being contracted from the one agreed upon and approved in advance. We sincerely apologize to the fans who have been waiting, and deeply thank all fans who always support Kim Junsu. We ask for your generous understanding.'
