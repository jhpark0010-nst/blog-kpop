LE SSERAFIM, ILLIT, and KATSEYE dominated the screen with an aura that commanded even the most unsettling environments. Director Cody Critcheloe, who helmed the music video for the three groups' collaboration track 'ICONIC BY MISTAKE', sat down with Weverse Magazine on June 17 to share the aesthetics and creative intentions behind the work.

Critcheloe described the music video as 'a project of twisting and subverting existing norms,' adding that he 'wanted to break away from conventional standards of beauty.' He cited 'the depth of story that allows fans to interpret and find meaning on their own' as a key strength of K-pop content, but said he did not want to rely on familiar visual grammar. He felt the message and narrative should be rich, while the way they are expressed must be unfamiliar and unpredictable.

What he chose was not polished, orderly beauty. He twisted the familiar frame of K-pop visuals through unsettling settings — a graveyard, a dental office, a field engulfed in spreading flames — and strange, unstable imagery. Critcheloe explained that 'the police officers appearing in the video are a broad metaphor for the entertainment industry, where constant evaluation and scrutiny repeat endlessly.' He emphasized that what truly matters is the active agency the three groups display under that gaze.

LE SSERAFIM, ILLIT, and KATSEYE remain unshaken even knowing they are subjects of surveillance and analysis. Critcheloe said, 'It is ultimately the artists who take control of the situation within the music video,' and added, 'Unexpected situations end up working as strengths. Even mistakes, coincidences, and imperfections can be the elements that make someone an icon.' This directly connects to the song title 'ICONIC BY MISTAKE.'

Each group's narrative was also given a fresh variation within a single world. LE SSERAFIM's gravestone scene, ILLIT's wisdom tooth motif, and KATSEYE's stage and flames are references familiar to fans, but within 'ICONIC BY MISTAKE' they are given new meanings and woven into one story. Critcheloe said, 'I wanted to create a world where each group's identity is preserved, yet they can coexist within one larger narrative.' The video ends with the three groups coming together — members innocently roasting marshmallows, strumming an acoustic guitar, laughing and chatting in front of a blazing fire. Critcheloe called it 'surprisingly simple, yet lovely and somehow absurd,' and noted that in America such a moment is jokingly called 'campfire bonding' — a sense of being connected and experiencing something together.

'ICONIC BY MISTAKE' entered Spotify's Daily Top Songs Global chart at No. 25 as of June 16, showing signs of a climb. The music video topped YouTube's Music Video Trending Worldwide chart immediately upon its June 1 release and has now surpassed approximately 29 million views. The dance practice video released on June 14 (LE SSERAFIM x ILLIT x KATSEYE 'ICONIC BY MISTAKE' Dance Practice) also subsequently reached No. 1, further proving fans' enthusiasm.
