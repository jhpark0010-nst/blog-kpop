Korean Netflix drama Cham Gyoyuk, starring actor Kim Mu-yeol, has ranked #1 among non-English shows on Netflix globally. According to the Netflix Top 10 data compiled on the official Netflix site Tudum, the series recorded 6.4 million views (calculated by dividing total watch time by runtime) for the week of June 1 to 7, topping the non-English show category.

By country, Cham Gyoyuk entered the top 10 in 48 nations, including South Korea, the Philippines, Singapore, Turkey, Argentina, and Egypt.

Released on June 5, Cham Gyoyuk depicts a fictional agency called the 'Teacher Rights Protection Bureau,' established to defend South Korea's crumbling educational environment against students, teachers, and parents who cross the line.

The original webtoon had been embroiled in controversy over racism and sexism in certain episodes. After the live-action adaptation was confirmed, some cast members publicly declined to appear during production, and certain teacher organizations called for the project to be halted.

Taking the controversy into account, the production team worked to remove as many problematic elements as possible and focused on the true meaning of education, posing questions to society. Kim Mu-yeol's action sequences — including rain-soaked fight scenes and one-against-many bare-handed combat — also spread by word of mouth, propelling the show to #1 in the non-English category just three days after its release.

Forbes described the series as having 'a solid script and outstanding entertainment value that deeply immerses viewers and keeps them curious about what comes next,' calling it 'one of the best dramas released so far this year.' In the non-English show rankings, Korean dramas and variety shows also dominated the upper tiers: Brave New World came in at #2, Wonderfuls at #4, and Sold Out Today at #9, while variety show Yoo Jae-seok Camp placed #10 — putting five Korean titles in the top 10. In the English-language film category, K-Pop Demon Hunters ranked #10 with 3.7 million views, marking its 51st consecutive week in the top 10.
