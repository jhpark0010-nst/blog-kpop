TWS is set to open their first Asia tour on June 27-28 at KSPO Dome in Olympic Park, Songpa-gu, Seoul, according to an announcement by Pledis Entertainment on June 26. The tour is titled '2026 TWS TOUR 24/7:FOR:YOU IN SEOUL' and is themed around the concept of 'a gift prepared for you.'

At the Seoul concerts, TWS plans to present stages being shown for the first time, along with a variety of songs, offering fans a time that feels like a gift. Following Seoul, the group will continue the tour across 8 cities in Asia — including Fukuoka in Japan, Bangkok in Thailand, and Kaohsiung in Taiwan — for a total of 15 performances.

(G)I-DLE has released a preview of their upcoming mini album 'We Made,' set for release on July 6, according to Cube Entertainment. The preview video includes portions of the title track 'Gimme Dat Love,' as well as album tracks 'Morning' and 'Love Is Pain.'

'Gimme Dat Love' is described as a track featuring an appealing whistle melody, expressing instinctive love that goes beyond reason.

Comedian Park Semi will release a trot duet titled 'Mwondeul Mot Hagesseoyo' with trot singer Kim Soyu on July 6, according to Meta Comedy. Park Semi is taking on a new challenge as a trot singer, following her work as a creator, radio DJ, and web entertainment MC.

'Mwondeul Mot Hagesseoyo' is a semi-trot song that cheerfully captures the pure feeling of being willing to do anything for the person you love. The song was written and composed by Goo Hee-sang, who also created Young Tak's hit song 'Why Are You There.'
