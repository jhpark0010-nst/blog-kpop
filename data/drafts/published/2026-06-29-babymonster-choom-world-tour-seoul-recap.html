BABYMONSTER held their '2026-BABYMONSTER WORLD TOUR [CHOOM] IN SEOUL' over three days, from June 26 to 28, at Jamsil Indoor Stadium in Seoul. This was the group's second concert, arriving one year and five months after their first. The venue was packed with fans holding BABYMONSTER's official light sticks, and cheers erupted from the audience even before the show began.

BABYMONSTER opened with 'WE GO UP', with Ahyeon's bridge part drawing immediate attention. They followed with the mini album 3 title track 'CHOOM', debut song 'BATTER UP', and 'DRIP', driving the energy hard from the very start. The set continued with 'MOON', 'CLKL CLAK', 'SHEESH', and 'PSYCHO', building the atmosphere further. What stood out most was not the elaborate lighting or special effects, but BABYMONSTER's own energy commanding the choreography, live vocals, and stage movement. The members moved between the main stage and a sub-stage set up close to the fans, making full use of the venue. Despite being only in their third year since debut, they clearly showed their own chemistry and team color on stage.

The solo cover stages were another highlight of the concert. Rora chose Camila Cabello's 'Havana' — the same song she had performed in front of executive producer Yang Hyun-suk during a monthly trainee evaluation — and delivered it with confidence in front of thousands of fans, completing a personal growth story. Asa reinterpreted 'Temple', featuring G-Dragon, with a Japanese-inspired mood. Vocalist Pharita showed a surprising side with Nicki Minaj's 'Super Bass'. Chiquita combined 'Buttons' and 'Worth It' to highlight her dance-pop appeal. Main rapper Ruka unleashed hip energy with 'RATATA'. Ahyeon demonstrated her all-rounder abilities — vocals, rap, and performance — through Ariana Grande's 'Problem'.

The performance of 'Really Like You', which made a chart comeback this year through a reverse-charting surge, was met with a massive fan sing-along that filled the venue before the members even began. Rora said, 'I wanted to show a ballad-style stage so fans could feel that we can do this kind of performance too.' BABYMONSTER then kept the heat going with 'FOREVER', 'WILD', 'HOT SAUCE', new song 'SUGAR HONEY ICE TEA', and 'I LIKE IT', with fans on their feet throughout.

Before the encore, the members said, 'Would anyone really believe there are only 3 songs left? You have to enjoy the remaining songs too before we go to the encore,' showing their confidence. The true highlight of the roughly 2-hour-and-10-minute show was the 30-minute encore, performed in a more relaxed atmosphere without stage costumes. It was in those moments — making direct eye contact with fans and playing freely — that it became clear why BABYMONSTER is a group built for the stage.

Rora explained the meaning of the world tour: 'We wanted to express our ambition to color the whole world with a single dance line.' Ahyeon said, 'It feels so good to feel the fans' energy up close,' adding, 'Starting the world tour in Seoul makes it feel even more special. I want to repay everyone with great performances.' Ruka shared, 'We get to visit so many countries. I'm so curious what memories we'll make.' Pharita said, 'I'm happy to be in a good mood and deliver good stages.' Ahyeon also reflected, 'When I practice in the studio, I felt something was missing — I think it was because you weren't there in front of me,' and pledged, 'I want to be an Ahyeon and a BABYMONSTER that gives 100 out of 100, or 200 out of 200.' After the three Seoul dates, BABYMONSTER now heads to a larger stage — a world tour spanning five continents including Asia, North America, and for the first time since their debut, Oceania, Europe, and South America.
