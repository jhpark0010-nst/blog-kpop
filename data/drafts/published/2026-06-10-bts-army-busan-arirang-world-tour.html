Two days before the BTS World Tour 'Arirang IN Busan' concert, global ARMY fans began flooding into Busan on June 10, 2026. The international arrivals hall at Gimhae Airport was busy from early in the morning as fans poured in from around the world. An Indonesian couple caught attention with their matching couple T-shirts — the wife's read 'I follow you (bts) into BUSAN' and the husband's read 'I follow her into BUSAN.' The husband said he came to Busan following his wife, who is a BTS fan, and that he hopes to sightsee around the city.

Most arrivals came from Singapore, Japan, China, and Taiwan, which have direct flights to Busan, though many ARMY fans also arrived via connecting flights through Japan or China. A long line formed at the welcome booth set up by the Busan Tourism Organization and Korea Tourism Organization, where fans could pick up welcome kits containing a Busan tourism map, a hand fan, and a name tag. Many fans were spotted with purple luggage and wearing red outfits — the main color of BTS's latest studio album.

Yasuda Chieri, a woman in her 30s from Kagoshima, Japan, said, 'I was so excited about visiting Busan for the first time in three years and seeing the BTS concert that I couldn't sleep and couldn't focus on work.' She added that she plans to stay in Busan for five days and participate in various BTS-related events prepared by Busan City and HYBE. She also shared her frustration over accommodation, saying she suddenly received a cancellation notice from the hotel where she had originally booked three nights, and was forced to book five separate hotels because no single place had consecutive nights available. This is her third visit to Busan — twice for BTS concerts and once to meet a friend she made through BTS. She said she had previously visited Haeundae and Gwangandaegyo and hopes to go again this trip.

Yelena, a woman in her 20s who introduced herself as coming from a small village in Russia, said, 'I took three flights to get to Busan.' She explained that she traveled by domestic flight to Vladivostok, then took an international flight via Shanghai to reach Busan. 'This is my first visit to Busan. I heard the sea and food are famous here,' she said, adding, 'It's the hometown of the BTS members, so I really wanted to come.'

At the 'BTS THE CITY Arirang Busan Welcome Center' set up at Busan Station's Eurasia Platform, fans who had just arrived by train were also streaming in throughout the afternoon on June 10. BTS music played inside the center as fans carrying luggage and bags adorned with purple merchandise browsed around and soaked in the concert atmosphere. The center, which also offers Busan tourism information and luggage storage and delivery services, has been operating since June 5. A staff member on site said, 'We opened at 10 a.m. and nearly 1,000 visitors came within three and a half hours,' noting that over 90% were international tourists.

Eliza (33), who visited Busan from the Philippines with two friends, said she arrived at Gimhae Airport on June 8 and plans to stay in Busan for about a week. 'I first came to Busan last year for a conference and was impressed by how beautiful the city is,' she said. She added, 'There were no issues with accommodation and the people of Busan are kind, so I'm having a great trip. Yesterday I went to Gwangalli, and after Saturday's concert I'm planning to visit Haeundae and Gamcheon Culture Village — I'm really looking forward to it.' Busan City expects more than 100,000 domestic and international ARMY fans to visit Busan for the concert.
