BTS met with Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum on May 6 (local time). At her regular morning press conference known as the Mañanera, President Sheinbaum personally announced that BTS would be visiting the presidential office and that the presidential balcony would be opened so the group could greet citizens gathered outside.

President Sheinbaum had previously welcomed the confirmation of BTS's Mexico City concerts back in January, calling the occasion a 'historic moment.' She also emphasized that the concerts were something local young people had been waiting for a long time, underscoring her high regard for the group.

BTS's visit to Mexico is also generating significant economic expectations. The Mexico City Chamber of Commerce estimated that the concerts would produce an economic ripple effect of approximately $107.5 million USD (roughly 155.7 billion Korean won).

The intense interest in BTS is backed by data. According to figures released by global audio and music streaming platform Spotify last year, Mexico ranks fifth in the world for K-pop listening. Among all K-pop acts, BTS was named the most-streamed artist by local K-pop fans.

Their fourth studio album 'MAP OF THE SOUL : 7,' released in 2020, was selected as the most-played K-pop album in Mexico. Eight of the top 10 albums in that ranking belong to BTS. Their song 'Dynamite' ranked first as the most-streamed K-pop track in Mexico since the K-pop boom began.

BTS will take the stage at Estadio GNP Seguros in Mexico City on May 7 and May 9–10, for a total of three nights.
