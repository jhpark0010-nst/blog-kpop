Stray Kids will headline a brand-new music festival called STRAYCITY, set to take place across three Latin American countries in September 2026. Live Nation and JYP Entertainment announced on May 23 that the festival will visit Colombia, Argentina, and Mexico, with Stray Kids serving as headliners for every show.

STRAYCITY kicks off on September 9 (local time) in Bogotá, Colombia, followed by Buenos Aires, Argentina on September 14, and Mexico City, Mexico on September 25. Stray Kids will headline all three dates of the newly launched festival.

Fellow JYP Entertainment group NEXZ will also appear at the festival, alongside local artists including Andrés Obregón, Rene, Bad Milk, K Lynch, Caos, and Cocho.

JYP Entertainment described the festival as follows: 'STRAYCITY is a music festival for everyone to enjoy, aiming to be a space where people awaken another self within and discover a new version of themselves.' The label added, 'Stray Kids will unleash their unrivaled energy through this festival inspired by the group's own name, and share a special musical world with the audience.'

Stray Kids previously completed their world tour 'dominATE,' meeting fans across 35 regions in 56 shows worldwide. The group has also appeared as headliners at major international music festivals including Lollapalooza Paris in France, I-Days in Italy, British Summer Time Hyde Park in the UK, and Lollapalooza Chicago in the US. JYP Entertainment noted that 'STRAYCITY will continue with additional venues to be added in the future.'

Stray Kids are also scheduled to perform at The Governors Ball Music Festival in New York, USA on June 6, and at Rock in Rio in Brazil on September 11. Earlier, in March and April, the group held their sixth fan meeting at Inspire Arena, where they met their domestic fanbase, STAY.
