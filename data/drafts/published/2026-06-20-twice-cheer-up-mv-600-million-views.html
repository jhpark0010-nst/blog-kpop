TWICE's hit song 'CHEER UP' has surpassed 600 million views on YouTube, according to an announcement by their agency JYP Entertainment on June 20. The milestone marks another major achievement for the group in their decade-long career.

With this achievement, TWICE now has five music videos with 600 million or more views — following 'TT', 'What Is Love?', 'LIKEY', and 'FANCY'. 'CHEER UP' becomes the fifth entry in that record-setting list.

'CHEER UP' is the title track from TWICE's second mini-album, released in April 2016. The upbeat dance track, known for its refreshing sound and bright energy, became a massive hit upon release.

TWICE kicked off their largest-ever world tour, 'THIS IS FOR', in Incheon in July of last year, performing 81 shows across 44 regions worldwide over the course of one year. In North America alone, the tour drew 550,000 attendees, setting a new audience record for a K-pop girl group.

The tour will conclude with a finale concert at KSPO Dome (formerly the Gymnastics Arena) in Seoul Olympic Park on July 10 through 12. All seats for the finale have already sold out.
