BLACKPINK member and solo artist Jennie turned heads at the Time100 Gala on April 23 (local time), held at Jazz at Lincoln Center in New York City. The event celebrated TIME magazine's '100 Most Influential People in the World 2026,' drawing a large gathering of global celebrities.

Jennie wore a bold outfit from French haute couture house Schiaparelli, drawing widespread attention. The structured top left a strong impression with its distinctive silhouette, and despite the avant-garde styling, she carried the look with a polished, urban sophistication.

She paired the outfit with a sleek updo that accentuated her facial and neckline, while her sharp, square shoulder line added to the overall refined fashion statement. On the red carpet, she was also spotted chatting warmly with Hollywood actress Dakota Johnson and Hailey Bieber, showcasing a relaxed presence befitting a global star.

Jennie was the only K-pop star named to this year's Time100 list. Singer-songwriter Gracie Abrams, who wrote her tribute, praised her saying, 'Jennie has an undeniable presence that cuts through all the noise,' and added, 'Her strength within softness makes her all the more remarkable.'

Meanwhile, Jennie previously made history with her solo album 'Ruby,' simultaneously placing three songs on the Billboard Hot 100 — an unprecedented feat for a female K-pop solo artist. She then broke her own personal best with 'Dracula (Remix),' a collaboration with Kevin Parker of Australian band Tame Impala, reaching No. 15 on the Hot 100 (dated the 25th), marking her first top-20 entry as a solo artist.

With 'Dracula (Remix),' Jennie extended her total Hot 100 entries to seven songs, once again setting a new record for the most Hot 100 entries by a female K-pop solo artist. She has also been nominated for 'Song of the Summer' at the American Music Awards (AMAs), further cementing her global influence.
