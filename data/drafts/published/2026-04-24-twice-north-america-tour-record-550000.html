TWICE has drawn approximately 550,000 fans through their North American tour, JYP Entertainment announced on April 24. As part of their ongoing world tour 'THIS IS FOR,' the group held a total of 35 concerts across 20 cities in North America from January through April 18 of this year, setting a new personal record for their largest-ever North American tour.

TWICE will also perform three concerts at Japan's National Olympic Stadium in Tokyo on April 25–26 and 28, becoming the first overseas artist to headline the venue as a solo act.

ENHYPEN will embark on a Japan four-dome tour from December through February of next year, Belift Lab announced on April 24. The group will kick off at Tokyo Dome on December 1–2, followed by Vantelin Dome Nagoya on December 26–27, Mizuho PayPay Dome Fukuoka on February 6–7, 2027, and Kyocera Dome Osaka on February 19–20, for a total of 8 concerts across four Japanese cities.

These dome venues each hold 40,000 to 50,000 people, reflecting the scale of ENHYPEN's popularity in Japan. ENHYPEN will launch their new world tour at KSPO Dome (formerly the Olympic Gymnastics Arena) in Olympic Park, Songpa-gu, Seoul, from May 1 to 3.

Tiffany Young, a member of Girls' Generation, will release a new single 'Summer's Not Over' on May 8 at 6 p.m., Pacific Music Group announced on April 24. 'Summer's Not Over' is a pre-release single commemorating the 10th anniversary of Tiffany's solo debut. The song delicately captures the feeling of a loved one 'feeling as comfortable as home.' Tiffany Young is also planning to release a full-length studio album afterward.
