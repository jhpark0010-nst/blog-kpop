BTS drew a total of 150,000 fans across three concerts in Mexico City on May 7, 9, and 10 (local time) as part of their world tour 'ARIRANG,' label Big Hit Music announced on May 12. It marks the group's first team concert in Mexico in 10 years and 10 months, since July 2015. Tickets for all three shows sold out the moment booking opened.

BTS wove local culture throughout the performances to show thoughtful consideration for their Mexican fans. They performed 'Airplane pt.2,' which features the lyric 'We goin' from Mexico City,' and brought out dancers wearing masks from the traditional Mexican professional wrestling style Lucha Libre during the 'Aliens' stage. V also picked up a local snack, a bandeja, from a stand set up at one side of the venue while performing 'IDOL.'

The members shared their feelings, saying, 'You were truly the best. What more can we say?' and 'We will definitely come back to Mexico.' They also said in Spanish, 'I don't think I'll ever forget the energy that carried over from the Zócalo plaza,' and 'We're leaving with so many wonderful memories of Mexico.'

According to local authorities, approximately 35,000 people gathered outside the venue on May 9 and 10. The crowds spread to surrounding roads, leading to traffic controls on some sections. The LA Times reported that it is rare for a boy band to become a matter of international diplomatic interest, noting that Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum invited the members to the presidential palace. The outlet also reported that more than one million people attempted to purchase tickets, and those who could not get in gathered around the venue.

The Mexico City Chamber of Commerce projected that the concerts would generate an economic impact of approximately $107.5 million USD (around 158.6 billion Korean won) from ticket sales, flights, accommodation, food and beverage, and local commercial spending.

BTS will continue the tour with shows in Stanford, USA on May 16, 17, and 19, followed by concerts in Las Vegas on May 23, 24, 27, and 28. They will then meet their fandom ARMY at Busan Asiad Main Stadium on June 12 and 13. The second day of the Busan concerts, June 13, falls on the group's 13th debut anniversary, making it a meaningful occasion for both BTS and their domestic fans.
