BIGBANG — consisting of G-Dragon, Taeyang, and Daesung — will release a new song for the first time in four years. According to MyDaily's exclusive reporting on July 7, BIGBANG has a filming schedule set for the third week of July related to the new track. The three members' schedules were coordinated well in advance, and music video as well as content filming will take place on that day.

BIGBANG's last release was four years ago. Their previous song, 'Still Life' (봄여름가을겨울), released in April 2022 with T.O.P also participating, was their final track. 'Still Life' captured the group's long journey through the four seasons, and despite no promotional activities, it ranked No. 7 on Melon's annual chart — the largest music streaming platform in South Korea — that year, receiving an enormous amount of love.

After T.O.P departed in June 2023, BIGBANG became a trio and focused on individual activities, releasing no new group music. G-Dragon, Taeyang, and Daesung each released solo songs and carried out their own tours. The upcoming new song is set to be released ahead of a tour — their first in nine years since 2017.

BIGBANG will hold the 'BIGBANG 2026 WORLD TOUR IN GOYANG' at Goyang Sports Complex in Gyeonggi Province for three days, from August 21 to 23. Following that, they will perform a total of 31 shows across 18 cities worldwide, including stops in North America, Europe, Oceania, and Asia. Additional tour cities will be announced on a rolling basis.

This tour marks BIGBANG's first large-scale world tour in approximately nine years. The shows are expected to feature a stage combining their numerous hit songs with the musical artistry built over 20 years and an overwhelming concert production.
