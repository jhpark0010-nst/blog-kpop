BIGBANG's Taeyang is set to release his fourth solo studio album, 'QUINTESSENCE,' on May 18, 2026. His agency, The Black Label, announced the news on April 21, marking one of the most anticipated K-pop comebacks of the year for the veteran artist.

The new album arrives approximately three years after his second mini-album 'Down to Earth,' released in April 2023. More significantly, 'QUINTESSENCE' is Taeyang's first full-length solo record since 'WHITE NIGHT' in August 2017 — a gap of nearly nine years that has fans eagerly awaiting what he has in store.

The album title 'QUINTESSENCE' carries the meaning of 'essence' or 'the purest form' of something, hinting at a deeply personal and refined musical statement from the artist. The Black Label described the project as a showcase of Taeyang's power as an all-around solo artist, promising a new musical world that is entirely his own.

Taeyang debuted as a member of BIGBANG in 2006, making 2026 the group's 20th anniversary year. The milestone has already been celebrated in a big way: BIGBANG performed as a full group at the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival on April 12 and April 19 (local time), where they also announced a new group album and plans for a world tour beginning in August.

With 'QUINTESSENCE' dropping next month and a BIGBANG world tour on the horizon, 2026 is shaping up to be a landmark year for Taeyang and his bandmates. Fans who have followed him since his debut two decades ago will be watching closely to see how this chapter of his solo career unfolds.
