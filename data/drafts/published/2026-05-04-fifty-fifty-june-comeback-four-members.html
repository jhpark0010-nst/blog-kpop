FIFTY FIFTY is set to make a splendid return this summer. Their agency ATTRACT announced on May 4 that the group is preparing a new album with an early June 2026 comeback as the target date.

This upcoming comeback marks approximately seven months since the group's last release, the digital single 'Too Much Part 1,' which dropped in November of last year.

Through their previous comeback activities, FIFTY FIFTY earned widespread love with their title track 'Gawi Bawi Bo' as well as the fan-favorite b-side 'Skittlez,' which charted on Mediabase 40 and the US Billboard Pop Airplay chart. Building on that momentum, the group has firmly established their signature easy-listening sound and plans to bring listeners an even wider variety of new music.

Regarding the upcoming comeback, ATTRACT stated: 'Member HANA has been focusing on her recovery after halting activities in March due to health reasons. We held sufficient discussions with the members and HANA herself regarding this comeback, and as a result, we have decided to proceed with album activities as a four-member unit.' The agency added: 'We will do our best to greet fans in an even better form.'

FIFTY FIFTY has continued to connect with global fans through fan meetings and fan concerts. The group is also set to make their acting debut in the short-form drama 'After-School Exorcism Club,' scheduled to premiere in May, showcasing their diverse range of activities.
