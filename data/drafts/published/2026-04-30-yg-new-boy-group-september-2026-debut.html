YG Entertainment chief producer Yang Hyun-suk has officially announced that a new boy group will debut in September 2026. On April 30, YG revealed the plan through a video posted on its official blog, with Yang Hyun-suk detailing the rookie boy group launch. This marks YG's first male rookie act in approximately six years since TREASURE, drawing anticipation from K-pop fans worldwide.

In the interview, Yang Hyun-suk stated, 'My current expectation is to introduce YG's new male group in September. The number of members that many people are curious about is five.' Unlike TREASURE, which operates with a larger lineup, the new group has been configured as a tight five-member unit.

Yang Hyun-suk also added, 'We will continue to sequentially reveal the new IPs and rookies that YG has been planning.' This hinted at the construction of a strong rookie lineup, including the next girl group tentatively titled 'Next Monster.'

He noted that 2026 is shaping up to be a pivotal year for YG, with BIGBANG's world tour marking their 20th debut anniversary, alongside active activities from BABYMONSTER and TREASURE, now further bolstered by the arrival of major new acts.

BABYMONSTER is set to release their third mini album 'CHOOM' on May 4, with Yang Hyun-suk personally participating in the choreography for the chorus. TREASURE has also confirmed the release of a new hip-hop-based mini album on June 1. Regarding the TREASURE album, Yang Hyun-suk said, 'This album is personally the result I like the most,' expressing confidence in the project.

With the September debut of the new five-member boy group added on top of the existing artists' solid schedules, attention in the industry is focused on the new blueprint YG is set to draw in the second half of 2026.
