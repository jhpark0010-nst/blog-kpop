aespa held a press conference on May 28 in Songpa-gu, Seoul, to celebrate the release of their second full-length album, LEMONADE. At the event, the group introduced the album by saying, 'Our music is often described as having a metallic taste, but this time we came back with a sour taste,' adding that they would 'take charge of summer in a cool and refreshing way.'

They also added, 'In this album, you can feel aespa's intense charm along with a more relaxed, cool energy.' LEMONADE is aespa's first full album since their debut album Armageddon, released in May 2024 — a two-year gap between releases.

While the pre-release track 'WDA,' which features G-Dragon, expressed a mutant self born from a crack through a cold and heavy hip-hop rhythm, the title track LEMONADE depicts agency and wit in overcoming crisis through an electronic dance sound characterized by intense synthesizer bass. Karina described the song's message: 'The trials and pain that come your way are called lemons, and the message is to blend them into delicious lemonade. I hope listeners get energy from this song.' Winter said, 'Fans described the new song as having a metallic-sour-sweet taste, and I loved that so much.'

When asked about aespa's vision of self-agency, Winter answered, 'I want to do what I like and love as the subject of my own life, but I often hesitate because of the impact it might have on those around me. I think agency means doing what I like and love without worrying about others' eyes, and existing as myself.' The album contains a total of 10 tracks, including the dance track 'SHAKIN',' which carries a bold message about shaking up someone else's world; the rock track 'Can't Help Myself,' which blends cool vocals with rough guitar sound; the hyper-pop track 'Camouflage,' featuring refined synthesizer sound; and 'Switchblade,' featuring American hip-hop star Ty Dolla $ign. The digital album also includes a version of LEMONADE featuring American Latin pop singer Becky G.

Winter described the album as follows: 'This second album is one where aespa's own universe has expanded to the next level. There is a familiar intensity, but you will also feel a more solid message and direction. I would be grateful if fans listen to this album and feel that aespa has opened yet another new door.' Karina explained the album's lore in detail: 'Because we kept traveling back and forth across the multiverse, a crack appeared in the real world. It could be something to panic about, but after finding Black Mamba, Navis, and ae in the kwangya, it became no big deal. The worldview of this chapter is about turning crisis into opportunity.' Giselle added, 'I am a fan of the universe. If you notice that the lyric from the past song Next Level — 'Never look back, do not covet the things of the kwangya' — connects all the way to today's LEMONADE, it will be a fun story.'

To mark the album's release, aespa will hold a pop-up event called 'aespa WEEK - MAKE IT LEMONADE' from May 29 through June 7 at Yeouido and Banpo Hangang Park in Seoul, as well as simultaneously in Los Angeles, New York, and Shenzhen. The event will also take place in Bangkok, Tokyo, and Taipei. Additionally, aespa will kick off a new world tour starting August 7 and 8 at Gocheok Sky Dome in Seoul — their first-ever solo concert at the venue. Winter shared, 'I used to watch other artists' concerts at Gocheok Sky Dome and think I wish we could become artists like that, and I still can't believe we are entering it with our second full album. The fact that we have gained that level of influence is thanks to our fans. I have renewed my resolve to deliver even brighter and better energy.'
