K-pop's four biggest entertainment companies — HYBE, SM Entertainment, JYP Entertainment, and YG Entertainment — announced on April 16, 2026 that they are working to establish a joint venture (JV) to stage a large-scale global K-pop festival. The event, named 'Fanomenon,' is a portmanteau of 'Fan' and 'Phenomenon,' reflecting the cultural force that K-pop fandoms represent worldwide.

The four labels made the announcement through their shared body, the Popular Culture Exchange Committee, stating that the music division's four member companies are currently in the process of setting up a legal entity to drive the Fanomenon event forward. They described the joint venture as 'a collaborative model being discussed to explore the potential for expanding K-culture, including K-pop, in global markets.'

The companies also confirmed that necessary procedures are underway, including a review of the inter-company cooperation structure and filing a business combination report with South Korea's Fair Trade Commission. However, they stressed that specific business details and operational plans have not yet been finalized.

The vision for Fanomenon was first publicly outlined in October 2025, when JYP producer and Popular Culture Exchange Committee chairman Park Jin-young unveiled the concept at the committee's launch event. He expressed ambitions to hold the festival both in Korea and across the world, explicitly naming the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival as a benchmark to surpass.

According to Park, the plan is to hold an annual Fanomenon festival in Korea every December starting in December 2027, then expand to major cities around the world from May 2028 onward. The four companies noted that all decisions going forward will be made carefully, taking market conditions and a wide range of perspectives into account as discussions progress.
