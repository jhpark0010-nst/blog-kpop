BTS swept the front pages of Japan's five major sports newspapers on April 17 and 18, 2026, as the group's 'WORLD TOUR ARIRANG IN TOKYO' concerts at Tokyo Dome dominated coverage across the country's top sports dailies.

Nikkan Sports, Sports Nippon, Sports Hochi, Sankei Sports, and Daily Sports all ran the BTS concerts as front-page stories on both days. Convenience stores near Tokyo Dome saw a rush of concertgoers trying to purchase the papers, with multiple outlets selling out early in the morning, reflecting the intense local interest in the shows.

Japanese media highlighted keywords such as 'full-group return after 7 years,' 'Tokyo Dome frenzy,' and 'reunion with ARMY (the fandom name),' focusing on the significance of BTS performing together as a complete group in Japan for the first time in years and on the passionate response from the packed dome. The last full-group Japan concert was the 'BTS WORLD TOUR LOVE YOURSELF: SPEAK YOURSELF – JAPAN EDITION' held in July 2019, approximately seven years ago. Both shows on this tour were completely sold out, once again demonstrating the explosive demand for BTS.

The enthusiasm was also reflected in album sales figures. According to the Recording Industry Association of Japan (RIAJ), BTS's fifth full-length album ARIRANG surpassed 750,000 cumulative shipments as of March, earning a Gold Disc 'Triple Platinum' certification. This marks their second career Triple Platinum certification in Japan, following their fourth Japanese album 'MAP OF THE SOUL: 7 ~THE JOURNEY~.' Notably, ARIRANG received the certification just 11 days after its release on March 20, and became the first album — domestic or international — to receive a Triple Platinum certification in Japan this year. The RIAJ awards Gold Disc certifications monthly based on cumulative shipments: Gold (100,000+), Platinum (250,000+), Double Platinum (500,000+), Triple Platinum (750,000+), and Million (1,000,000+).

BTS also showed strong performance on the Oricon charts. On the Weekly Streaming Surge Ranking (dated April 20, covering April 6–12), BTS placed five songs in the chart. New album tracks '2.0' and 'Hooligan' ranked 1st and 3rd respectively, while mega-hit classics 'Butter' (6th), 'Dynamite' (7th), and 'Permission to Dance' (9th) also entered the top 10, demonstrating that the new releases boosted the popularity of their existing catalog as well.

Meanwhile, BTS continues to maintain a powerful presence on major charts around the world.
