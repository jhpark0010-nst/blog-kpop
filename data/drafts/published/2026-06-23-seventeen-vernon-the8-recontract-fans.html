SEVENTEEN's Vernon and THE8 spoke openly about the group's re-signing contract on June 23, when their episode of 'Salon Drip' on the YouTube channel TEO was released.

During the episode, host Jang Doyeon brought up SEVENTEEN's re-signing, saying, 'Not a single member left — all 13 are staying together. Isn't that an exceptional case?' Vernon responded candidly, 'We find it remarkable ourselves.'

SEVENTEEN had announced their re-signing at a concert in April. THE8 explained the reasoning behind that timing: 'Our fans were very anxious before the re-signing was confirmed. So we brought it up on the last day of our concert.' He added, 'Fans didn't know whether we would re-sign or not, and there was also the gap period with members going to the military. We wanted to reassure them. Since we were going to stay together anyway, we thought we should put fans at ease first.'

Vernon, who said he doesn't take re-signing for granted, confessed, 'We put in a lot of effort for each other and showed a lot of consideration.' He cited 'our relationships and the potential SEVENTEEN still has' as the biggest reasons behind the bond that made re-signing possible.

Host Jang Doyeon then brought up fellow member Boo Seungkwan's past remark that fighting brings members closer, asking, 'How are things now? Have you gotten all the fighting out of your system?' Vernon replied, 'We still have small arguments.' THE8 also shared, 'There is never a quiet day. When close guys get together, it feels like our collective intelligence drops. Everyone acts like a kid, like a goofball — teasing each other over the smallest things. Even after more than 10 years, it's exactly the same.' He added, 'We only get older in age. When we're together, the pace and energy don't feel all that different.'
