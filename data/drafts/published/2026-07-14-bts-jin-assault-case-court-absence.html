A Japanese woman indicted for forcibly kissing BTS member Jin failed to appear at her first court hearing on July 14. The Seoul Eastern District Court's Criminal Division 9 (presiding judge Lee Ji-min) opened the first trial session for the woman, identified only as 'A,' a Japanese national in her 50s, who faces charges of forcible indecent act. The session ended without proceeding due to her absence.

Presiding judge Lee Ji-min stated, 'The defendant submitted a written document by mail, but I cannot determine exactly what it means. There is another scheduled hearing on the 16th at 11 a.m., so today's session will be postponed.'

The woman is accused of forcibly kissing Jin on the cheek during a fan hugging event held on June 13, 2024 — the day after Jin completed his mandatory military service — which was attended by 1,000 fans.

The police investigation began after an internet user filed a complaint against the woman. Police identified her through Interpol cooperation, but suspended the investigation in March of last year, determining that the inquiry would take considerable time.

The woman subsequently entered South Korea and voluntarily presented herself to authorities. Police forwarded the case to prosecutors in May of the same year, and prosecutors indicted her in November.
