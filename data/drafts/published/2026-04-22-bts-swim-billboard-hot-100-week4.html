BTS's single 'SWIM,' the title track from their fifth studio album 'Arirang,' has debuted at No. 10 on the Billboard Hot 100 in its fourth week of release. Billboard announced the chart position on April 20 (local time) via a chart preview, marking a five-spot drop from the group's No. 5 peak the previous week. The milestone comes as BTS makes their highly anticipated return after a four-year hiatus.

Despite slipping five places on the overall Hot 100, 'SWIM' continues to dominate the digital song sales category. The track moved 11,000 digital downloads this week, securing the No. 1 spot on digital song sales for the fourth consecutive week. This sustained performance highlights the enduring commercial pull BTS holds with their global fanbase, known as ARMY.

Topping the Hot 100 this week is Ella Langley's 'Choosin' Texas,' which held on to the No. 1 position for the seventh time. Olivia Dean's 'Man I Need' climbed to No. 2, while Bruno Mars's 'Ah Just Might' rounded out the top three. The animated film K-Pop Demon Hunters' theme song 'Golden' also maintained its No. 7 position for a second straight week.

The Billboard Hot 100 is the United States' premier weekly singles chart, combining streaming data, radio airplay scores, and sales figures to determine rankings. BTS's ability to hold a top-10 position through four weeks — while simultaneously leading digital sales — underscores the broad reach of 'Arirang' across multiple chart metrics since its release in 2026.
