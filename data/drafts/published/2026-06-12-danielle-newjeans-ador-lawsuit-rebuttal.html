Danielle's legal team fired back at ADOR's exclusive contract breach claims on June 11, 2026, stating that 'the activities were premised on a lawful termination of the contract.' The second pleading session in the damages lawsuit filed by ADOR against Danielle, her family, and former CEO Min Hee-jin was held that afternoon at the Seoul Central District Court's Civil Division 31.

At the hearing, Danielle's side argued: 'ADOR claims there was wrongdoing because of the collaboration with Emotional Oranges, but the NewJeans members judged that there were grounds for contract termination due to ADOR's violation of its duty to protect artists.' They added, 'Although this was not accepted by the court, it was a circumstance the members had reasonable grounds to believe.'

Danielle's team further countered: 'The ComplexCon performance and the establishment of a union were things that appeared commonly across all NewJeans members,' calling ADOR's position — that Danielle alone committed illegal acts and therefore could not continue together — 'contradictory.'

In response to ADOR's claim that 'Danielle is free to pursue entertainment activities,' Danielle's side pushed back, stating that 'Danielle is currently unable to engage in any entertainment activities whatsoever.' The team appealed: 'The penalty clause amount ADOR is claiming alone reaches approximately 100 billion KRW — what agency would take on an artist with such a massive damages claim hanging over them?' They also argued that 'this lawsuit is itself an intention to prevent Danielle from pursuing entertainment activities.'

The presiding judges then asked whether Danielle's position was that the exclusive contract with ADOR remains in force, or that it has since been terminated due to other circumstances. Danielle's team replied: 'Our position is that the contractual relationship is ongoing. However, because the plaintiff side is tormenting Danielle, the trust between the parties is being destroyed as the litigation continues.' They added: 'She is in a situation where she cannot work elsewhere — realistically, what agency would take Danielle on?'

The total amount claimed in this lawsuit, including the penalty clause, stands at approximately 33.1 billion KRW. The original claim was 43.1 billion KRW, but the amount was revised after the scope of the claim was partially adjusted during the course of the proceedings.
