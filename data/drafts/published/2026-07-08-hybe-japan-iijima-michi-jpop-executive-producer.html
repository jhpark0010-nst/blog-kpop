HYBE Japan, the Japanese headquarters of HYBE, has moved to significantly strengthen its lineup for the local J-Pop market by recruiting a legendary figure from the Japanese entertainment industry.

HYBE Japan announced on July 7 that it has newly established the position of 'J-POP Executive Producer' and appointed veteran producer Iijima Michi (飯島三智), a major force in Japanese popular culture, to the role.

Iijima Michi, the newly appointed Executive Producer, is a heavyweight who has led artist management, producing, and video content planning at the forefront of the Japanese entertainment industry for approximately 50 years since 1978. She is widely recognized as a pioneer who established Japan's distinctive 'multi-talent model' — expanding artists' activities beyond the music market into variety shows, dramas, and films. She is particularly well known, both domestically and internationally, as the central figure who managed and produced SMAP, the iconic group that defined an era in Japan, and guided them to stardom. As a film producer, she also demonstrated exceptional instincts, producing numerous box office hits including 'Midnight Swan,' which won Best Picture at the 44th Japan Academy Film Prize.

In a statement upon her appointment, Iijima said, 'Throughout my long career in the entertainment industry, I have created a wide range of content with a focus on each artist's individuality and human appeal.' She continued, 'At a time when the entertainment industry is at a major turning point, I am convinced that the future lies in fandom. I will work to expand the possibilities of Japanese entertainment through the fusion of fandom and entertainment, and to ensure that artists and content born in Japan are loved for a long time.'
