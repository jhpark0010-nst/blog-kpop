Ahead of BTS's upcoming Busan concert, the local accommodation industry has once again come under fire. Hotel and motel rates have surged in line with the concert schedule, and fans are growing increasingly frustrated by reports that already-confirmed reservations were cancelled and the same rooms relisted at significantly higher prices.

Cases of drastic price changes during the concert period have been widely shared across online communities and social media. Rooms that were previously bookable for around 60,000 KRW have been spotted listed at 760,000 KRW to coincide with concert dates. Some users have claimed that their existing reservations were abruptly cancelled, only for the same rooms to reappear at far higher rates.

An investigation by the Korea Fair Trade Commission and the Korea Consumer Agency found that accommodation prices in Busan on the concert weekend more than doubled compared to normal rates. Motels and hotels saw particularly steep increases, with some establishments found to be charging several times their usual prices.

Fan sentiment has turned cold. Many have said they will skip overnight stays entirely and head home immediately after the concert, while a so-called 'boycott' movement — aimed at reducing local spending in Busan — has been spreading on social media. International fans have also been sharing their own cancellation experiences, extending the controversy beyond Korea.

This is not the first time such a situation has arisen. The same problem of skyrocketing accommodation prices emerged during BTS's 2022 Busan concert. At the time, some establishments were criticised for excessive price hikes, and the city of Busan conducted on-site inspections and crackdowns. However, criticism continues that the same issues repeat themselves around every major event.

The city of Busan is currently conducting inspections of accommodation businesses ahead of this concert, checking for unlicensed operations, proper display of rate schedules, and compliance with reservation terms. The city is also reviewing the possibility of utilising public accommodation facilities. While forcibly capping private-market room rates has practical limitations, concerns have been raised that cancelling reservations and relisting at higher prices — or charging amounts different from posted rates — constitute consumer harm requiring regulation. Voices within and around the tourism industry are also warning that short-term profit-seeking by some establishments could damage Busan's overall tourism image, and that a fundamental solution to the recurring accommodation controversy is needed.
