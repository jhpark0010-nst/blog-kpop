K-pop is set to open and close the world's biggest sporting event. At the 2026 FIFA World Cup — hosted across the United States, Canada, and Mexico, and expanded to a record 48 qualifying nations — Korean artists have been placed front and center, from the regional opening ceremonies to the globally watched final.

The festival's grand opening will be led by BLACKPINK's Lisa and singer-songwriter Lee Jae. On June 12 (local time), Lisa will take the stage at the U.S. opening ceremony held at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles. She becomes the first K-pop girl group member ever to perform at a World Cup opening ceremony. Lisa will share the stage with global pop stars including Katy Perry, and is set to deliver the first live joint performance of 'Goals,' the official World Cup album track she recorded with Anitta and Rema.

At the same time, the official opening ceremony at a stadium in Mexico City, Mexico will feature singer-songwriter Lee Jae as a surprise hidden guest. Lee Jae gained worldwide recognition for singing 'Golden,' the theme song of the Netflix animated series 'K-Pop Demon Hunters.' He will perform alongside world-renowned opera singer Andrea Bocelli, singing the official theme song 'DNA,' produced by global producer David Guetta with contributions from Megan Thee Stallion and others. The song carries a message about football uniting people across borders and generations. Lee Jae has woven Korean-language lyrics into the track — including the line 'Even if I fall again, I will rise again' — and is expected to bring those Korean words to audiences worldwide through the global live broadcast.

BTS, who have completed their mandatory military service and returned as a full group, will close out the North and Central America World Cup. At the 2022 Qatar World Cup, member Jungkook became the first Korean artist to perform at a World Cup opening ceremony, presenting the official soundtrack 'Dreamers.' Now, the entire group has been named as the headliners of the World Cup Final halftime show — the first such halftime show ever introduced in World Cup history.

BTS will perform at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey on July 19, co-headlining the historic stage alongside legendary pop stars including Madonna and Shakira.

With Lisa and Lee Jae opening the festivities and BTS closing them out, the 2026 North and Central America World Cup is expected to serve as a clear milestone demonstrating that K-pop has moved beyond Asia to occupy the center of global mainstream culture. All eyes around the world are on these artists as they leave their mark on both global music history and the history of sport.
