South Korean police have launched a forced investigation into a Busan accommodation business suspected of canceling existing reservations and reselling rooms at inflated prices after BTS announced their Busan concert schedule. The Busan Metropolitan Police Agency's Anti-Corruption and Economic Crime Investigation Unit announced on June 2 that it has booked accommodation business 'A,' located in Busanjin-gu, on fraud charges and is currently conducting an investigation.

The establishment is suspected of calling guests who had booked rooms for the night of the BTS Busan concert, claiming that 'overbooking had occurred' to pressure them into canceling their reservations, then relisting the same rooms on online accommodation platforms at significantly higher prices.

One victim told police they had booked the room in January for approximately 90,000 won, only to receive a cancellation request from the establishment four months later. It was subsequently confirmed that the same room was being resold at a price more than eight times the original amount.

A police official explained that the investigation was launched on the grounds that deliberately deceiving customers into canceling reservations in order to profit from price gouging could constitute fraud. The official also emphasized that if criminal charges are substantiated through a thorough investigation, strict action will be taken.

Reports from concertgoers have been spreading across social media, with many claiming their reservations were unilaterally canceled around the BTS concert period, only to see the same listings reappear at dramatically higher prices. One user claimed that a room they had booked for 100,000 won was canceled under the pretext of a duplicate booking, then resold for 1.5 million won.

It was also found that approximately 80% of accommodation price gouging complaints filed with the Korea Tourism Organization's Tourism Complaint Center between January and May of this year came from foreign visitors. Police believe there are more victims who were unable to file official complaints due to language barriers, and plan to obtain the complaint center's records to expand the investigation to other accommodation businesses.
