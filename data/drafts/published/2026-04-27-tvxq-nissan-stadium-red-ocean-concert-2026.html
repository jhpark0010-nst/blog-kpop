TVXQ drew 130,000 fans across two nights at Japan's Nissan Stadium on April 25 and 26, 2026, creating a third 'Red Ocean' — a sweeping wave of red fan lights — at the country's largest concert venue. The performances came just two days before the group's 21st anniversary of their Japanese debut, and confirmed the duo's enduring popularity more than two decades after they first stepped onto the Japanese music scene.

On stage, TVXQ expressed their emotions openly. 'I thought I wanted to stand here once more, and now it truly feels like a dream,' they said. They also told the crowd: 'Thank you so much for always lighting up our world. From now on, the two of us want to keep shining so that you can shine even brighter.' They added, 'We hope the songs we sing can be a source of strength for you.'

At the members' invitation, 65,000 fans simultaneously shouted 'We Are T!' and raised their T-shaped fan lights, filling the stadium with a spectacular red wave. TVXQ debuted in December 2003 and first set foot in Japan in 2005. In 2013, they became the first foreign act to hold a solo concert at Nissan Stadium, and in 2018 they became the first to perform there across three consecutive nights. This time, their third concert series at the venue set yet another record as the most performances by a foreign artist at Nissan Stadium. TVXQ's total solo concert count in Japan now stands at 265 shows, with a cumulative audience of 6.31 million.

Unlike most K-pop groups who perform similar setlists in Japan as in Korea, TVXQ filled their approximately three-and-a-half-hour show entirely with Japanese-language songs — the majority of them original Japanese releases. U-Know Yunho and Max Changmin opened from stages on opposite sides of the stadium before moving to the main stage. After opening with 'Small Talk' and 'Reboot,' the Japanese version of their hit 'Why' sent the crowd into a frenzy, with 65,000 fans rising in unison to sing along to 'Keep your head down.'

The setlist moved between high-energy numbers and emotional ballads. TVXQ performed 'Choosey Lover,' 'Special One,' and 'Champion,' then shifted the mood with 'One and Only One' and 'Time Works Wonders.' The ballad 'Ashita wa Kuru Kara,' originally released in 2006 and re-recorded as a duo version in 2018, drew fans into quiet, nostalgic sing-alongs. The show also featured hits including 'Share The World' and 'We Are!' — both known as One Piece OST tracks — as well as 'Rising Sun' and '"O"-Jung.Ban.Hap,' performed with their original choreography from over 20 years ago. During the One Piece songs, the two members rode replica pirate ships 'Going Merry' and 'Sunny' through the red sea of fan lights.

U-Know Yunho explained the concert title: 'I named this concert Red Ocean with gratitude, as we are able to close out our 20th anniversary year together with everyone here. The jacket of last year's release Tsuki no Ura de Aimashou features an illustration expressing our wish to one day stand on the Nissan Stadium stage — a wish that had not yet been confirmed at the time. That dream has now become reality. We achieved it together with all of you here.' Fan Tanaka Mutsumi, 54, who has supported TVXQ for 15 years, said: 'Their performances are outstanding, but the bond between the two members keeps growing deeper — they feel like family. You can feel the sincerity when they say they always want to keep performing.' Another attendee, Yumi Yamazaki, 50, said: 'It is truly remarkable that a foreign artist has been active in Japan for over 20 years and is now holding their third concert at Nissan Stadium. We are so happy to have had the chance to see them here again.'
