BIGBANG made a major announcement during their second Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival performance on April 19, 2026, in Indio, California. Taking the Outdoor Theatre stage, the legendary K-pop group revealed that a new 20th anniversary album is finished and that a world tour will kick off in August — their first concert tour in nine years since 2017.

G-Dragon led the announcement with unmistakable energy: 'BIGBANG has reached its 20th anniversary. This is a restart. BIGBANG continues.' He then told the crowd, 'I want to share some special news here first — we have finished preparing a new album, and we plan to start a world tour from August.' The crowd erupted in cheers at the news.

Member Daesung expressed deep gratitude to fans, saying, 'I am thankful to be starting again at Coachella. This is a special night and a memory I will never forget.' He added, 'Thank you for cherishing us and letting us sing for 20 years despite all our shortcomings. BIGBANG will continue to be a group that delivers happiness.'

Taeyang also addressed fans warmly: 'It is BIGBANG's 20th anniversary. I sincerely thank you for continuing to love us for such a long time. I believe the only way we can repay you is through great music. We could not have made it this far without you. I love you.'

The second-weekend Coachella set was packed with hits spanning the group's career. BIGBANG opened with 'BANG BANG BANG' and rolled through 'FANTASTIC BABY,' 'Sober,' 'Tears,' and 'LOSER,' among others. The members playfully swapped lyrics — changing 'This place is Valhalla' to 'This place is Coachella' and 'Wow fantastic baby' to 'Wow Coachella baby.' Crowd sing-alongs erupted during 'Haru Haru' and 'Lies,' and each member delivered solo stages before closing with their 2022 release 'Spring Summer Fall Winter' after roughly one hour of performance. BIGBANG had also performed at Coachella the previous weekend on April 12.
