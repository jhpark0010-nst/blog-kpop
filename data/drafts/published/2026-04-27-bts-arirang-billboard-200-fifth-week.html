BTS has maintained a top position on the Billboard charts for the fifth consecutive week since the album's release. According to a chart preview article posted on Billboard's official website on April 26 (local time), BTS's fifth studio album 'ARIRANG' debuted at No. 4 on the main album chart Billboard 200, dated May 2.

The album continues to show stable chart retention alongside a steady consumption trend. According to Billboard, 'ARIRANG' recorded 62,000 album units on this week's chart. This figure is calculated by combining physical and digital sales, SEA units (sales equivalent from streaming counts), and TEA units (sales equivalent from digital track downloads).

Now in its fifth week, 'ARIRANG' has proven its broad public appeal by holding firm in the top five. As time passes, the album continues to attract a wide range of listeners, and its sustained performance points to a long-running run on the charts. The success of 'ARIRANG' marks a brilliant start to what is being called 'BTS 2.0,' and is especially meaningful in that an album reflecting the group's identity and future direction has resonated with music fans around the world.

BTS kicked off the 'BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG IN NORTH AMERICA' on April 25 at Raymond James Stadium in Tampa, United States. The North America tour spans a total of 31 shows across 12 cities, including Mexico City, Las Vegas, and Chicago. Tickets for all shows sold out quickly. Following the Tampa performances on April 25, 26, and 28, the group will meet fans at Sun Bowl Stadium in El Paso on May 2 and 3. BTS is the first Korean artist to hold a solo concert at that stadium.
