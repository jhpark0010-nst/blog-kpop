The Korean-culture-inspired animated film K-Pop Demon Hunters (also known by its Korean abbreviation 'Ke-De-Heon') is winning the hearts of American children, making its way into birthday parties, after-school classes, and fast-food restaurants across the United States.

The Los Angeles Times reported on April 29 (local time) under the headline 'Move over Elsa — the hottest entertainer at LA kids' parties is from K-Pop Demon Hunters,' highlighting the animation's enduring popularity among local children. According to the LA Times, requests for entertainers dressed as the girl group Huntrix or the boy group Saja Boys from K-Pop Demon Hunters have surged sharply at children's birthday parties.

Entertainers are professional performers who dress in costumes as specific characters and run activities such as magic shows, face painting, and games at parties. Where Disney's Frozen characters Elsa and Anna once held the top spot, K-Pop Demon Hunters has now claimed that throne.

At the entertainer company Funky Divas and Dudes, booking a 90-minute party featuring entertainers dressed as the girl group Huntrix costs $1,700 (approximately 2.52 million Korean won) — and yet it is currently the most in-demand theme. Co-president Marie Lazareski said, 'We are not doing anything other than K-Pop Demon Hunters parties these days,' adding, 'Sometimes we run K-Pop Demon Hunters parties five times in a single day.'

The popularity extends beyond parties into school after-school programs as well. Lazareski noted that after-school dance classes set to K-Pop Demon Hunters songs including the theme song 'Golden' are being held at schools in the LA area, saying, 'It's popular not only among kindergarteners and first graders, but also among kids in third through fifth grade.' A Spanish-language K-Pop Demon Hunters entertainer team also recently performed at multiple McDonald's locations near LA. Team leader Catherine Diaz told the LA Times that 'McDonald's specifically requested it,' and that when she asked 'But it's Christmas?', she was told the kids were dying to see Huntrix.

K-Pop Demon Hunters is an animated film released on Netflix in June of last year, telling the story of a K-pop girl group that fights evil spirits and protects the world through song. Nearly a year after its release, it continues to appear on Netflix's weekly Top 10 English-language films chart, and its theme song 'Golden' remains in the upper ranks of the Billboard Hot 100. Netflix has recently announced that a sequel to the series is in production.
