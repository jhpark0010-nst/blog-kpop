YG Entertainment is set to launch a new boy group in September 2026 — the label's first new boy group in six years. YG is now laying out a clearer blueprint and formalizing its roadmap for expanding its next-generation global IP.

On April 30, YG posted 'BABYMONSTER, TREASURE, AND UPCOMING ROOKIES | YG ANNOUNCEMENT' on its official blog. General Producer Yang Hyun-suk personally appeared in an interview to introduce YG's major projects planned for the second half of this year.

Yang Hyun-suk stated, 'In a previous interview, I mentioned via subtitles that we were planning to launch a new YG boy group this fall.' He added, 'At this point, we are thinking of introducing YG's new boy group in September. The number of members, which many of you are curious about, is 5.'

As YG's first new boy group in six years, the five-member lineup stands out as a contrast to TREASURE's larger roster. The lean member count points toward the birth of a 'complete group' that maximizes each individual's personality and skills.

Yang Hyun-suk also said, 'We will continue to reveal new IPs and rookies that YG has in the works, one by one,' suggesting that a strong rookie lineup will be built at YG alongside the previously talked-about next girl group tentatively called 'Next Monster.'

YG had already announced that this year would see new album releases and active global activities from BABYMONSTER and TREASURE, as well as a world tour by BIGBANG marking their 20th debut anniversary. As a result, 2026 is expected to be a significant turning point, with both the active schedules of existing artists and the large-scale emergence of new groups who will carry YG's future.
