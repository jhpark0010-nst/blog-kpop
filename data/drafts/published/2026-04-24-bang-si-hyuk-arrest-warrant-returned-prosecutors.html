South Korean prosecutors returned an arrest warrant for HYBE Chairman Bang Si-hyuk on April 24, 2026, citing a lack of sufficient justification for detention. The Seoul Southern District Prosecutors' Office Joint Investigation Unit for Financial and Securities Crimes sent the warrant back to police, who had applied for it on charges of fraudulent unfair trading under the Capital Markets Act.

Prosecutors stated, 'We determined that there is insufficient substantiation of the reasons requiring detention at this stage, and have requested supplementary investigation.' Police are expected to review reapplying for the warrant after conducting additional investigation. A police official said, 'We will make a judgment according to procedure.'

Bang is accused of deceiving HYBE investors in 2019 by telling them there were 'no plans to list the company on the stock exchange,' thereby inducing them to sell their shares to a private equity fund connected to him, after which HYBE was subsequently listed.

Police believe Bang received approximately 190 billion won — equivalent to 30% of the post-listing sale profits — under a private agreement with the private equity fund, and that his total illicit gains amounted to around 260 billion won.

Under the Capital Markets Act, it is prohibited to obtain financial gain through false means or to use fraudulent schemes in connection with financial investment products such as unlisted shares. Violations resulting in profits of 5 billion won or more are punishable by life imprisonment or a prison term of five years or more.

Police obtained intelligence on Bang in late 2024 and launched a preliminary investigation, then converted to an open investigation in June and July of last year after conducting searches of the Korea Exchange and HYBE. In early August of last year, Bang was banned from leaving the country upon returning from the United States, and was summoned for questioning a total of five times between September and November of that year. The court also froze approximately 156.8 billion won worth of HYBE shares held by Bang. However, police subsequently took no significant action for more than five months, citing legal review. Separately, prosecutors received a complaint against Bang from financial authorities in July of last year and assigned the case to the Seoul Southern District Prosecutors' Office, with the Financial Supervisory Service's special judicial police conducting the investigation under prosecutorial direction.
