Former NCT member Mark has officially launched his own independent label, Upper Room, just two months after departing NCT and ending his exclusive contract with SM Entertainment in April 2026. On June 3, Mark opened a new official account under the name 'Upper Room,' with the account ID explicitly including the word 'label,' signaling that this is his new agency. He also shared a trailer video on his personal account.

According to business registration records, 'Upper Room Co., Ltd.' has recently completed its registration as a management-based company, with Mark's legal name, LEE MARK, listed as its representative. This confirms that Mark has taken on the role of CEO of his own one-person agency.

Three short videos have been posted to the official channel. While they do not contain specific activity plans, they feature lyrical scenes of wind blowing through an open window and verses from the Gospel of Mark in the Bible, creating a mysterious yet sensory atmosphere. Fans have been expressing a wide range of anticipation for his upcoming solo activities.

NCT member Chenle also drew attention from global fans by leaving a congratulatory emoji comment on the post, showing their continued friendship.

Mark, who has been a core member of key NCT units including NCT 127 and NCT DREAM since his debut in 2016, previously shared his feelings about leaving through a handwritten letter in April. At the time, he wrote, 'At the point of completing a 10-year contract, I began to think about what the greatest purpose and dream could be for a person named Mark.' He added that he wanted to fully immerse himself in that dream.

Mark also promised, 'I will carry gratitude toward SM, the members, and the fans for the rest of my life, and I will do my best to show an even better version of myself going forward.' With his one-person agency now established, the music industry is watching closely to see what his second musical chapter will bring.
