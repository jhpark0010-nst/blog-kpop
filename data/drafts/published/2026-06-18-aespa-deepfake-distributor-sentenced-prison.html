A person who produced and distributed deepfake content maliciously superimposing the faces of aespa members has been sentenced to prison by a court. SM Entertainment has reaffirmed its zero-tolerance stance toward criminal acts targeting its artists.

SM Entertainment announced on June 18 that the individual — identified only as 'A' — who created deepfake videos of aespa members Karina and Winter and sold them for profit has been sentenced to 2 years and 6 months in prison. The First Criminal Division of the Daegu High Court also ordered A to complete 80 hours of a sexual violence treatment program and imposed a 7-year employment ban from institutions related to children, youth, and welfare facilities for people with disabilities.

In response to the ruling, SM stated it would apply a zero-tolerance principle and pursue full legal accountability for all copycat crimes and illegal acts that infringe upon the personality and rights of its artists. SM said, 'Through fan reports and our own monitoring, we have collected thousands of pieces of evidence of malicious posts and comments across various online communities and social media platforms,' and added, 'Based on this, we have filed complaints with investigative authorities, and the identities of a number of the accused have already been confirmed, with investigations currently underway.'

SM further noted that beyond deepfake sexual crime content, it is continuously collecting evidence — without exception — on all criminal acts that damage artists' reputations, including the creation of malicious rumors about aespa, repeated spreading of false information, writing of sexually harassing posts, and production and distribution of insulting or distorted content. The agency plans to carefully review targets for legal action in consultation with its legal representatives and to progressively expand legal proceedings.

The following is the full text of SM Entertainment's official notice regarding legal actions related to aespa's Karina and Winter: 'Hello, this is SM Entertainment. We are informing you that a guilty verdict has been handed down against the defendant in the case involving the production and for-profit sale of deepfake videos of our artists Karina and Winter — including a sentence of 2 years and 6 months in prison, an order to complete 80 hours of a sexual violence treatment program, and a 7-year employment ban from institutions related to children and youth.'

SM continued: 'We are closely monitoring fan reports and major domestic and international platforms — including Theqoo, Instiz, X, DCinside, Nate Pann, MLBpark, Instagram, Facebook, TikTok, YouTube, Ilbe, Bobae Dream, FM Korea, Naver, Daum, and others — and meaningful results continue to follow, as noted in our previous quarterly notice. We are also continuously filing complaints with investigative authorities against those who write malicious posts and comments related to our artists, on charges of violating the Act on Special Cases Concerning the Punishment of Sexual Crimes, the Act on Promotion of Information and Communications Network Utilization and Information Protection, and the Framework Act on Telecommunications. We have already secured extensive evidence and, with the assistance of platform operators and relevant agencies, are identifying anonymous posters. We declare that we will respond firmly — without any leniency or settlement — to acts of spreading malicious rumors, false information, sexual harassment, mockery, and distorted content. We will continue to do our utmost in every way to protect the rights and interests of our artists. Thank you.'
