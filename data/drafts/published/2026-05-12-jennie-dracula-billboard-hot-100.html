BLACKPINK member Jennie has entered the top 10 of the Billboard Hot 100, the main U.S. singles chart, with the song 'Dracula.' Billboard announced on May 11 (local time) in a chart preview that 'Dracula' by Tame Impala and Jennie jumped from No. 18 to No. 10 the previous week.

The song was originally released in October last year as a solo track by Australian artist Tame Impala. A remix version featuring Jennie was then unveiled in February of this year, generating significant buzz on short-form video platforms.

Streaming numbers for 'Dracula' reached 12.1 million, up 5% from the previous week, while radio airplay surged 20% to 23.1 million spins. The Hot 100 is a weekly chart ranking the most popular songs in the United States, compiled using a combination of streaming data, radio airplay scores, and sales data.

With this achievement, Jennie becomes the second BLACKPINK member to chart as a solo artist in the Hot 100 top 10. Fellow member Rosé previously reached No. 3 with 'APT.,' her collaboration with Bruno Mars.

BLACKPINK has also become the fifth group to have two female members each chart solo in the top 10, following Destiny's Child, Fifth Harmony, Fleetwood Mac, and the Go-Go's.
