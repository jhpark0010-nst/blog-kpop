ATEEZ has claimed their third No. 1 on the Billboard 200, the main U.S. album chart, with their 14th mini-album 'GOLDEN HOUR: Part.5'. Billboard announced the chart result on July 5 (local time) via a preview article, confirming the album reached the top spot.

According to Billboard, 'GOLDEN HOUR: Part.5' beat out strong competition including Olivia Rodrigo's 'YOU SEEM PRETTY SAD FOR A GIRL SO IN LOVE' and Drake's 'ICEMAN' to claim the No. 1 position.

This marks ATEEZ's third time topping the Billboard 200, following 'THE WORLD EP. FINAL: WILL' in 2023 and 'GOLDEN HOUR: Part.2' in 2024. Billboard noted that ATEEZ has now produced a total of nine top 10 albums on the Billboard 200 since 2022, making them the group with the most top 10 entries on the chart in the 2020s.

The Billboard 200 ranks albums based on total album units, which combine physical and digital album sales, streaming equivalent albums (SEA), and track equivalent albums (TEA). 'GOLDEN HOUR: Part.5' recorded 228,000 album units during the chart's tracking period, of which 223,000 came from physical and digital album sales — earning the No. 1 spot on Top Album Sales this week as well. Both figures set new personal records for ATEEZ. SEA accounted for 5,000 units, while TEA was negligible.

'GOLDEN HOUR: Part.5', released on June 26, captures ATEEZ joyfully embracing moments driven by instinct and sensation. The album contains five tracks, including the title track 'BAD'. The album also performed strongly in South Korea, selling 1.88 million copies in its first week according to the Hanteo Chart — another personal best for the group.

ATEEZ will hold a fan meeting at Korea University's Hwajung Gymnasium in Seoul from July 17 to 19.
