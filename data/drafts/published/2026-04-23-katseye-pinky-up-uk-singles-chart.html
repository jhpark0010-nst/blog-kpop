KATSEYE, the Korean-American girl group formed through a collaboration between HYBE and Geffen Records, has scored their highest-ever UK chart position. Their new single 'PINKY UP' entered the UK Official Singles Chart Top 100 at No. 14 on April 17, 2026 (local time), making it the highest new entry on the chart that week.

The Official Charts Company confirmed the milestone in a preview article, stating that 'PINKY UP' was the highest-charting new entry on the Official Singles Chart for the week, and that No. 14 also represents KATSEYE's personal best on the chart. The achievement marks a significant step forward for the group on the global stage.

'PINKY UP' is a funk-style track driven by a heavy, punchy bassline. The song carries a message of living boldly and staying fully immersed in the present moment. KATSEYE gave the song its live debut at the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival in the United States, where the performance was met with strong praise from audiences and critics alike.

BTS also continued their presence on the UK chart this week. Their fifth album title track 'SWIM' entered at No. 25, dropping seven spots from the previous week but marking its fourth consecutive week on the chart. Album track 'Body to Body' came in at No. 90, down eighteen places, also for the fourth week running.

Two tracks from the Netflix animated film K-Pop Demon Hunters also appeared on the chart: the original soundtrack song 'Golden' landed at No. 29, while 'Takedown' entered at No. 100. On the UK Official Albums Chart Top 100, BTS's fifth album ARIRANG held steady at No. 5, matching its position from the previous week.
