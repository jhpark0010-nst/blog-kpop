TWICE completed performances at Tokyo National Stadium in Japan on April 25–26 and 28 as part of their sixth world tour, 'This Is For,' drawing a total of 240,000 fans over three days, JYP Entertainment announced on May 1.

TWICE's appearance at Tokyo National Stadium marks a first for any K-pop group, and also a first among all overseas artists to perform at the venue.

Last year, the same tour brought TWICE to Japan's four major dome venues — Kyocera Dome Osaka, Vantelin Dome Nagoya, PayPay Dome Fukuoka, and Tokyo Dome. Including this Tokyo National Stadium run, TWICE has now drawn a cumulative 640,000 fans through their Japan tour alone.

ENHYPEN is launching a new world tour called 'Blood Saga' at KSPO Dome in Olympic Park, Songpa-gu, Seoul, running from May 1 to 3, Belift Lab announced on May 1.

The show's stage design and setlist reflect ENHYPEN's vampire concept, in keeping with the tour name, which translates to 'a saga of blood.'

After completing the Seoul concerts, ENHYPEN plans to continue the world tour across 21 cities worldwide, including a Japan four-dome tour as well as stops in Asia, North America, and Europe.
