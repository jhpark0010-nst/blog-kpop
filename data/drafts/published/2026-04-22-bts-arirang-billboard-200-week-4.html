BTS's fifth studio album 'ARIRANG' has landed at No. 3 on the Billboard 200 in its fourth week of release. Billboard announced the chart update on April 19 (local time), confirming the album had slipped from the top spot after holding No. 1 for three consecutive weeks — a historic achievement for any K-pop act on that chart.

The album moved 78,000 units in its fourth week, a 37% decrease from the previous week's total. The Billboard 200 calculates rankings using a combined unit figure that includes physical and digital album sales, streaming equivalent albums (SEA), and track equivalent albums (TEA), making the continued strong performance all the more notable.

'ARIRANG' made history as the first album by a K-pop artist to spend three straight weeks at No. 1 on the Billboard 200. The milestone drew widespread attention from fans and industry observers alike, underscoring BTS's enduring global reach even after an extended hiatus.

The album marks BTS's return after roughly four years away, a gap largely attributed to the members fulfilling their mandatory South Korean military service obligations. 'ARIRANG' features 14 tracks in total, with lead single 'SWIM' serving as the title track of the comeback.

Elsewhere on this week's Billboard 200, Ella Langley's 'Dandelion' claimed the No. 1 spot and Morgan Wallen's 'I'm the Problem' came in at No. 2. The Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival also gave a notable boost to several artists who performed at the California festival: Justin Bieber's 'SWAG' surged from No. 55 to No. 7, while Sabrina Carpenter's 'Man's Best Friend' climbed from No. 18 to No. 10.
