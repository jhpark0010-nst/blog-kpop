SHINee is set to return as a full group for the first time in approximately one year. The group will release their sixth mini album, 'Atmos,' across various online music platforms on June 1 at 6 p.m. KST. As it is their first full-group release in a long time, fan anticipation is running high.

This comeback is drawing extra attention as it coincides with the return of member Key. Key had previously suspended his activities in connection with the so-called 'injection aunt' (주사 이모) controversy last year.

At the time, Key's team explained, 'Through an introduction from an acquaintance, he visited a clinic in Gangnam-gu and received treatment from a person he believed to be a licensed doctor.' They added, 'In situations where visiting the clinic was difficult, he also received treatment at his home on several occasions.'

Key's team further stated, 'It was only after the recent controversy surrounding the individual's medical license emerged that he learned the person was not actually a doctor, and he himself has been experiencing great confusion.'

Key's team also issued an apology, saying, 'We take the seriousness of this matter very gravely,' and, 'He is deeply reflecting on his own carelessness and poor judgment.' Following the controversy, Key stepped down from his scheduled appearances and programs and halted his activities.

Now, approximately six months later, all eyes are on Key's return as SHINee resumes activities as a complete group in front of fans once again.
