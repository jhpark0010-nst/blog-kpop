BTS's title track 'SWIM' from their fifth full-length album ARIRANG has been named in a US copyright infringement lawsuit, with the group's label Big Hit Music fully denying the plagiarism allegations.

Big Hit Music told MyDaily on July 10: 'This lawsuit is nothing more than the plaintiff's one-sided claims,' adding, 'We clearly state that SWIM is an independently created work.' The label further stated, 'We plan to respond firmly through future legal proceedings.'

According to a report by Billboard on July 9 (local time), composers Steve Fucker, John Sandler, and Graylyn Johnson filed a copyright infringement lawsuit in a US court the previous day, alleging that BTS's 'SWIM' infringes upon a demo track of the same name that they created.

The plaintiffs claim there are substantial similarities between the two songs in terms of the hook, harmonic progression, rhythm, and sonic texture, as well as certain lyrical expressions. Named as defendants are HYBE, HYBE America, Big Hit Music, and the composers who participated in producing 'SWIM'.

The plaintiffs also claim that in March of last year, they shared the demo track with multiple music industry figures, and that during this process the song was shared with some of the composers involved in producing 'SWIM.' They further explained that they attempted to resolve the dispute with HYBE before filing the lawsuit but were unable to reach a settlement, ultimately leading them to take legal action.

Big Hit Music, however, has fully denied the plagiarism allegations and made clear its intent to fight the case legally. The dispute over whether copyright infringement occurred is expected to continue in US court.
