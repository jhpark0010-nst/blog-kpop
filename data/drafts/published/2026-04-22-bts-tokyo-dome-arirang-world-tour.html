BTS electrified 110,000 fans across two sold-out nights at Tokyo Dome on April 17–18, 2026, marking the group's first return to the iconic venue in 7 years and 5 months. Big Hit Music confirmed the milestone performances as part of the group's ongoing world tour titled 'Arirang.' The emotional reunion saw the members read handwritten letters to the crowd and deliver a setlist packed with fan favorites and brand-new tracks.

Key Points
  • BTS performed at Tokyo Dome on April 17–18, 2026 — their first time back in 7 years and 5 months
  • Both nights sold out, drawing a combined audience of 110,000 fans
  • The setlist included hits like 'Not Today,' 'MIC Drop,' and 'Butter,' plus new songs from the 'Arirang' album
  • New track 'Body to Body' featured a traditional Korean folk melody (arirang) that sparked a massive crowd sing-along

Why Is BTS's Tokyo Dome Return Such a Big Deal?

BTS last stood on the Tokyo Dome stage in November 2018 during a previous tour run. The nearly seven-and-a-half-year gap — filled with solo activities, military service, and a global pandemic — made this comeback (a new active phase after a hiatus) all the more meaningful for Japanese ARMY (the BTS fandom name). Both April 17 and April 18 shows sold out completely, with a combined 110,000 attendees packing one of Japan's most prestigious concert venues.

The scale of the turnout underscores how deeply BTS's fanbase has remained loyal through the years-long wait. Big Hit Music officially announced the concert results on April 19, confirming the sold-out status and total attendance figures.

What Songs Did BTS Perform at the Arirang Tour Tokyo Shows?

The setlist blended beloved catalog hits with fresh material from BTS's new album, also titled 'Arirang.' Fan favorites 'Not Today,' 'MIC Drop,' and 'Butter' were among the tracks performed, giving longtime fans the anthems they had been waiting years to hear live again.

A standout moment came during the new song 'Body to Body,' which incorporates the melody of the traditional Korean folk song 'Arirang.' When the familiar tune rang out through Tokyo Dome, the entire crowd erupted in a mass sing-along (떼창), a spontaneous moment that Big Hit Music highlighted as one of the night's most emotional highlights.

What Did BTS Say to Their Japanese Fans?

In a heartfelt segment, each BTS member read a personally written letter to the audience. They expressed that despite the long time apart, the fans' unchanged energy — their cheers and smiles — gave the members strength rather than the other way around. 'We missed you,' they told the crowd.

The members also shared that they visit Tokyo as travelers from time to time, walking its streets and imagining the daily lives of their fans there. 'Being able to come back is truly a joy and an honor. And we are happy. Thank you for waiting, and we sincerely love you,' they said, drawing an emotional response from the 55,000-strong crowd each night.

Where Is BTS Headed Next on the Arirang World Tour?

Following the Tokyo Dome dates, BTS is set to continue the Arirang world tour in the United States. The group is scheduled to perform at Raymond James Stadium in Tampa, Florida on April 25–26 and April 28, 2026. The stadium shows signal the tour's expansion into large-scale outdoor venues as BTS ramps up their global live schedule.
