BTS's North America tour is projected to generate an economic ripple effect of approximately 110.5 billion KRW (around $75 million USD) for the local region. On the morning of April 27 (local time) at 9:30 a.m., the El Paso County Commissioners Court in Texas approved a resolution to present BTS with the 'Estimado Amigo' award and to officially declare May 2–3 — the dates of their upcoming performances — as 'El Paso BTS Weekend.'

El Paso County recognized BTS's contribution to boosting local tourism, fostering community pride, and spotlighting El Paso as a major concert destination. The county also praised the social messages and public value embedded in BTS's music. In particular, Brooke Underwood, Executive Director of the El Paso tourism authority, projected that the two-day concert series would bring $75 million USD (approximately 110.5 billion KRW) in economic impact to the local economy.

Enthusiasm for BTS also spread through local fan activities. El Paso County highlighted a fundraising campaign organized by local ARMY (the group's fandom name) to illuminate the city's landmark 'Star on the Mountain' in purple for seven days, from April 28 through May 4. El Paso had previously lit the same landmark in red and white back in January to celebrate the announcement of the concert, demonstrating the city's keen anticipation for BTS's visit.

Meanwhile, BTS kicked off 'BTS WORLD TOUR 'ARIRANG' IN NORTH AMERICA' on April 25 at Raymond James Stadium in Tampa. After completing three shows in Tampa, they will head to Sun Bowl Stadium in El Paso, where they will become the first Korean artists to hold a solo concert at the venue.
