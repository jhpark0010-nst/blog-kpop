BTS took the stage at the FIFA North America World Cup 2026 final half-time show on July 19 (local time) at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, where Spain and Argentina faced off for the championship. It marked the first time in the World Cup's 96-year history that a half-time show — long considered the exclusive domain of the NFL's Super Bowl — was staged at a World Cup final.

The show opened with Madonna, known as the 'Queen of Pop,' performing her year-2000 hit 'Music.' A pre-recorded video set in a roller-skating rink played first, after which Madonna entered the stadium riding an off-road buggy, singing the chorus 'music makes the people come together.' Behind the wheel of the buggy were Brazilian football legends Ronaldo and Ronaldinho. The crowd erupted as football and music merged into one spectacle.

Venezuelan conductor Gustavo Dudamel then led an orchestra in a performance of the White Stripes' 'Seven Nation Army,' a song widely used as a football chant. Puppets from the children's TV programme Sesame Street joined the stage, miming along on guitar and drums. BTS followed, entering to their hit 'Dynamite.' The seven members, dressed in outfits combining black, red, and white, had performed in Paris, France the previous day as part of their 'Arirang' world tour and flew to the United States without a rest day — yet they commanded the stage without any sign of fatigue. BTS also adapted the song's lyrics to reference football and the World Cup, drawing an enthusiastic response from the crowd.

Major US entertainment outlets reported BTS's appearance as breaking news, praising their dynamic performance. Billboard lauded BTS for choosing their Billboard Hot 100 No. 1 hit and delivering explosive energy to the audience. Variety also praised BTS for lighting up the half-time show with a dynamic 'Dynamite' performance. Rolling Stone noted that BTS delivered a hit-song set in under 24 hours of arriving in the United States.

After BTS, a character from the American football drama Ted Lasso appeared and staged a substitution-board performance as if swapping players, bringing out Canadian artist Justin Bieber. Bieber played guitar himself and continued the energy with his song 'Everything Hallelujah,' released last year. Colombian pop star Shakira and Nigerian artist Burna Boy then performed 'Dida Dida,' the official theme song of the tournament, joined by Ugandan dancers in traditional festival costumes. The show paused briefly when an image of football legend Pelé appeared on screen — a tribute recalling his final professional match in 1977 at the old Giants Stadium, located right next to the current venue. The PS22 Elementary School choir from Staten Island, New York, then closed the show alongside Coldplay and Sesame Street puppets on a stage decorated with the word 'LOVE' and an image of the Earth, performing the song 'We Dance.'

The show was produced under the artistic direction of Coldplay frontman Chris Martin, and ran for just 11 minutes of actual performance time. BTS's appearance at the World Cup's first-ever half-time show was seen as a moment that showcased the global standing of K-pop. Considering that EJAE — who plays the lead character Rumi in the film 'K-pop Demon Hunters' — performed the World Cup theme at the opening ceremony in Mexico City on June 11, and that BLACKPINK's Lisa performed at a separate opening match in Los Angeles, Korean artists effectively bookended the tournament from start to finish. For BTS, it was also a meaningful return to the World Cup stage as a full group, four years after member Jungkook performed solo at the 2022 Qatar World Cup opening ceremony. FIFA President Gianni Infantino described the show as 'the largest of its kind in the world,' noting it was organised to support the FIFA Global Citizen Education Fund, which aims to raise $100 million to expand education and football opportunities for children worldwide.
