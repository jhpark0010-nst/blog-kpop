MBC's investigative program PD Notebook is set to air an episode titled 'MC Mong and the Chairman's K-pop Trade Secrets' on June 2, 2026, shining a spotlight on the various allegations surrounding One Hundred Label. The episode will examine issues ranging from unpaid artist settlements to alleged overseas gambling.

The controversy has grown significantly after The Boyz filed for an injunction to suspend their exclusive contract with the agency. Artists including Lee Mu-jin, EXO-CBX (Chen, Baekhyun, Xiumin), BIBI, and Lee Seung-gi subsequently notified the company of contract terminations, widening the fallout.

According to the broadcast, One Hundred Label is an entertainment company led by chairman Cha Ga-won, who accumulated substantial assets through the construction industry. The company grew rapidly after signing top-tier artists such as SHINee's Taemin and EXO members Chen, Baekhyun, and Xiumin. Pre-investment funds received in a single year are reported to have exceeded 115 billion won, yet some artists claim they have not received their settlement payments.

PD Notebook also announced that it has exclusively obtained internal accounting documents from the company, and plans to investigate how large sums of advance payments moved through the company's accounts. Chairman Cha Ga-won is set to appear on the program directly to present his position on the allegations.
