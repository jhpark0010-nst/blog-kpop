ADOR, the agency behind NewJeans, has appointed four new legal representatives. According to MyDaily's reporting on May 12, ADOR recently retained four lawyers from law firm Rihan and immediately filed a request to change the scheduled court date.

The five attorneys from Kim & Chang who had previously represented ADOR all submitted their resignations on April 24. The newly appointed law firm Rihan is currently handling the defense of HYBE chairman Bang Si-hyuk in a capital markets law violation case.

The Seoul Central District Court is set to hold the first formal hearing at 3:10 PM on May 14, where both sides' arguments will be officially examined. This marks the first full trial session in approximately two months since the first preparatory hearing held on March 26.

ADOR, which has been engaged in an exclusive contract dispute with the NewJeans members, filed a damages lawsuit worth approximately 43 billion KRW against Danielle in December of last year, alongside the termination of her exclusive contract. ADOR maintains that Danielle's side and Min Hee-jin bear significant responsibility for NewJeans' departure and the delay in their return, and intends to hold them legally accountable. The case is being heard by Civil Division 31 of the Seoul Central District Court (presiding judge Nam In-su), the same division that ruled in favor of former CEO Min Hee-jin in the first-instance lawsuit over the put option dispute between Min Hee-jin and HYBE.

Additionally, the Seoul Central District Court granted ADOR's application for a real estate provisional seizure against Danielle's mother and Min Hee-jin on February 2. The total claim amount is 7 billion KRW — it is reported that Danielle's mother's real estate was seized within the range of 2 billion KRW, and Min Hee-jin's within the range of 5 billion KRW.
