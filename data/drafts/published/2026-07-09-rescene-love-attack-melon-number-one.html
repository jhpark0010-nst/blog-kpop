RESCENE has completed a stunning chart-reverse story, with their title track 'LOVE ATTACK' reaching number one on Melon's TOP100 — South Korea's largest music streaming chart — nearly two years after its original release.

RESCENE — consisting of members Wonyi, Liv, Minami, May, and Zena — originally released 'LOVE ATTACK' as the title track of their first mini album 'SCENEDROME' in August 2024. The song has now claimed the top spot on Melon's TOP100, an achievement realized roughly two years after the track first dropped.

The dramatic turnaround was sparked by member Wonyi's active presence on her personal YouTube channel. Videos featuring her warm regional dialect and distinctive gyaru-concept content went viral across short-form platforms and social media, igniting a wave of interest in RESCENE as a whole. This renewed attention brought 'LOVE ATTACK' back into the spotlight, and the song rapidly climbed the charts on the strength of word-of-mouth buzz before finally reaching the top of Melon.

The fact that the song reached number one through organic public support long after its release has drawn wide attention in the Korean music industry as a standout example of a track proving its worth over time. Currently, 'LOVE ATTACK' holds number one on Melon's TOP100 and number three on the daily chart, as well as number three on FLO, number four in real-time and number five on the daily chart on Genie Music's TOP 200, and number four on both Bugs' daily and real-time charts.

The surge has also lifted RESCENE's older catalog. Other tracks including 'Pinball,' a second title track from the first mini album, 'Deja Vu' from their second single album, and digital single 'Runaway' have all entered the upper ranks of Melon's TOP100, reflecting strong and broad public interest in the group's music.
