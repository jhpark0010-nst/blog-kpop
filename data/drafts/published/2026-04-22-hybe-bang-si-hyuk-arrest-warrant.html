South Korean police dealt a major blow to HYBE, the country's top K-pop entertainment company, on April 21, 2026, when the Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency's Financial Crimes Investigation Unit filed for an arrest warrant against HYBE chairman Bang Si-hyuk. The warrant application, submitted to the Seoul Southern District Prosecutors' Office, alleges fraudulent securities trading in violation of the Capital Markets Act — a charge that places the label at its most critical juncture in its more than 20-year history.

Investigators allege that Bang deceived investors in 2019 by claiming he had no plans to list the company on the stock exchange, then sold shares to a private equity fund connected to him before proceeding with HYBE's IPO. HYBE became the first entertainment company in South Korea to list on the KOSPI in October 2020, and in 2024 it was designated a large business group — another industry first. Bang's legal team responded to the warrant application, stating they found it 'regrettable' given his 'long period of sincere cooperation with the investigation' and pledged to 'faithfully engage in all future legal proceedings.'

Bang Si-hyuk, born in 1972, founded Big Hit Entertainment — HYBE's predecessor — in 2005 and launched BTS in 2013. Under his leadership, the company grew from a small agency into an industry giant through acquisitions including Pledis Entertainment (home to SEVENTEEN), Source Music, and U.S.-based Ithaca Holdings. As of April 1, 2026, Bang holds 28.86% of HYBE shares, making him the largest single shareholder and an active member of the board.

Industry observers note that Bang's influence extends well beyond typical owner-management. He has served as Chief Producer on BTS's upcoming album 'ARIRANG,' personally driving creative decisions such as incorporating the traditional Korean folk melody 'Arirang' into the track 'Body to Body' and staging a large-scale comeback show at Gwanghwamun Square. He has also been involved in music production for HYBE acts including TXT, LE SSERAFIM, ENHYPEN, and KATSEYE — the U.S. girl group formed through an audition program in which Bang himself appeared as a judge.

HYBE now faces a sharp contrast of fortunes: the highly anticipated BTS comeback offers a significant commercial upside, while the chairman's legal jeopardy threatens content production and overseas expansion in markets such as India and Latin America. The company posted record annual revenue of approximately 2.65 trillion Korean won in 2025, but operating profit fell 72.9% due to rookie debut costs and business restructuring. HYBE's share price closed down 2.35% at 249,000 won on April 21. Cultural critic Jung Deok-hyun commented that 'the biggest risk factor for HYBE is the owner risk,' adding that public anxiety stems directly from Bang's absolute influence over the company's artists and direction.
