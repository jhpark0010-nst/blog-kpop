As South Korea's national football team celebrated a thrilling first victory over Czech Republic at the 2026 FIFA North America World Cup, K-pop stars have been making their presence felt both on and off the pitch, adding even more excitement to the global festival.

K-pop's commanding presence was on full display from the very opening ceremony. BLACKPINK member Lisa took the stage at the 2026 FIFA World Cup opening ceremony held at SoFi Stadium in Los Angeles, captivating football fans around the world. Lisa performed the official World Cup album track 'Goals' alongside Brazilian pop star Anitta and Nigerian artist Rema.

Lisa commanded the pitch alongside dozens of dancers, delivering a steady live vocal performance and intense choreography that instantly electrified the crowd. With this performance, Lisa set a historic record as the first K-pop girl group member to perform live at a FIFA World Cup opening ceremony. She had previously shared her feelings about joining the global project, saying, 'Music connects people from all over the world.'

The joy of South Korea's first win also blazed at street cheering events back home. At the 'KT 2026 Cheering: All Together. For Victory' event held at Gwanghwamun Square in Seoul, CORTIS attended and delivered a special performance. CORTIS was the only K-pop group to take the stage at the event, appearing alongside band Transfixion to perform the cheering anthem 'For Victory.' Members dressed in red uniforms reminiscent of the Red Devils cheered, 'The sun is hot today, but show us a passion hotter than the sunlight,' and performed a total of four energetic songs including their debut track 'What You Want', 'GO!', and their second mini-album title track 'REDRED'. CORTIS, who recently surpassed 2.31 million copies in first-week sales with their second mini-album and reached No. 3 on the Billboard 200, also posted short-form cheering videos on their official account.

The finale of this World Cup, where football and K-pop have come together perfectly, is set to be headlined by BTS. BTS will take the stage as headliner for the World Cup final halftime show, to be held in New Jersey on July 19. As the first halftime show ever introduced at a World Cup final, it is drawing intense attention from around the world. With Lisa opening the festival, and BTS set to close it out, an all-time lineup has been completed.

With strong support from the cultural and arts world, South Korea's national team under coach Hong Myung-bo is aiming to secure an early round-of-32 berth and top spot in Group A. Having come from behind to beat Czech Republic 2-1, Korea faces Mexico in their second group stage match at Guadalajara Stadium on June 19 at 10 a.m. local time. Depending on the result of the Czech Republic vs. South Africa match played earlier the same day, a win over Mexico could clinch first place in the group ahead of schedule.
