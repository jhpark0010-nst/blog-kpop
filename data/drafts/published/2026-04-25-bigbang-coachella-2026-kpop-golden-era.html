BIGBANG took over the Coachella stage in a triumphant reunion, drawing high praise from major international media outlets who called them 'the kings of K-pop' and building anticipation for a world tour set to begin in August.

BIGBANG successfully completed two performances at the Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival on April 12 and 19 (local time). The members officially announced that they will kick off a full-scale world tour starting in August, the same month the group originally debuted, saying, 'We hope our voyage continues.'

International media were generous with their praise. Forbes called BIGBANG 'the kings of K-pop,' writing that 'Coachella is the perfect stage for BIGBANG to begin this celebration — a group that laid the foundation for the global K-pop industry and profoundly influenced the musical style, fashion, and ambitions of nearly every group that came after them.'

Billboard highlighted BIGBANG's performance in its coverage of the best moments from Coachella's third day. The outlet noted that 'BIGBANG's popularity and influence in the K-pop world remain powerful,' citing 'passionate fans who rushed to the stage to relive their memories' as proof. Billboard also praised the setlist packed with mega-hits and stages that moved between unit and solo performances, saying the group 'took fans back to what can only be called the golden era of K-pop.'

The LA Times reported that 'BIGBANG, one of the most influential groups in K-pop, delivered a stage celebrating their 20th anniversary.' In its on-site review, the publication described the electrifying moments as the group 'drawing in the crowd with sensual and exciting party music,' underscoring BIGBANG's presence as they once again captivated global fans with their distinctive musical identity.
