BTS has topped Spotify's weekly global charts for four consecutive weeks with their album 'ARIRANG'. According to Spotify's latest weekly chart (tabulation period: April 10–April 16), BTS held No. 1 on both the Weekly Top Albums and Weekly Top Songs global charts, setting a remarkable record of four straight weeks at the top of both charts simultaneously. All 14 tracks from the new album charted on the Weekly Top Songs Global for the fourth week in a row, demonstrating the album's broad and consistent popularity. The title track 'SWIM' ranked No. 2 on the Daily Top Songs Global (dated April 16), marking 28 consecutive days in the top tier.

On the UK Official Charts announced on April 18, the fifth full-length album 'ARIRANG' settled at No. 5 on the Official Albums Top 100 (April 17–April 23), maintaining a top-tier position for four weeks. 'SWIM' entered the Official Singles Top 100 at No. 25, while album track 'Body to Body' also charted at No. 90. 'SWIM' held No. 1 on the Official Singles Downloads chart for four consecutive weeks, and ranked No. 2 and No. 3 on the Official Singles Sales and Official Physical Singles charts, respectively.

BTS placed five songs on Japan's Oricon Weekly Streaming Rising Ranking (dated April 20 / tabulation period: April 6–April 12). 'ARIRANG' tracks '2.0' (No. 1) and 'Hooligan' (No. 3) appeared alongside the group's mega-hit catalog entries 'Butter' (No. 6), 'Dynamite' (No. 7), and 'Permission to Dance' (No. 9). Half of the top 10 was filled by BTS songs, suggesting that the new album's success has reignited interest in their earlier hits. BTS also held strong on the Weekly Combined Album Ranking (No. 3) and Weekly Digital Albums (No. 4), while 'SWIM' ranked No. 6 and '2.0' ranked No. 7 on the Weekly Streaming chart.

The album's popularity has remained steady in Europe and Australia a month after release. 'ARIRANG' reached No. 2 on the Offizielle Deutsche Charts Top 100 Albums (dated April 17), holding a top position for four consecutive weeks. On the SNEP (French music industry association) Top Albums chart, it debuted at No. 1 in its first week and has sat at No. 2 for three consecutive weeks (tabulation period: April 10–April 16). On the Australian ARIA Charts dated April 20, the album ranked No. 3 on the Top 50 Albums and No. 2 on the Top 20 Vinyl Albums.

BTS continues its dominant run on the Gaon-based Hanteo Chart as well. According to the World Chart for the second week of April (tabulation period: April 6–April 12), BTS claimed No. 1 in the United States, Japan, and China for the fourth consecutive week, achieving a so-called 'Triple Crown.' This comprehensive chart is compiled using global data including physical album sales, digital track performance, and social portal metrics by country and region.
