The finale of the world's biggest sporting event was filled with dazzling performances by Korean global stars. The 2026 FIFA World Cup Final between Spain and Argentina was held on July 20 (Korean time) at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey, with BTS and actress and former top model Jung Ho-yeon at the center of the spectacle.

Jung Ho-yeon opened the festivities in style. The global star, who rose to international fame through the Netflix original series 'Squid Game,' made a surprise appearance at the official trophy unveiling ceremony held just before kickoff. Currently serving as a global ambassador for luxury brand Louis Vuitton, she took the stage alongside Spanish tennis star Carlos Alcaraz. The two carried out the Louis Vuitton trophy case — specially designed to hold the FIFA World Cup trophy — onto the pitch, raising anticipation and excitement for the final to its peak.

Jung Ho-yeon has also been riding a wave of success with director Na Hong-jin's new film 'HOPE,' her screen debut, which has surpassed 2 million cumulative admissions. Her appearance at the World Cup Final stage left yet another powerful impression on viewers around the world.

After the first half concluded, the World Cup's first-ever halftime show was unveiled, transforming the stadium into a massive concert venue. BTS took the stage as co-headliners, performing together as a full group. While member Jungkook had performed a solo stage at the 2022 Qatar World Cup opening ceremony, this marked the first time all BTS members had ever performed at a World Cup Final. Having made their comeback in March with their fifth studio album 'Arirang' after their mandatory military service hiatus, BTS had just wrapped up a solo concert drawing 90,000 fans at Stade de France in Paris — and took the New Jersey stage in less than a day's time, demonstrating the stamina of true global icons.

Dressed in matching bold red and black outfits, BTS appeared on the center stage on the pitch and performed their global mega-hit 'Dynamite.' Tens of thousands of fans in attendance and viewers around the world responded with explosive cheers and a massive sing-along.

The halftime show lineup beyond BTS was equally spectacular. Pop legend Madonna opened with her 2000 hit 'Music,' followed by Justin Bieber delivering an emotional performance of 'Everything Hallelujah.' Shakira and Burna Boy then took the stage with 'Dai Dai,' the official anthem of the 2026 tournament, bringing the atmosphere to its peak.
