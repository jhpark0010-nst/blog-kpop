Girls' Generation's Yoona is set to make her presence felt once again on the global stage. As global brand ambassador for Qeelin, a fine jewelry brand under the Kering Group, Yoona will walk the red carpet at the 79th Cannes International Film Festival on May 17 (local time).

Yoona has been officially invited to the event, which will be held at the Palais des Festivals in Cannes, France. Fans and media alike are already anticipating what kind of visual impact and aura she will bring to captivate audiences around the world.

Yoona previously attended the 77th Cannes International Film Festival in 2024 as a Qeelin ambassador, drawing overwhelming attention from fans and media worldwide. As she returns for the second consecutive year, expectations are high that she will leave an even deeper impression.

Beyond the red carpet, Yoona is also scheduled to attend the 'Kering Women in Motion' awards ceremony, which spotlights the creativity and contributions of women in the film industry, where she will interact with prominent figures from around the world. She is also set to participate in magazine photo shoots and interviews on location, further capturing the attention of global fans. Her continued appeal across acting, advertising, and editorial work has firmly established her as an icon both at home and abroad.

Yoona debuted as a member of the girl group Girls' Generation in 2007, and has been active as an actress since that same year, starting with the drama '9 Innings, 2 Outs,' and going on to appear in numerous works including 'King the Land,' 'Big Mouth,' and 'Hush.'

Most notably, she received much love for her role as French chef Yeon Ji-young, who time-slips back to her prime, in the tvN drama 'The Tyrant's Chef,' which concluded in September of last year. She is currently set to meet viewers as the lead actress playing UDI forensic examiner Im Do-eun in the Korean remake of the popular Japanese drama 'Unnatural.'
