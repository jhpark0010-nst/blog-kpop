BTS is generating massive global economic impact through what is being called 'BTSnomics' — following in the footsteps of pop star Taylor Swift's 'Taylornomics' — according to their label Big Hit Music, which announced the news on May 9.

BTS is currently conducting their world tour 'ARIRANG' across 34 cities worldwide, with a total of 85 performances scheduled.

Industry observers expect BTS's activities to create economic ripple effects across a wide range of sectors — including concerts, tourism, and accommodation — far beyond simple music consumption.

UK news agency Reuters projected that the total revenue from BTS's world tour will reach approximately $1.8 billion (around 2.7 trillion Korean won).

Reuters described the figure as 'comparable to Taylor Swift's The Eras Tour, which recorded the highest revenue of all time, and Coldplay's Music of the Spheres World Tour.'

Their label estimated that just the three concerts BTS has been holding in Mexico since the 7th (local time) will generate an economic impact of approximately $107.5 million (around 150.5 billion Korean won).
