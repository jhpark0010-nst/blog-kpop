South Korean police have moved again to secure the detention of HYBE chairman Bang Si-hyuk, who is suspected of deceiving investors ahead of the company's IPO and inducing them to sell their shares. According to police on May 7, the Seoul Metropolitan Police Agency's Financial Crime Investigation Unit reapplied for an arrest warrant against Bang on April 30 on charges of fraudulent unfair trading under the Capital Markets Act.

The reapplication came just six days after prosecutors returned the warrant. The Seoul Southern District Prosecutors' Office Joint Financial and Securities Crime Investigation Unit had sent the warrant back to police on April 24, stating at the time that it had determined 'the grounds justifying the need for detention at this stage were insufficiently substantiated' and requested supplementary investigation.

After receiving the returned warrant, police are understood to have conducted additional investigation into the possibility of Bang fleeing, destroying evidence, and the risk of reoffending, before concluding that detention remained necessary and resubmitting the warrant.

Bang is accused of telling HYBE investors in 2019 that there were 'no plans to list the company on the stock exchange,' thereby inducing them to sell their shares to a private equity fund connected to him, after which HYBE was listed. Police believe Bang collected approximately 190 billion won — equivalent to 30% of the profit from post-listing share sales — under a private agreement with the private equity fund, for a total alleged illicit gain of around 260 billion won.

Under the Capital Markets Act, obtaining financial gain through false statements or fraudulent schemes in connection with financial investment products such as unlisted shares is prohibited. If a profit of 5 billion won or more is made in violation of this provision, the penalty is life imprisonment or a minimum of five years in prison.

Police obtained intelligence on Bang in late 2024 and launched a preliminary investigation, before conducting searches of the Korea Exchange and HYBE in June and July of last year, converting the case to an open investigation. In early August of last year, police imposed a travel ban on Bang after he returned from the United States, and summoned him for questioning five times between September and November of that year. A court also froze approximately 156.8 billion won worth of HYBE shares held by Bang.
