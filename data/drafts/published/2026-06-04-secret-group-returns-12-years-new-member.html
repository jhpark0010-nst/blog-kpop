K-pop group Secret is set to resume activities after 12 years, according to label RBW. The group will be restructured as a trio, centered around original members Jeon Hyosung and Jung Hana, with a new member joining the lineup. Comprehensive content company RBW will oversee production. Song Jieun will not be participating in this comeback.

Secret debuted in 2009 and gained popularity with addictive tracks including 'Magic,' 'Madonna,' and 'Starlight Moonlight.' The group did not release new music after their fifth mini-album 'SECRET SUMMER' in 2014. In 2016, Han Sunhwa left the team, reducing the group to a trio. Although they never officially disbanded, group activities came to a halt in 2021 when their agency TS Entertainment closed down.

Virtual idol group PLAVE will embark on their first-ever world tour. According to their agency Blast, PLAVE will perform on September 12–13 in Incheon and September 26–27 in Kanagawa, Japan.

In October, the tour continues with stops on October 3 in Kaohsiung, Taiwan; October 10 in Bangkok, Thailand; October 17 in Singapore; October 24 in Taipei, Taiwan; and October 31 in Macau. Additional cities for the world tour will be announced sequentially.

Singer and actor Lee Seung-gi will hold a solo concert on October 24–25 at Blue Square WON Banking Hall in Yongsan-gu, Seoul, according to concert organizer DCI. This will be Lee Seung-gi's first concert since 2013, marking a 13-year gap. The concert title 'Gi-Seung-Jeon: Rak (樂)' combines 'gi-seung-jeon' (起承轉), meaning the flow of a narrative, with 'rak' (樂), meaning music, conveying the idea of Lee Seung-gi and his audience reconnecting through music.
