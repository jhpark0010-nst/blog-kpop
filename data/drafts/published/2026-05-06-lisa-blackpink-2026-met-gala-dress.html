BLACKPINK member Lisa captivated the world on the red carpet of the 2026 Met Gala, held on May 4 (local time) at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City. She made a striking impression in an elaborate gown that drew global attention.

The Met Gala is a charity fundraising event organized by fashion magazine Vogue. Since its founding in 1948, it has presented a specific theme and dress code each year. Because top stars from around the world appear on the red carpet dressed to that concept, the event is often called the 'Oscars and Super Bowl of fashion.' This year's theme was 'Costume Art,' with a dress code of 'Fashion Is Art,' calling for creative styling that interprets fashion as art.

At this year's event, Lisa was named a member of the Host Committee, demonstrating her elevated standing. This goes beyond a simple invitation — she was given an official role. The achievement is especially meaningful as it came just one year after her first attendance at the event.

Lisa also drew attention wearing an all-white see-through dress created by Hong Kong designer Robert Wun. The look immediately captured eyes with its distinctive structure: a sculptural arm extending from the shoulder and holding a veil decorated with Swarovski crystals.

According to the designer, the dress took approximately 2,860 hours to make and was inspired by the 2025 F/W Haute Couture collection. The additional arm structure extending from the shoulder creates an optical illusion of two different movements happening simultaneously, visually embodying a narrative of self-revelation through the staging of 'lifting a veil with one's own arm.' The look symbolically interpreted this year's dress code of 'Fashion Is Art.' In addition, the dress features a total of 66,960 white Swarovski crystals, and a tag bearing the phrase 'For Lisa' was attached alongside the designer label, adding to its special significance.

Meanwhile, all BLACKPINK members attended the event alongside Lisa, showcasing their global influence. Also in attendance were Ahn Hyo-seop, a voice cast member of the Netflix animation 'K-Pop Demon Hunters,' as well as Huntrix vocalists Lee Jae and Audrey Nuna, and aespa members Karina and Ningning.
