JO1 member Ohira Shosei is leaving the popular Japanese boy group. On April 27, LAPONE Entertainment announced through their official account that Ohira Shosei will terminate his exclusive management contract with LAPONE Entertainment Co., Ltd. and conclude all activities as a member of JO1, effective May 31, 2026.

According to the agency, during his hiatus period, Ohira Shosei had been conveying specific proposals regarding his future activities to the company. The agency stated, 'After repeated discussions with us, we have reached this conclusion out of respect for his wishes,' and added, 'We sincerely apologize to fans for delivering this sudden news.' Details regarding the previously postponed fan privilege event (a special fan meeting) will be announced separately at a later date.

JO1 is a 10-member Japanese boy group selected through the final episode of 'PRODUCE 101 JAPAN,' a Korea-Japan co-produced audition program, on December 11, 2019. The group has been praised for successfully transplanting the PRODUCE 101 series format to Japan and has enjoyed sustained popularity since their debut.

The following is the full official statement from the agency: 'Thank you sincerely for always sending your warm support to JO1. We hereby inform you that Ohira Shosei will terminate his exclusive management contract with LAPONE Entertainment Co., Ltd. and conclude his activities as a member of JO1, effective May 31, 2026.'

'During his hiatus, there were proposals from him regarding his future activities, and after repeated discussions with us, we have reached this conclusion. We sincerely apologize to fans who have always given us their warm support for delivering this sudden news. We ask for your understanding.'

'We ask for your continued unwavering support going forward. Regarding the postponed fan privilege event, we will provide separate notice in due course.'
