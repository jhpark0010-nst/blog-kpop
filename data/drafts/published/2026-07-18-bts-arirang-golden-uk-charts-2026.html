The Netflix animated film K-pop Demon Hunters original soundtrack 'GOLDEN' and BTS's fifth studio album 'Arirang' have both appeared on the UK Official Charts' mid-year rankings. 'GOLDEN' placed 8th on The Official Biggest Songs of 2026 So Far (Top 40), while 'Arirang' came in at No. 20 on The Official Biggest Albums of 2026 So Far (Top 40). Both lists were published by the UK Official Charts on July 16 (local time).

Regarding 'GOLDEN,' the Official Charts noted that the track 'spent 10 weeks at the top of the chart, setting the record for the longest No. 1 run by an animated act last year.' Australian artist Tame Impala's 'Dracula' also appeared on the songs chart at No. 14. The track was originally released as a Tame Impala solo song in October of last year, but a remix featuring Jennie of BLACKPINK was unveiled in February, gaining popularity on short-form video platforms and climbing back up the chart.

The Official Charts said of 'Dracula': 'The song was given a whole new lease of life when BLACKPINK's Jennie joined the remix, and it successfully crossed over into the mainstream.'

On the albums chart, the Official Charts described BTS's comeback album 'Arirang' as 'the best-performing K-pop project on the UK Official Charts so far in 2026.' Only six acts — including BTS, Drake, Harry Styles, and Olivia Rodrigo — placed a newly released 2026 album on this chart.

In this week's updated Official Singles Chart Top 100, 'Iconic by Mistake,' the collaboration track by HYBE girl groups LE SSERAFIM, ILLIT, and KATSEYE, charted at No. 70, down eleven places from the previous week. The song is an alternative pop track characterized by a heavy beat and unconventional sound, featuring an addictive hook with lyrics about 'becoming iconic by mistake thanks to your hate.' This marks the track's fifth consecutive week on the UK singles chart.

The K-pop Demon Hunters OST 'GOLDEN' rose four places from the previous week to No. 49, marking its 56th consecutive week on the chart. BTS's fifth album 'Arirang' charted at No. 35 on the Official Albums Chart Top 100, down seventeen places from the prior week, extending its run to 17 consecutive weeks on the chart.
