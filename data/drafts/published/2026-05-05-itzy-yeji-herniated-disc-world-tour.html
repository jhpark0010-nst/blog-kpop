ITZY member Yeji has been diagnosed with a herniated disc just two weeks before the group's scheduled comeback. On May 5, JYP Entertainment announced that Yeji had visited a hospital due to recent back pain, and that after detailed examinations, she received a herniated disc diagnosis along with a specialist's recommendation to minimize movements that place strain on her back.

JYP Entertainment further stated that as a result, Yeji's choreography and overall performance will be limited during ITZY's 3rd World Tour 'TUNNEL VISION' in JAPAN, scheduled for May 9 and 10. The agency added that while her performance will be restricted, Yeji intends to participate on stage to the fullest extent possible within those limits, in accordance with her own strong determination to meet fans.

The agency also expressed its apologies, saying it was 'sincerely sorry for delivering such sudden news to fans who had been looking forward to Yeji's performances.' JYP added that it will 'make every effort to prioritize the artist's health above all else and ensure sufficient treatment and recovery.'

Meanwhile, ITZY is set to make their comeback on May 18 with the mini album 'Motto'.

Below is the full official statement from JYP Entertainment: Hello, this is JYPE. We would like to provide an update regarding ITZY Yeji's health condition and her participation in the Japan concerts. Yeji recently visited a hospital due to back pain, and following detailed examinations, she received a herniated disc diagnosis along with a specialist's recommendation to minimize movements that place strain on her back.

We would like to inform you that Yeji's choreography and overall performance will be limited during the ITZY 3RD WORLD TOUR in JAPAN, taking place on May 9-10, 2026. Although her performance will be restricted, she intends to participate on stage to the greatest extent possible within those limits, driven by her own strong determination to meet fans. We are sincerely sorry for delivering such sudden news to fans who have been looking forward to Yeji's performances. We will make every effort to prioritize the artist's health and ensure sufficient treatment and recovery. Thank you.
