NCT's Taeyong released his first solo full-length album, WYLD, on May 18 at 6 p.m. KST across major online music platforms. It is his first new release following his mandatory military service, capturing an expanded musical world built on a more solid inner self and deepened sensibility.

The title track, also called WYLD, is a hip-hop song inspired by the instinctive movements of wild animals. Layers of intense bass, heavy rhythm instruments, and experimental sounds are stacked with precision to create an immersive, three-dimensional atmosphere reminiscent of the wild.

Through WYLD, Taeyong channels the artistic identity he has compressed over a long period of time. From the very opening, lines like 'Now watch me move baby / Break the frame' declare his intent to shatter his own mold, while 'From the jungle to the throne' expresses his confidence and ambition to keep moving toward his goals without stopping.

The raw energy builds and explodes through repeated lines such as 'Like animal' and 'Wild Yelling Loud just,' leading into the chorus 'Dancing in the wild.' In the lyrics, Taeyong sings 'I can't stop, keep Runnin',' expressing his will to reveal himself more freely and more fiercely.

The music video released alongside the album brings out the instincts latent within the human interior through Taeyong's energy, performance, and visuals. It captures moments where opposing elements of instinct and control collide and intersect, completing an intense and refined visual aesthetic reminiscent of a fashion film.

Taeyong participated in producing all tracks on WYLD. In addition to the title track, the album includes nine more songs: Storm, Hypnotic, I'm a Dancing Cactus, Mermaid, 404 Euphoria, Skiii, Hot, Feeling Myself, and Run, for a total of 10 tracks.
