'GOLDEN,' the theme song from the Netflix animated film K-Pop Demon Hunters, has won Song of the Year at the American Music Awards (AMAs). The award was presented on May 25 (local time) at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas, USA.

'GOLDEN,' performed by Korean-American artists Jae, Audrey Nuna, and Rei Ami, beat out nine competing songs — including Taylor Swift's 'The Fate of Ophelia' and Ales Warren's 'Ordinary' — to take home the Song of the Year honor.

Jae and Rei Ami took the stage to share their acceptance speech. 'It feels like we properly closed the spirit gate,' they said. 'We are so grateful to the entire K-Pop Demon Hunters team.'

K-Pop Demon Hunters, released in June of last year, is an animated film following Rumi, Mira, and Zoe — members of K-pop girl group Huntrix — as they protect the world against the evil spirit boy group Sazaboys, who target human souls.

The film has had a remarkable awards run: it won Best Animated Feature and Best Original Song at the 83rd Golden Globe Awards, Best OST at the 68th Grammy Awards, and both Best Animated Feature and Best Original Song at the 98th Academy Awards held this past March.
