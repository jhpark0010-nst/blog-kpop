ADOR and former NewJeans member Danielle, along with former ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin, are heading back to court over a damages lawsuit worth hundreds of billions of Korean won. The Seoul Central District Court's Civil Division 31 (presiding judge Nam In-su) is holding the third pleading session on the afternoon of July 2 for the damages claim filed by ADOR against Danielle, her family, and former CEO Min Hee-jin.

ADOR filed the lawsuit after notifying Danielle of the termination of her exclusive contract in December of last year. The label initially sought approximately 43.1 billion KRW in penalty fees and damages. After replacing its legal representatives, the claimed amount was adjusted to approximately 33.09 billion KRW. ADOR explained, 'In the process of the new representatives reviewing the case, the claims were partially restructured and the amount was also adjusted.'

At the second pleading session held previously, both sides clashed sharply over whether Danielle had violated her exclusive contract. ADOR's side stated, 'Unlike the other members, Danielle committed serious contract violations carried out independently, including pursuing a collaboration with a US band and taking unilateral actions,' presenting this as grounds for terminating the exclusive contract.

Danielle's side countered, 'We reviewed the possibility of other activities because we were convinced that the contract with ADOR would be lawfully terminated.' They also argued, 'It is unreasonable to view Danielle as having committed independent unlawful acts based solely on the US band collaboration, excluding matters that commonly applied to other members as well, such as attending the Hong Kong ComplexCon.'

Danielle's side also claimed that the massive penalty fee claim has made it practically difficult to find a new agency, thereby restricting her entertainment activities. ADOR responded, 'There is no reason to block Danielle's activities, and she is free to work.'

The exclusive contract dispute between NewJeans, who debuted in July 2022, and their agency ADOR has entered a prolonged phase. The conflict surfaced in November 2024 when the members demanded the reinstatement of former CEO Min Hee-jin; when that demand was not accepted, the members declared contract termination citing ADOR's breach of contract. ADOR countered by asserting the contract remained valid and immediately filed lawsuits, leaving both sides on an unresolved parallel track with no sign of compromise. In this damages lawsuit, ADOR maintains that 'Danielle, her family, and former CEO Min Hee-jin bear responsibility for causing the NewJeans exclusive contract dispute and for the members' departure and delayed return.' Danielle's side, meanwhile, continues to assert the legitimacy of the contract termination and the injustice of the activity restrictions, and fierce arguments from both parties are expected to continue at today's hearing.
