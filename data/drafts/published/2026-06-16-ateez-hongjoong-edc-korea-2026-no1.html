Electric Daisy Carnival Korea (EDC Korea) has revealed its full lineup for 2026. The festival is set to take place on October 3 and 4 at Inspire in Incheon. The complete lineup was unveiled to the public for the first time at a press conference held on June 15.

EDC began in Los Angeles in 1997 and has grown since 2011 into one of the world's largest electronic music festivals, drawing hundreds of thousands of attendees annually in Las Vegas and expanding to Mexico, Brazil, Japan, and beyond. EDC Korea 2026 is also expected to serve as a platform for the next generation of EDM artists. A music industry insider said, 'The moment new artists take the stage at EDC Korea 2026 is a scene that shows the future of EDM.'

The full DJ lineup revealed at the June 15 press conference includes Tiësto, DJ Snake, ILLENIUM B2B DABIN, FISHER, Alok, Subtronics, R3HAB, Vini Vici, Cosmic Gate, Kayzo, and Sara Landry, among other world-class artists. Notably, ATEEZ's Hongjoong will join EDC Korea 2026 under the name NO1, marking his first solo festival appearance, which adds special significance to the event.

EDC Korea 2026 also designated the audience itself as 'the most important headliner of all,' conveying the message 'AND THE MOST IMPORTANT HEADLINER OF ALL — YOU,' reaffirming the philosophy that the true stars are the fans who create the light together. At the press conference, a video message from Pasquale Rotella, founder of Insomniac — the company behind EDC — was shown. He said, 'Korea feels like home,' expressing his deep affection for Korean fans. Techno artist Sara Landry announced she is preparing a K-pop remix for Korean fans, and Hongjoong shared his excitement about his first solo stage.

One Pulse Group, the global live entertainment company leading EDC Korea 2026 and operating across Asia, North America, and the Middle East, has collaborated since its founding in 2015 with global EDM artists including Martin Garrix, Marshmello, Steve Aoki, and ILLENIUM, as well as Korean artists including G-Dragon, EXO, aespa, IVE, and Kim Jong-kook. The company began producing EDC Korea through a partnership with Insomniac in 2025 and established OPG Korea to support ongoing Korean projects. Founder and CEO Luffy Huang stated, 'Live music connects people across languages, cultures, and borders,' adding that he hopes 'EDC Korea 2026 will be a festival that maintains its global identity while being meaningful to Korean fans and executed with excellence from start to finish.'

One major change for EDC Korea 2026 is the shift in dates from April to October. October 3 and 4 fall during a Korean public holiday period and coincide with China's Golden Week, allowing audiences from across Asia to attend more comfortably. The venue, Inspire, is located approximately five minutes by car from Incheon International Airport, offering excellent accessibility. Fan feedback from last year's event has been directly incorporated into the 2026 planning, with improvements promised in weather response, stage production, sound quality, crowd flow, and on-site operations. VIP-exclusive benefits have also been newly introduced. The next ticket sale for EDC Korea 2026 is scheduled for June 18 at 10 a.m. KST via NOL Ticket and Ticketlink.
