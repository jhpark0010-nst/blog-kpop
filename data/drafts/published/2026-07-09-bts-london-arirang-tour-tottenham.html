BTS held two nights of their ARIRANG world tour at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium in London, UK on July 6 and 7 (local time), drawing approximately 130,000 fans over both days, their agency Big Hit Music announced on July 9.

According to Live Nation, which organized the concerts, the approximately 65,000 attendees per show set a new record for the highest single-concert attendance at Tottenham Hotspur Stadium since the venue opened in April 2019. Both London shows sold out the moment tickets went on sale.

This marks the first time BTS has held a full-group solo concert in the UK in seven years, since their historic June 2019 performance at Wembley Stadium — the first Korean artist to headline the iconic venue.

During the shows, the members performed tracks from their fifth album including 'Hooligan,' 'Normal,' and 'SWIM,' alongside classic hits such as 'Idol' and 'Fire.'

BTS shared their thoughts after the performances: 'We felt the same emotion we experienced at Wembley Stadium seven years ago. We had a truly wonderful time today thanks to your passion.' They added, 'Many memories of past concerts came back to us, and we were once again amazed that London is such a passionate city.'

UK outlets Rolling Stone UK and The Guardian each gave the concert a perfect five-star review. Rolling Stone wrote that 'BTS have reclaimed their throne as the biggest band in the world,' while The Guardian called it 'an unbelievably joyful spectacle.' BTS launched the ARIRANG tour — the largest world tour in K-pop history — starting in Goyang in April, and will next perform at Allianz Arena in Munich, Germany on July 11 and 12.
