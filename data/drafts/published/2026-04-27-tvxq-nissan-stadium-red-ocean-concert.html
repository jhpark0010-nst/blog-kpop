TVXQ held their third solo concert 'RED OCEAN' at Nissan Stadium — Japan's largest concert venue — on April 25, drawing 65,000 fans per night and 130,000 over two days. After the show, the duo met with press and said, 'It feels like we experienced the greatest luxury a singer can have. We are so grateful to our fans.'

Yunho said, 'Standing on the Nissan Stadium stage, I was overwhelmed by its sheer scale. Fans in front were crying, and some were smiling brightly, so I felt like I could cry at any moment. There were several moments like that today. The venue was massive, but the warmth of the fans made it even more emotional.'

The two members performed more than 30 songs over approximately three and a half hours, moving all across the stadium as waves of red — the group's signature color — filled the stands. Yet they greeted Korean and Japanese press with energy and warmth, their faces lighting up every time the crowd numbers were mentioned.

Changmin said when asked for his thoughts, 'It is so special. I really wanted to stand on the Nissan Stadium stage one more time. Honestly, even among ourselves we sometimes wondered, could we really fill a venue this big again?'

TVXQ debuted in December 2003 with 'Hug' and made their Japan debut in 2005, building their popularity from the ground up on small stages. This concert served as the finale of a project commemorating their 20th anniversary in Japan, and was also a celebration shared with fans ahead of their 21st Japan debut anniversary on April 27.

Despite Yunho turning 40 and Changmin turning 38 this year, both members sprinted across the full stadium just as they had at their previous Nissan Stadium concerts. They ran the entire lap of the stadium while performing 'Somebody To Love,' released in 2005 during their first year in Japan. Yunho said, 'When the concert directors told me we should run again this time, I thought to myself, really? Ha ha. But going around the whole stadium to greet everyone felt right. Adrenaline kicked in, I shouted let's go, and that's how I did it.' He added, 'TVXQ's strength is giving everything on any stage — not just performing well, but with a different kind of feeling. I think the Somebody To Love stage showed that symbolically. Quite a few people came wanting to see exactly that, and whether we do it well or not, we have to answer that expectation.'
