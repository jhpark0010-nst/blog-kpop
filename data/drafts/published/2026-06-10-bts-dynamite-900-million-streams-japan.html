BTS hit song 'Dynamite' has surpassed 900 million cumulative streams on Japan's Oricon chart, label Big Hit Music announced on June 10. This makes it the eighth song in Oricon history to reach 900 million streams, and the first ever by an overseas artist.

'Dynamite' has recorded a cumulative total of 900.95 million streams as of this week. BTS is the sixth artist to hold a 900-million-stream record in Japan, following YOASOBI, Yuuri, Official HIGE DANdism, and Mrs. GREEN APPLE, among others.

BTS's 'Permission to Dance' has also surpassed 500 million cumulative streams. This makes it the third BTS song to cross the 500-million-stream mark on Oricon, after 'Dynamite' and 'Butter'. BTS is also the only overseas artist to have a single exceed 500 million streams in Japan.

'Dynamite' is a disco-pop track with a bright and upbeat feel, capturing the preciousness of everyday life and the special quality of living. 'Permission to Dance' is a dance-pop song carrying the message that 'you can dance however your heart desires, without needing anyone's permission,' dedicated to those who have had an exhausting day.

Both songs were released during the COVID-19 pandemic and brought comfort to listeners around the world. Both tracks also reached number one on the US Billboard main singles chart, the Hot 100.
