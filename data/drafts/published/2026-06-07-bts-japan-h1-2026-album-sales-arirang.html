BTS has topped Japan's first-half 2026 album sales chart with their fifth studio album 'ARIRANG,' according to Billboard Japan's mid-year 'Top Album Sales' chart released on June 6. The album sold 706,961 copies, placing BTS above local Japanese artists at number one.

A total of six K-pop-related acts appeared in the chart's Top 20, reflecting the continued strength of the Korean Wave in Japan. HYBE Japan's local boy group &TEAM placed second with their third mini-album 'We on Fire,' while ENHYPEN's seventh mini-album 'THE SIN : VANISH' came in at fourth.

TXT's eighth mini-album '7TH YEAR: When the Wind Briefly Stopped in the Thorny Thicket' ranked seventh, and TWS's 'NO TRAGEDY' landed at tenth. BTS, &TEAM, ENHYPEN, TXT, and TWS are all signed to labels under HYBE.

Regarding &TEAM's performance, HYBE commented, 'It is highly significant that this is a team introduced by a HYBE Japan label that transplanted the K-pop production system.' The company added, 'The combination of K-pop-style training, production, and management models with local Japanese sensibilities has led to strong results.'

Outside of HYBE acts, TREASURE's 'LOVE PULSE' rounded out the K-pop presence in the chart, coming in at thirteenth place.
