Co-ed K-pop group KARD is officially disbanding after nine years together. Agency DSP Media announced on July 6 that, following careful discussions with all four members, the company has agreed to conclude KARD's journey at the end of their current activities.

According to DSP Media, KARD will release their debut full-length album 'Where To Now? (Part.2) : NOWHERE' on July 28, after which they will embark on a world tour. These activities will mark the group's final official schedule. The agency added, 'We thank the fans who have walked alongside KARD for so long, and ask for your warm support for the new paths each member will take going forward.'

KARD is composed of BM, J.Seph, Jeon Somin, and Jeon Jiwoo. The group made their official debut in 2017 as one of the rare co-ed acts in the Korean entertainment industry. Even before their debut, they drew widespread attention with tracks such as 'Oh NaNa' and 'Don't Recall,' earning a large and devoted fanbase both in Korea and internationally.

Below is the full official statement from DSP Media: 'Hello, this is DSP Media. First, we sincerely thank HIDDEN KARD, who have always cherished and supported our artist KARD. KARD will release their debut full-length album 'Where To Now? (Part.2) : NOWHERE' on July 28, followed by a world tour.'

'After careful discussions with all four members, we have agreed to conclude KARD's journey at the end of these activities. We once again thank HIDDEN KARD for the long road you have shared with KARD, and ask for your continued warm encouragement for the new futures each member will pursue.'

'We hope the new album and tour, prepared with the members' whole hearts, will remain as precious memories for HIDDEN KARD. We ask for your unwavering support for these final activities, which will be filled with the most KARD-like moments until the very end. Thank you.'
