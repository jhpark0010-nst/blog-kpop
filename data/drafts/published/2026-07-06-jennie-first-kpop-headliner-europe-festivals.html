BLACKPINK's Jennie has become the first K-pop artist to headline two major European music festivals, performing at Denmark's Roskilde Festival on July 3 and Poland's Open'er Festival on July 4, 2026. Her agency OA Entertainment (Odd Atelier) announced the news on July 6.

Jennie took the stage at both festivals on consecutive days. She is the first K-pop act ever to appear as a headliner at two large-scale European music festivals.

During her sets, Jennie performed a selection of her signature tracks, including 'Mantra,' 'ExtraL,' 'Starlight,' and 'like JENNIE.'

Thomas Søndergby Jepsen, music director of Roskilde, said, 'Jennie's inclusion further solidified Roskilde's identity as a festival that reflects current trends and moves toward the future.'

The organizers of Open'er also introduced Jennie as 'one of the most influential female artists of this era.'

Jennie has been active in the global music market, reaching No. 10 on the Billboard Hot 100 with 'Dracula,' her collaboration with Tame Impala. She is also set to appear at additional major international festivals, including Spain's 2026 Mad Cool Festival, Lollapalooza Chicago in the United States, and Summer Sonic 2026 in Japan.
