BOYNEXTDOOR's first full-length album 'HOME' sold 1,085,715 copies in its first week on sale, achieving million-seller status, their agency KOZ Entertainment announced on June 15. With this achievement, BOYNEXTDOOR has now sold over one million copies across four consecutive releases.

The four albums are the mini-albums '19.99', 'No Genre', and 'The Action', followed by this latest full-length record. The album 'HOME' contains autobiographical stories from the members, and its title track 'VIRAL' entered the Melon daily chart at No. 41 on June 13.

In other news, girl group ILLIT will release their second Japanese single, 'I Got Your Back', on July 29, according to their agency Belift Lab. The digital release will come three days earlier, on July 26.

'I Got Your Back' is a single about girls growing through repeated worries and hardships. It will include a total of three tracks, among them 'Sunday Morning', the opening theme for Season 2 of the Japanese anime 'It's Time for Princess Punishment', which was released in January. ILLIT surprised fans with the announcement of the new release at their first local tour concert, held at the Aichi Arts Center in Japan on June 13 and 14.

Meanwhile, girl group Hearts2Hearts will hold their first-ever pop-up store since their debut, running from June 23 to July 1 at the Event Plaza on the basement level of The Hyundai Seoul, to celebrate the release of their mini-album 'Lemon Tang', SM Entertainment announced.

The pop-up store will feature sculptures capturing the members' charms, photo zones, a game themed around defeating the 'Lemon Sun' — the main concept of the new album — and other varied content. Visitors can also hear welcome messages recorded by the members themselves. Hearts2Hearts previously made waves in the K-pop scene this year with their hit dance track 'RUDE!', which sparked a house music trend.
