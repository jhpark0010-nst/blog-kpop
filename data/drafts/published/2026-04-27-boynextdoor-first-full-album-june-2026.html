BOYNEXTDOOR (members Sungho, Lew, Myungjae, Taesan, Leehan, and Woonhak) is set to make a comeback with their first full-length studio album. According to MyDaily reporting on April 27, the group will release a pre-release single in May before dropping their debut full album in June 2026.

This will mark the first time BOYNEXTDOOR has released a full-length album since their debut in 2023. Having participated directly in the songwriting process, the group has built their own distinct identity through relatable charm and lyrics that resonate with fans, consistently hitting career highs with each release.

Their track record speaks for itself. Last year's fifth mini album 'The Action' made them three-consecutive-million-sellers, following their third mini album '19.99' and fourth mini album 'No Genre.' Their digital single 'Only Today I LOVE YOU' ranked 12th on the Melon 2025 Year-End Chart, the highest position among male idol groups.

Having proven themselves in both physical and digital music, the group has also shown their strengths on stage. Last year they held their first solo tour 'KNOCK ON Vol.1' across 13 cities for 23 performances, and in August of the same year they took the stage at Lollapalooza Chicago, one of the world's largest music festivals.

Having built a well-rounded record across albums, digital releases, and live performances, BOYNEXTDOOR has earned the nickname '믿보넥' — meaning a group you can always trust to deliver — thanks to their solid skills and commanding stage presence. Fans are eagerly anticipating what kind of summer the group will usher in, starting with the pre-release single and leading up to their first full album.
