Korean electronic music has seen remarkable growth recently, according to data released by Spotify on July 20, 2026. The world's largest music streaming platform reported that domestic electronic music streams surged 60.4% year-on-year in June 2026, signaling a major shift in listener habits.

The growth trend extends beyond a single month. Electronic music streams in South Korea for the first half of 2026 rose 46.2% compared to the second half of 2025. The genre's share of total music streaming also expanded, climbing from 3.06% to 3.42%.

Spotify highlighted that domestic artists were the key drivers of this growth. 'The streaming volume of Korean electronic artists in the first half of this year increased by more than 300% compared to the second half of last year,' Spotify stated. 'During the same period, the share of Korean artists within domestic electronic music streaming expanded significantly, from 9.8% to 24.4%,' the platform added.

In response to this growth, Spotify is partnering with UNBORN SOUNDS, a creative collective dedicated to discovering and nurturing the next generation of DJs, to expand support for the domestic electronic music community.

UNBORN SOUNDS runs artist development programs, live performances, community events, and creative support initiatives.

Spotify plans to continue supporting domestic artists by introducing music from up-and-coming DJs to listeners both in Korea and abroad through playlist updates.
