The Grammy Awards has established a new category encompassing K-pop, with the 69th Grammy Awards ceremony set for February 7, 2027. U.S. broadcaster ABC reported on June 16 (local time) that the Grammy Awards will add five new categories to next year's ceremony: 'Best Asian Pop Music Performance,' 'Best Latin Song,' 'Best Traditional Pop Vocal Performance,' 'Best R&B Collaboration or Duo/Group Performance,' and 'Best Traditional Folk Album.'

The category drawing the most attention is Best Asian Pop Music Performance. It was created to honor excellence in Asian pop music — including K-pop, J-pop (Japanese pop music), and C-pop (Chinese pop music) — that meaningfully uses the language of one or more Asian countries and is produced by or recognized as representing Asian artists.

The Recording Academy explained that the new category was established in recognition of the growing influence Asian pop music is having on the global music industry.

K-pop artists including BTS and BLACKPINK's Rosé have previously competed for main Grammy awards but have not taken home a win. Earlier this year, 'Golden,' the original soundtrack from the Netflix animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters,' became the first K-pop track to win a Grammy Award.

With the addition of a category specifically targeting Asian pop, there are now forecasts that K-pop artists such as BTS are much closer to winning. Gold Derby, a media outlet that runs a U.S. pop culture awards prediction site, said, 'The Grammy expansion is good news for BTS and (country singer) Ella Langley.'

Existing award rules were also revised. For the 'Best New Artist' award, the maximum number of times an artist can be nominated has increased from three to four, and the required proportion of newly recorded material on an album has been lowered from 75% to 66%.
