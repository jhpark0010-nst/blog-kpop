The 2026 Melon Music Awards (MMA2026) will be held on November 14 and 15 at Gocheok Sky Dome in Seoul, Kakao Entertainment's music platform Melon announced on June 9. This marks the first time in MMA history that the ceremony will span two consecutive days. Organizers explained the expanded format is intended to create the biggest K-pop festival possible, bringing together more artists and fans.

The key theme for this year's ceremony is 'Connect.' The main slogan has been set as 'K-POP CONNECT: The New Pulse.' Organizers say the slogan reflects a vision of fans around the world transcending time and space to become one through K-pop, with MMA serving as the starting point of a new musical movement for artists and global fandoms.

The MMA was first held in 2005 and is a music awards ceremony and festival that uses Melon's streaming data, fan votes, and expert judging to celebrate K-pop's achievements over the year to audiences worldwide. Organizers stated, 'By expanding MMA2026, we will capture the voices of K-pop fans around the world across an even more diverse range of stages.' They added, 'We will make it a festival where artists and fans share their passion and enjoy themselves together.'

Organizers also announced that the newly launched Korea-China-Japan integrated 'Global-K Chart,' introduced this month, will be linked to select award categories. Detailed award categories and criteria for how the chart will be reflected are to be announced at a later date. The Global-K Chart ranks artists based on streaming volume and fan activity data from major music platforms across three countries: Korea (Melon), China (Tencent Music), and Japan (LINE MUSIC).
