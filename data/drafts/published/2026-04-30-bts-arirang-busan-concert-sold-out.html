BTS has sold out all seats for their Busan concerts. The group will hold 'BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG IN BUSAN' at Busan Asiad Main Stadium on June 12 and 13. The FC membership pre-sale conducted through NOL Ticket on the 29th resulted in a complete sellout for both dates. The tour had already achieved a perfect sellout from its opening three shows in Goyang, and tickets for Japan, North America, and Europe have also sold out, proving BTS's global popularity.

BTS returns to the same stage for the first time in 3 years and 8 months since their October 2022 concert 'Yet to Come' in BUSAN, held in support of Busan's bid for the 2030 World Expo. The venue holds special significance as the site of the group's last full-member concert before their military enlistments. The second day, June 13, carries particular meaning as it falls on the team's debut anniversary.

The excitement extends beyond the concert venue and into the city itself. BTS will hold a large-scale city project, 'BTS THE CITY ARIRANG,' across Busan from June 5 to 21. The project transforms the entire urban space into a massive cultural hub by projecting the team's music and narrative onto the city. It first launched in Seoul alongside the release of their fifth studio album ARIRANG in March, and is now expanding in scale to Las Vegas and Busan, offering a distinctive experience where the whole city can be enjoyed like a theme park.

The tour's momentum is also shifting overseas chart trends. According to the Oricon Weekly Streaming Surge Ranking (dated May 4) released on the 30th, 'Crystal Snow' — a track from BTS's Japanese single Vol. 8, released in 2017 — ranked No. 1. 'MIC Drop,' also released that same year, entered at No. 8. This is attributed to the impact of the Tokyo Dome concerts held on the 17th and 18th.

Notably, 'Crystal Snow' was not part of the official setlist but was performed as an impromptu stage during the concert, yet it generated enormous buzz and led to a surge in streaming. As a result, the song charted anew approximately nine years after its release. The live performance's impact drove a reverse-charting phenomenon, demonstrating the enduring power of a BTS concert.
