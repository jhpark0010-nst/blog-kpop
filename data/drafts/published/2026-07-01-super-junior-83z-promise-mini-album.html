Super Junior's Kim Heechul is set to meet fans through the group's new unit Super Junior-83z. The unit, formed by fellow 1983-born Super Junior members Leeteuk and Heechul, will release their first mini album 'Promise' (너를 위한 약속) on July 13, marking the start of their full-scale activities.

Pre-order sales began on July 1 — Leeteuk's birthday — at various online and offline music stores. The album contains a total of six tracks, including the title track 'Promise' and 'ONSAEMIRO' (온새미로), which was previewed at the encore performance of Super Junior's 20th anniversary world tour in April and drew significant attention.

Alongside the album release, the duo will also hold their first fan-con tour titled '1983.' The tour kicks off in Seoul at Olympic Hall from July 24 to 26, followed by stops across nine regions in Asia, including Tokyo, Bangkok, Hong Kong, Kuala Lumpur, Macau, Kaohsiung, Singapore, and Taipei. The Seoul shows have already sold out, demonstrating strong fan interest.

Super Junior-83z is a unit that brings together Leeteuk and Heechul, who share the same birth year of 1983 but each bring distinct charms. Both members have been active across a wide range of fields including vocals, performance, variety shows, and MC work, and fans are eager to see what kind of synergy the two will create. Through this album, the pair plans to showcase new sides of themselves not previously seen.

Heechul had announced in April that he would be taking a temporary break from JTBC's long-running variety show 'Knowing Bros,' which he had been part of for approximately ten years. At the time, JTBC stated that 'Heechul will be stepping away from Knowing Bros activities for a while due to health reasons.'
