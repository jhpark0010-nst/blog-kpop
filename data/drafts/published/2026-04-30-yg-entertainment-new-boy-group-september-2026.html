YG Entertainment is set to introduce a new 5-member boy group in September 2026. The announcement marks the label's first boy group debut in six years, since TREASURE launched in 2020.

Yang Hyun-suk, YG's chief producer, shared the news on April 30 via the label's official blog. 'My current expectation is to introduce YG's new male group in September,' he wrote. 'The number of members that many of you are curious about is five.'

Yang also hinted at further upcoming acts, stating, 'We will sequentially reveal new intellectual properties and rookies that YG is planning.' Among those previewed is a next-generation girl group tentatively titled 'Next Monster.'

YG has a number of major projects lined up for 2026, including new albums from BABYMONSTER and TREASURE, as well as a world tour by BIGBANG to mark the group's 20th debut anniversary.
