ILLIT has swept the top positions on major album charts in both South Korea and Japan. According to the latest Oricon chart released on May 6 (dated May 5), ILLIT's fourth mini album MAMIHLAPINATAPAI — featuring members Yunah, Minju, Moka, Wonhee, and Iroha — debuted at No. 1 on the Daily Album Ranking upon its local release in Japan. The album had previously sold 276,145 copies on its very first day in South Korea, topping Hanteo Chart's daily album chart dated April 30.

Buoyed by strong performances at home and abroad, the album set a new personal best for first-week sales (the total number of copies sold in the week immediately following release). According to Hanteo Chart on May 7, MAMIHLAPINATAPAI sold 411,654 copies during its first week (tallied April 30 – May 6), surpassing the previous record held by the third mini album 'bomb'.

The title track 'It's Me' is also showing impressive streaming power. The song, which has spread by word of mouth thanks to its upbeat and addictive melody, charted at No. 6 on Spotify Global's Top Song Debut (tallied May 1–3) and also appeared on Apple Music's Today's Top 100 global chart, demonstrating listener interest worldwide. On Melon's Top 100, which reflects domestic music trends in South Korea, the track has been climbing steeply — ranking No. 46 on May 4, No. 36 on May 5, and No. 15 on May 7.

Gaining attention as a so-called 'dopamine song,' 'It's Me' is also seeing increased usage in short-form content. Videos using the track on global platform TikTok (including original sound) surpassed 100,000 within just six days of release. The song also ranked No. 2 on Instagram's Reels Rising Audio chart, which tracks songs with sharply increasing usage, as of 9 p.m. on May 6.

The performance version music video for 'It's Me,' released on May 6 via the HYBE Labels YouTube channel, has also been met with an enthusiastic response. The video presents a full performance version set primarily in a dojo and an exotic restaurant — the same locations featured in the main music video, where members compete for the coveted 'favorite' spot. Powerful moves with a strong impact sync perfectly with the beat, delivering visual catharsis to viewers. The group's energetic synchronized choreography and each member's outstanding ability to interpret any song in a trendy way also stand out.
