TOMORROW X TOGETHER (TXT) member Yeonjun will make his solo comeback on July 10 with his second mini album, 'NO LABELS: PART 02', according to an announcement by Big Hit Music on June 19. The title track is called 'Ice Cream', a summer-themed song befitting its name.

This marks Yeonjun's first new album in eight months, following his debut solo release 'NO LABELS: PART 01' in November of last year. The new project is expected to showcase an even more defined identity as a solo artist.

Singer Kwon Jin-ah will also release a new mini album, 'SAVE ME', on July 15. According to her agency Another, the album includes five tracks: the title song 'MONSTER', along with 'Rain on me' and 'WHO CAN CHANGE', among others.

Having built her career as a ballad singer, Kwon Jin-ah is taking on the rock genre with this album. In a video released on June 16, she explained her reasons for the challenge: 'Many people told me that doing ballads was the right path for me.' She added, 'But there are too many genres and selves inside me, and I felt I couldn't live without letting them out.'

Meanwhile, Galaxy Corporation — an entertainment-tech company and the agency of G-Dragon — announced its participation in VivaTechnology 2026, Europe's largest tech exhibition, held in Paris, France from June 17 to 20 (local time). The company presented a humanoid robot performance at the event.

In the performance, a humanoid robot took the opening stage wearing a costume reinterpreted from designs by the late André Kim, using artificial intelligence technology. The project was planned to commemorate the 60th anniversary of André Kim's Paris debut, as he became the first Korean to hold a fashion show in Paris back in 1966.
