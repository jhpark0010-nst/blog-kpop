ADOR, the HYBE-affiliated label and management company of girl group NewJeans, has had its requests to freeze real estate assets belonging to the mother of terminated member Danielle and former ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin (currently CEO of OK Records) approved by a court. This was confirmed on April 29, though the court decision was made earlier.

According to legal sources, Judge Han Suk-hee of the Seoul Central District Court's Civil Division 58-1 approved ADOR's applications for provisional seizure of real estate against Danielle's mother, identified as Person A, and former CEO Min Hee-jin on February 2.

A provisional seizure (가압류) is a temporary court measure that prevents a debtor from concealing or selling assets. Creditors may apply for it in order to preserve assets in preparation for compulsory execution.

ADOR filed the provisional seizure applications against both individuals on January 23. The total claimed amount is approximately 7 billion KRW. It is reported that Danielle's mother Person A had real estate frozen within the range of 2 billion KRW, while former CEO Min Hee-jin had real estate frozen within the range of 5 billion KRW.

Meanwhile, ADOR's legal counsel submitted a notice of resignation to the court on April 24, approximately three weeks before the first hearing scheduled for May 15. ADOR, which has been in an exclusive contract dispute with NewJeans members, terminated its exclusive contract with Danielle in December of last year and filed a damages lawsuit worth approximately 43 billion KRW.

ADOR has stated that Danielle's side and former CEO Min Hee-jin bear significant responsibility for NewJeans' departure and the delay in their return, and that it intends to hold them legally accountable. The case is being heard by Civil Division 31 of the Seoul Central District Court (Presiding Judge Nam In-su), the same division that previously ruled in favor of Min Hee-jin in the first-instance trial over the put option dispute between Min and HYBE.
