BIGBANG has announced a large-scale world tour schedule, marking the group's full return to the global stage. On June 11, their agency YG Entertainment uploaded an image to its official accounts announcing the start of the 'BIGBANG 2026 WORLD TOUR.'

The tour is set to begin with a domestic stop in August, then continue through North America, Europe, Oceania, and across Asia including Japan, running through February of next year. According to the schedule confirmed so far, BIGBANG will visit a total of 18 cities worldwide for 31 performances. The cities listed include Goyang, Auckland, East Rutherford, Paris, London, Taipei, Singapore, Hanoi, Sydney, Bangkok, Hong Kong, Osaka, Nagoya, Tokyo, Fukuoka, Kuala Lumpur, Jakarta, and Kaohsiung.

One of the most notable aspects of this tour is that the entire schedule is filled with large stadiums and dome-class venues. The lineup includes major outdoor stages such as Goyang Sports Complex, MetLife Stadium, Stade de France, Tottenham Hotspur Stadium, and Kaohsiung National Stadium. It also encompasses major indoor dome venues across Japan and Asia, including Taipei Dome, Tokyo Dome, Kyocera Dome Osaka, and PayPay Dome Fukuoka, demonstrating the group's unrivaled ability to draw massive crowds.

The tour is a fitting large-scale project for BIGBANG's 20th debut anniversary this year, once again proving their unwavering global influence even after a long hiatus. With all members coming together to break a nine-year silence for this full-group tour, music fans around the world are feeling their hearts race.

YG stated, 'We are pouring our capabilities into every aspect — stage production, musical quality, and more — to quench the thirst of music fans around the world who have quietly held their ground and waited all this time,' and added, 'Additional cities will also be announced one by one, so we ask for your continued support.'

Alongside the tour, BIGBANG is also preparing various ways to connect with fans beyond performances to celebrate their 20th debut anniversary. The first of these is 'STILL ALIVE,' a fan-participation event where supporters can share cherished items such as lightsticks and old concert tickets they have kept over the years, along with their personal stories. The event is open for submissions through the fan platform Weverse until the 21st.
