SEVENTEEN celebrated their 11th debut anniversary by making a donation to the Korean National Commission for UNESCO, continuing their tradition of giving back. According to the Korean National Commission for UNESCO on May 26, the group carried out the donation in commemoration of the milestone. The donation amount was not disclosed, and the funds are set to be used for a scholarship program supporting secondary school enrollment in Laos.

SEVENTEEN has been consistently supporting this program since 2022. The Laos secondary education scholarship program provides financial incentives for students who have completed elementary-level accredited education programs at community learning centers, helping them transition into formal secondary public education. Through this initiative, students in underserved educational environments are able to continue their studies on a stable footing.

Hong Hyeon-ik, Secretary-General of the Korean National Commission for UNESCO, said, 'We are deeply grateful for SEVENTEEN's continued support since 2022.' He added, 'Thanks to this donation, marginalized learners in Laos will be able to stably enter public education and envision a better future.'

SEVENTEEN has maintained a steady presence not only in their music activities but also in social contribution efforts. Notably, in June 2024, they were appointed as UNESCO Youth Ambassadors, becoming the first K-pop artists to receive the designation, demonstrating their global influence.
