BTS's offline fan event 'BTS The City Arirang - Busan' (referred to as 'The City Busan') has wrapped up successfully, showcasing a variety of programs against the backdrop of Busan's major landmarks, Big Hit Music announced on June 19. The event drew global ARMY (the BTS fandom name) who had gathered in the city for the concerts.

'The City Busan' was held to commemorate the BTS world tour 'Arirang' Busan concerts, which took place on June 12 and 13. The fan event continued across various locations throughout the city for several days after the concerts.

Busan Station featured a media art wall and a multi-purpose event space, while TheBay101 hosted 'ARMY Madang' (ARMY Yard) and other installations. Global ARMY who had traveled to Busan for the concerts flocked to these venues throughout the event period.

According to the City of Busan, more than 200,000 visitors came to key event spaces including the 'Love Song Lounge' in Haeundae and the 'Drone Lighting Show' at Gwangalli Beach.

Major landmark bridges and facilities representing Busan — including Gwangandaegyo Bridge, Busan Harbor Bridge, and Nurimaru — were illuminated in red, the key color of BTS's fifth full-length album 'Arirang' (ARIRANG).
