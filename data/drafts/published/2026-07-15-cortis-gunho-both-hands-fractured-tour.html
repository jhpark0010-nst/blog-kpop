CORTIS member Gunho has expressed a strong determination to take the stage at the group's first solo tour despite suffering fractures in the fingers of both hands. Label Big Hit Music announced the injury news on July 15 through the fan platform.

Big Hit Music stated that 'Gunho recently fell and landed on both hands incorrectly, leading him to visit a hospital for medical evaluation,' and that 'examination results confirmed fractures around the little fingers of both hands, and he promptly received the necessary treatment and care and is currently recovering stably.'

Following medical advice to protect the injured areas by wearing casts and braces for the time being and to minimize movement of both arms, Gunho will perform at the '2026 CORTIS TOUR PUT YOUR PHONE DOWN IN INCHEON' concerts on July 18 and 19 focusing on singing rather than choreography.

Gunho also left a personal message for the fandom 'CORE' to reassure fans who may have been startled by the sudden news. He said, 'I ended up getting injured on both hands this time. First of all, I'm sorry for worrying you all so much — you must have been very surprised by my injury.'

He continued, 'The nearest tour is CORTIS's very first tour as a group, so I really wanted to show up as all five of us, and I had a strong desire to participate no matter what. I won't be able to join the choreography with my friends, but I will try my best to show everyone a great stage together with them within what I am able to do.'

CORTIS has been riding their biggest long-term chart run since debut, with their second mini album 'GREENGREEN,' released in May, charting on the Billboard 200 at No. 82 and remaining on the chart for nine consecutive weeks. The title track 'REDRED' has also been performing strongly on global charts. Despite the setback of an unexpected double-hand fracture, fans at home and abroad are pouring out enthusiastic support and encouragement for Gunho's fierce commitment to completing the group's first tour.
