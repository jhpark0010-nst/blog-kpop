TWICE's Japanese member Sana (age 30) is making a bold transition from idol to actress. On July 8, a representative from her agency JYP Entertainment officially announced, 'Sana has been confirmed to star as the lead in the new film Nyangi (working title).'

With this confirmation, Sana embarks on her first full-fledged acting challenge 11 years after her debut, set to make a powerful first impression on audiences through the big screen. The film Nyangi is a highly anticipated Korean-Japanese co-production project.

Notably, top Japanese actor Sato Takeru has been cast as Sana's co-star in her screen debut, drawing intense attention given that both are of Japanese origin.

Directed by Kwon Hyeok-chan, the film has already captured the film industry's attention, with many eager to see what kind of on-screen chemistry and acting synergy the two will deliver.

Meanwhile, TWICE — the group Sana belongs to — has been riding high as musicians as well. The group launched their world tour 'THIS IS FOR' at Inspire Arena in Incheon in July of last year and has since performed successfully across the globe.

TWICE is set to hold a Seoul encore concert at KSPO DOME (Olympic Gymnastics Arena) in Bangi-dong, Songpa-gu, Seoul over three days from July 10 to 12, marking the grand finale of this extensive tour.
