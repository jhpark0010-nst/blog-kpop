The 43.1 billion KRW (approximately $31 million USD) damages lawsuit filed by ADOR — the label of NewJeans — against member Danielle, her family, and former CEO Min Hee-jin has entered full trial proceedings. The Seoul Central District Court's Civil Division 31 (presiding judge Nam In-su) held the first pleading session on May 14.

Ahead of the hearing, ADOR's legal representation changed hands. All five attorneys from Kim & Chang law firm who had previously handled the case submitted their resignations, after which ADOR appointed law firm Rihan as its new counsel and filed a request to reschedule the hearing date. The court, however, did not accept the request and proceeded with the trial as originally scheduled.

At a preliminary hearing held in March, both sides clashed over whether the lawsuit was being deliberately delayed. Danielle's side argued that 'given the nature of idol activities, a prolonged lawsuit would cause serious harm during the most critical period,' and claimed that ADOR was intentionally stalling by expanding the scope of defendants and repeatedly requesting date extensions.

ADOR's side denied any intent to delay, stating that 'this case is a damages and penalty claim lawsuit and does not directly restrict Danielle's entertainment activities.' ADOR also maintained that the complex structure of the case requires sufficient time for review.

According to legal representatives, neither former CEO Min Hee-jin nor Danielle is expected to appear in person at the May 14 hearing. In December of last year, ADOR notified Danielle of the termination of her exclusive contract, coinciding with the news that members Hyein, Haerin, and Hanni had returned to the agency. ADOR determined that Danielle, her family, and former CEO Min bore significant responsibility for NewJeans' departure and the delay in their return, and filed the 43.1 billion KRW lawsuit seeking damages and penalty payments.

Among current NewJeans members, Haerin, Hyein, and Hanni have returned to ADOR, while Minji is reportedly in ongoing positive discussions regarding whether she will rejoin the group.
