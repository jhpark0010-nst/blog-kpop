SEVENTEEN member Joshua attended the closing ceremony of the global youth support program 'Going Together - For Youth Creativity & Well-Being' at the main hall of UNESCO headquarters in Paris, France on June 25 (local time), representing the group in its role as UNESCO Youth Goodwill Ambassadors. The event marked the conclusion of the program that SEVENTEEN and UNESCO had jointly pursued, and also served as an occasion to announce plans for further support of outstanding participating groups.

Taking the stage in a black suit, Joshua delivered a speech in English for approximately six minutes. 'It is a great honor to be here. The other members wanted to be here too,' he said. 'When we were appointed as Youth Goodwill Ambassadors, SEVENTEEN and UNESCO shared the belief that we must help empower young people to lead change.'

Joshua described 'Going Together' as 'a program that provides resources, mentoring, and various opportunities so that young people can turn their ideas into meaningful change.' He added, 'The passion and creativity of young people moved us deeply. We felt we could not stop here. We wanted to expand these precious projects so they could have an even greater impact. So we expanded our support.'

SEVENTEEN donated one million US dollars (approximately 1.51 billion Korean won) when they were appointed as UNESCO Youth Goodwill Ambassadors in 2024, establishing a World Youth Fund. The fund was used to support projects in areas including building youth confidence and resilience through music, arts, and sports, developing creative youth communities, and improving youth mental health. The group also made an additional donation from charity auction proceeds on World Youth Day last year.

Joshua shared a key insight from the experience: 'All of these experiences have shown that young people already hold the answers to change the world. They just need someone to believe in them.' He emphasized the importance of 'trust' and 'togetherness,' saying, 'The 13 members of SEVENTEEN, who have shared one dream for over ten years, have also faced uncertainty — but we were able to go further through trust in one another and the belief that we are stronger together. That is why we have promised to keep moving forward as one team.'

Joshua also sent a message of encouragement to young people around the world: 'I could see this beautiful synergy in you as well. You are each walking your own path across different regions and cultures. Even if the challenges you face differ, we are on the same road in that we are all working to create a better world. So let's keep going together.' As Joshua concluded his speech, the audience responded with warm cheers and applause. SEVENTEEN plans to continue their activities and support as UNESCO Youth Goodwill Ambassadors going forward.
