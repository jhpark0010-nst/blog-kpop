ILLIT is making waves ahead of the April 30 release of their 4th mini album, MAMIHLAPINATAPAI. The group's momentum is anything but ordinary as anticipation builds around the upcoming project.

The title track 'It's Me' marks ILLIT's first attempt at the techno genre, with an exciting beat and addictive melody designed to trigger a dopamine rush in listeners. Even with only a portion of the sound revealed so far, it already promises an auditory thrill distinctly different from their previous work, clearly signaling the team's new musical direction.

The concept has also defied fans' expectations. ILLIT's signature charm remains intact, but the way it is expressed has become far bolder and more assertive. In the campaign film released earlier, the five members do not simply agonize over an ambiguous relationship with someone they like — they let their emotions explode freely. In scenes where they actively pursue affection, they dominate the atmosphere with a cool charisma and confident aura.

This kind of transformation is not new for ILLIT. With their 1st single album 'NOT CUTE ANYMORE,' they declared that their identity is not limited to simply being 'cute.' It was a statement of intent to reject others' gazes and society's stereotypes, and to focus on their true selves. This delivered a thrilling twist to those who had only seen them as cute, and became a turning point that convinced fans of ILLIT's diverse appeal.

MAMIHLAPINATAPAI, which signals yet another bold departure, is the process of turning ILLIT's potential into certainty. It is not merely a concept change, but an inflection point that infinitely expands the boundaries of 'ILLIT core' — the term used to describe the group's unique sensibility and aesthetic. This trajectory aligns perfectly with the meaning of their group name: a group of great potential that can become anything, and whose next move is always worth anticipating.

There is also growing interest in what trend ILLIT will lead next. The group has consistently attracted attention from global fans in their teens and twenties through original music, performances, and style with each album. The title track 'NOT CUTE ANYMORE' swept the top of short-form charts worldwide and reached No. 7 on the US Billboard 'Bubbling Under Hot 100' chart, earning recognition in the mainstream pop market. Expectations are high for their next leap forward — a version of ILLIT that is both the most like themselves and the most unfamiliar they have ever been.
