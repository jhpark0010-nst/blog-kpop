aespa has revealed an expanded universe with their new world tour 'SYNK : COMPLaeXITY' and their second full-length album 'LEMONADE'. On April 27, the group released a motion logo video for the new world tour on their YouTube channel and announced the title 'SYNK : COMPLaeXITY'.

The tour will kick off at Seoul's Gocheok Dome on August 7–8, and is built around the new narrative chapter that unfolds from the second album 'LEMONADE'. Centered on the keyword 'CRACK,' the tour will feature performances, stage design, and visual production that are distinct from the previous 'aeXIS LINE' era.

A trailer released at midnight on April 27 shows a strange crack (Complaexity) beginning to appear in the real-world aespa, with the members finally coming face to face with an opened P.O.S. The video also raises curiosity about the newly unfolding universe chapter, while scenes from previous music videos — 'Supernova,' 'Whiplash,' 'Drama,' and 'Girls' — appear as easter eggs, adding to the viewing experience.

aespa also held '2026 aespa LIVE TOUR - SYNK : aeXIS LINE - in JAPAN [SPECIAL EDITION DOME TOUR]' at Tokyo Dome on April 25–26. Combined with the earlier Kyocera Dome shows, which drew 76,000 fans over two days, the Tokyo Dome concerts brought in 94,000 attendees, bringing the total dome tour audience to 170,000 and once again demonstrating the group's formidable ticket power.

With this, aespa has successfully completed 25 shows through the 'aeXIS LINE' tour — which began at Seoul Olympic Park KSPO DOME in August of last year — including arena tours of around 10,000 seats in major Japanese cities such as Fukuoka, Tokyo, Aichi, and Osaka, as well as stops across Asia including Bangkok, Hong Kong, Macau, and Jakarta, followed by the Kyocera Dome and Tokyo Dome concerts, further proving their global reach.

Meanwhile, aespa's second full-length album 'LEMONADE' will be released digitally across major music platforms at 1:00 PM on May 29, with a physical album release simultaneously.
