BTS's fifth full-length album ARIRANG has remained in the Top 10 of the Billboard 200 for ten consecutive weeks. According to Billboard's June 2 (local time) update, ARIRANG climbed one spot from the previous week to land at No. 9 on the main album chart. After debuting at No. 1 for three straight weeks — a first for a K-pop act on this chart — the album posted positions of No. 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, and 10 over the following six weeks.

The album's title track SWIM rose eleven spots from the previous week to reach No. 44 on the Hot 100, the main singles chart. On the Billboard Global Excl. U.S. chart, SWIM claimed No. 1 for four consecutive weeks. Since its release, the track has topped that chart during weeks 1–4 and weeks 7–10, marking its eighth total No. 1 on the chart.

Continuing the momentum, BTS is set to perform at the 2026 iHeartRadio Music Festival, to be held at T-Mobile Arena in Las Vegas on September 18 (local time). The group joins a lineup that includes global pop artists such as Cardi B and Snoop Dogg.

On the Billboard 200, LE SSERAFIM's second full-length album PUREFLOW made its chart debut at No. 10. With this entry, five consecutive LE SSERAFIM releases have now charted in the Top 10: their first full-length album UNFORGIVEN (No. 6), mini album EASY (No. 8), CRAZY (No. 7), HOT (No. 9), and now PUREFLOW.

Also on the Billboard 200, the original soundtrack album for the Netflix animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters' charted at No. 19, and CORTIS's GREENGREEN came in at No. 42. KATSEYE's 'Beautiful Chaos' entered at No. 110 and 'SIS (Soft Is Strong)' at No. 141.

On the Hot 100, Tame Impala's 'Dracula' — a remix version featuring Jennie — reached No. 14. KATSEYE's 'PINKY UP' charted at No. 92.
