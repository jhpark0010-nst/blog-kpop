SEVENTEEN has ranked 7th on Billboard's mid-year Top Tours chart after generating approximately $88 million (133.3 billion KRW) in revenue from their world tour over the past six months. According to Pledis Entertainment on June 1, the group drew around 574,000 fans across 25 shows, as reported in Billboard's Box Score annual mid-year report published on May 27 (local time).

The group launched their world tour 'NEW_' in September of last year and concluded the tour with encore performances in Incheon in April of this year.

Among K-pop acts, the next highest-ranking group after SEVENTEEN was TWICE, who came in at 12th place. TWICE drew approximately 434,000 fans across 28 shows, earning around $58.3 million (88.2 billion KRW) in revenue.

TOMORROW X TOGETHER placed 15th on the chart, drawing approximately 412,000 fans across 20 shows and recording around $53 million (80.2 billion KRW) in revenue.

Topping the overall chart was Lady Gaga with approximately $236.2 million (358 billion KRW), followed by Bad Bunny in second place with approximately $230.3 million (348.4 billion KRW).
