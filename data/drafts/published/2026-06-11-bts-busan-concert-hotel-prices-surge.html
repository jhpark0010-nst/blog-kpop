Ahead of BTS's Busan concerts, accommodation prices have surged more than tenfold, sparking widespread controversy. As the number of foreign tourists traveling to South Korea to attend the shows continues to rise, tourism complaint reports filed in Busan have also spiked sharply.

BTS is set to perform at Busan Asiad Main Stadium on June 12 and 13 as part of their world tour 'Arirang IN Busan.' Almost immediately after the concerts were announced, allegations emerged that some Busan accommodations had canceled existing reservations, raised room rates, and relisted the rooms at inflated prices.

Among the reported incidents, a Japanese fan identified as 'A' posted online claiming she had been subjected to verbal abuse by a lodging operator before having her reservation unilaterally canceled. A had booked the accommodation through a reservation platform and left an inquiry asking whether reservations could be automatically canceled. In response, she received a message containing crude insults before her booking was canceled without notice.

A later made the message public, saying, 'Even with a translation tool, the content was hard to understand and hard to believe,' as she described the harm she experienced.

According to Korea Tourism Organization's 'Korea Tourism Data Lab' on June 10, of the 368 tourism complaint reports filed nationwide in May, 185 were from Busan. Accommodation-related complaints alone — including unilateral reservation cancellations and excessive penalty charges — numbered 133.

BTS members addressed the accommodation controversy during a Weverse Live broadcast last month, noting that 'a lot of news has been coming out about the Busan lodging situation.' Leader RM said, 'Even if we want to fix it, there is nothing we can do. Of course prices can fluctuate between peak and off-peak seasons, but let's all keep it reasonable.' Despite the members' concerns, price gouging targeting foreign tourists has continued. With the concerts now just one day away, online users have reacted with comments such as 'This is an embarrassment to the country,' 'Those accommodations should be shut down,' 'There needs to be a crackdown,' and 'Have some shame.'
