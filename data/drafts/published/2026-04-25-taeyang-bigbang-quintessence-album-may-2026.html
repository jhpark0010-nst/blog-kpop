BIGBANG's Taeyang will release a new album on May 18. His agency, The Black Label, revealed the cover image of Taeyang's fourth full-length album, 'QUINTESSENCE,' on its official social media on the 20th, simultaneously announcing the May 18 release date.

This marks a comeback approximately three years after his second mini-album 'Down to Earth,' released in April 2023, and his first full-length album in about nine years since 'WHITE NIGHT,' released in August 2017. Through the new album, Taeyang is set to demonstrate his strength as an all-around solo artist and share a new musical world uniquely his own.

The revealed cover draws attention with an image of two angels shaped to represent the letter 'Q,' the first letter of the album title 'QUINTESSENCE.' The word 'QUINTESSENCE,' defined as 'essence' or 'the purest form,' had previously attracted the attention of K-pop fans through a lengthy teaser message centered on the word, heralding Taeyang's return.

Taeyang conducted a fan meeting tour across a total of seven cities, including Seoul, from July to December of last year. On the 12th and 19th (local time), he participated as a headliner at Coachella, the largest music festival in the United States, performing both as part of BIGBANG and as a solo artist, meeting fans from around the world.
