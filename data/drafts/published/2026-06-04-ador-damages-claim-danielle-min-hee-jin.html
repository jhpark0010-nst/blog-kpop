ADOR, the label behind NewJeans, has adjusted the damages claim it filed against Danielle (whose exclusive contract was terminated) and her family, as well as former ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin (currently CEO of OK Records). According to a MyDaily exclusive report on June 4, the claimed amount has been revised from the original 43.1 billion KRW to 33.09 billion KRW.

Regarding the change, ADOR told MyDaily: 'A new legal representative has been appointed and reviewed the case, restructuring the claims.' The label added, 'As a result, the claimed amount has been partially adjusted and revised. We plan to continue reinforcing our arguments and evidence as the lawsuit progresses.'

Last month, ADOR dismissed five attorneys from its previous legal representative Kim & Chang and appointed four attorneys from law firm Rihan. The next hearing date is scheduled for the afternoon of June 11.

ADOR originally filed the 43.1 billion KRW damages lawsuit in December of last year alongside the termination of Danielle's exclusive contract. The label's position is that Danielle's side and Min Hee-jin bear significant responsibility for NewJeans' departure and the delay in their return, and that legal accountability must be pursued.

Additionally, the Seoul Central District Court approved ADOR's application for a real estate provisional seizure against Danielle's mother and Min Hee-jin on February 2. The total claimed amount for the seizure was 7 billion KRW — reportedly up to 2 billion KRW against Danielle's mother and up to 5 billion KRW against Min Hee-jin.
