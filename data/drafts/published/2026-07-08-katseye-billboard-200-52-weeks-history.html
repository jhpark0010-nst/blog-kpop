KATSEYE, the global girl group formed through a joint venture between HYBE and Geffen Records, has set a historic milestone in the United States, the world's largest pop market. Their second EP, 'BEAUTIFUL CHAOS', released in June 2025, achieved 52 consecutive weeks on the Billboard 200 (as of the July 4 chart date) — the first girl group album to chart for a full year in 21 years. The group continued that run on the following chart (dated July 11), landing at No. 128 for a total of 53 weeks on the chart.

The last girl group album to chart on the Billboard 200 for a full year was The Pussycat Dolls' debut album 'PCD', released in 2005. The Pussycat Dolls were a defining girl group of the mid-2000s U.S. pop market, producing global hits such as 'Don't Cha' and 'Buttons' through 'PCD'. KATSEYE's matching of that record demonstrates once again the long-term commercial potential of girl groups in the American market, after a lengthy absence of such sustained success.

Staying on the Billboard 200 — a chart that reflects album-level longevity — for over a year amid rapidly shifting global music trends signals that KATSEYE's music, fanbase, and mainstream influence have firmly taken root. Their strength was equally evident on the Hot 100 singles chart. Starting with 'Gabriela' peaking at No. 21 (dated January 17), KATSEYE also charted 'PINKY UP' at No. 28 (April 25), 'Internet Girl' at No. 29 (January 17), and 'Gnarly' at No. 82 (January 10).

In June, KATSEYE joined fellow HYBE Labels girl groups LE SSERAFIM and ILLIT for a surprise collaboration single, 'ICONIC BY MISTAKE'. The track debuted at No. 38 on the Billboard Hot 100 (dated June 27), making it the highest-charting K-pop girl group song on that chart so far this year. With this, KATSEYE has now placed a total of five songs on the Hot 100, establishing themselves as a group with consistent results on both the album and singles charts.

KATSEYE's achievements extend to major awards shows and festivals. They were nominated for Best New Artist and Best Pop Duo/Group Performance at the 68th Grammy Awards held earlier this year, and swept three awards at the American Music Awards in May, including New Artist of the Year. The group also performed at major global festivals including Lollapalooza Chicago and South America, Coachella Valley Music and Arts Festival, and Governors Ball.

Their upcoming first North America and Europe tour, THE WILDWORLD TOUR, set to kick off in September, sold out all dates within 48 hours of tickets going on sale. The tour spans 31 shows across 27 cities in 10 countries. KATSEYE's remarkable trajectory represents the success of HYBE Chairman Bang Si-hyuk's 'Multi-home, multi-genre' project. Formed through the global audition program 'The Debut: Dream Academy' and officially debuting in 2024, the group has combined an advanced training infrastructure with a locally tailored strategy to firmly establish themselves in the mainstream U.S. pop market.
