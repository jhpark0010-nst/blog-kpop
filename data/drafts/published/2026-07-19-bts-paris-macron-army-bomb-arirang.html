French President Emmanuel Macron and First Lady Brigitte Macron attended BTS's Paris concert at the Stade de France in Saint-Denis on July 17-18 (local time), drawing attention from fans around the world. The two concerts, part of BTS's ARIRANG world tour, sold out both nights and drew a combined audience of more than 160,000.

On July 18, President Macron posted a video clip from the BTS concert on his social media with a short message in French: 'Bienvenue à Paris' ('Welcome to Paris'). The post spread rapidly among fans worldwide after it went up shortly following the show.

The Paris dates served as the final stop of BTS's European tour leg. The first night alone drew approximately 92,000 attendees, reported to be the largest single-concert audience in the group's career.

President Macron and the First Lady were spotted watching the show from the audience. The couple appeared on the large screen that panned across the crowd during the concert, drawing loud cheers from attendees. They were also seen holding BTS's official light stick, the ARMY BOMB, as they enjoyed the performance. Reactions on social media included comments such as 'The president has become an ARMY' and 'BTS's influence has been proven once again.'

The Paris concert featured BTS's signature songs including 'FAKE LOVE', 'IDOL', 'Butter', and 'Dynamite', along with special stages prepared for French fans, running for approximately 150 minutes in total. Stage production inspired by traditional Korean culture also received an enthusiastic response from the local audience.

With the Paris concerts concluded, BTS has wrapped up their European tour. On July 19 (local time), the group is set to perform at the halftime show of the 2026 FIFA World Cup final at MetLife Stadium in New Jersey, USA.
