A set of commemorative stamps featuring BLACKPINK is set to be released, drawing attention from Hallyu fans around the world. The Ministry of Science and ICT's Korea Post announced on May 7 that it will issue 10 types of BLACKPINK commemorative stamps on June 16, along with a commemorative stamp packet featuring portraits of the BLACKPINK members.

The stamps will be available for purchase at general post offices nationwide as well as through the internet post office. Pre-sales will be offered to registered internet post office members starting at 7 p.m. on May 12.

SK Telecom also announced an expansion of the 'Subscription Calendar' feature within its AI service app, A., into a wider range of categories including entertainment, beauty, retail, and fashion. The feature works by automatically adding relevant schedules to a user's personal calendar and sending notifications at the appropriate time when they subscribe to a brand or topic of interest. SK Telecom is partnering with Hana Securities and CU to expand the service into finance and retail, and is also pursuing partnerships with LG Electronics' official online store and Olive Young.

KT announced the launch of the 'Y Artist Label AI Video Contest,' designed to discover emerging creators in the AI video field and provide them with an opportunity to grow alongside KT's youth brand 'Y.' The contest runs in two categories: a short-form video introducing the 'Y-dum Data 2x Benefit' — a perk for customers aged 34 and under — in one's own style, and a brand film freely expressing the Y brand slogan 'Shine as You Are, Y.'
