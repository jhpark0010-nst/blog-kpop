Taeyang has unveiled the trailer for his fourth full-length album 'QUINTESSENCE', set to release on May 18. The album marks his first full album comeback in nine years, drawing enormous anticipation from fans around the world.

The Black Label released the 'QUINTESSENCE' trailer through official channels on May 4. The video features a mysterious landscape where small collisions set the world in motion, and a gradually emerging civilization, with a three-dimensional panoramic view of a vast city capturing viewers' attention.

In the trailer, crowds gather and disperse in the shape of 'Q' — the abbreviation for 'QUINTESSENCE' — and two angels forming the letter 'Q' appear to rise into the sky as if embracing the clouds. The imagery deepens curiosity about the essence of what Taeyang will present with 'QUINTESSENCE'.

Taeyang has been rolling out teaser content ahead of his May 18 full album comeback, drawing significant attention from global K-pop fans. This marks a comeback approximately three years after his second EP 'Down to Earth', released in April 2023, and a full album release approximately nine years after 'WHITE NIGHT', released in August 2017.

Through 'QUINTESSENCE', Taeyang is set to once again prove his strength as an all-around solo artist and showcase his own new musical world. He also successfully completed a fan meeting tour across seven cities including Seoul from July to December last year, and on the 12th and 19th (local time), he participated as a headliner at the Coachella Festival in the United States alongside BIGBANG members, actively engaging with global fans both as a group and as a solo artist.

Taeyang's fourth full-length album 'QUINTESSENCE' is scheduled for release at 6 p.m. on May 18.
