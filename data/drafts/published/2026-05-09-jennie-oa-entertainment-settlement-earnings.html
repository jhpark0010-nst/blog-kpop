BLACKPINK's Jennie received more than 20 billion KRW in settlements over the past two years through her one-person agency, OA Entertainment. According to an audit report disclosed on May 9 via the Financial Supervisory Service's electronic disclosure system, OA Entertainment paid Jennie approximately 9.47 billion KRW in settlements last year alone.

Adding the approximately 14.3 billion KRW paid out in 2024, the total amount disbursed over the two-year period comes to roughly 23.8 billion KRW. However, the disclosure only lists the expense under cost of sales, meaning the breakdown by specific revenue category — such as appearance fees, advertising, or music royalties — could not be separately confirmed.

OA Entertainment is a personal label that Jennie founded in November 2023, following the end of her exclusive contract with YG Entertainment. Jennie holds 100% of the company's shares, and her mother serves as the representative director. Currently, BLACKPINK group activities are managed through YG Entertainment, while Jennie's solo activities are centered on OA Entertainment.

The company's financial performance has also shown growth. In its first full year of operation in 2024, OA Entertainment recorded revenue of 18.9 billion KRW and an operating profit of 580 million KRW. Last year, revenue increased to 23.8 billion KRW. Operating profit, however, declined to approximately 390 million KRW compared to the previous year, which is attributed to increased selling and administrative expenses including salaries and service fees.
