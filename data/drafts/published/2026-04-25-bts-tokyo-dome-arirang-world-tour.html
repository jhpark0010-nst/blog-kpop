BTS wrapped up their 'BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG IN TOKYO' shows at Tokyo Dome on April 17 and 18, 2026, drawing a combined 110,000 fans across two completely sold-out nights. The concerts marked BTS's return to Tokyo Dome for the first time in 7 years and 5 months, since their 'BTS WORLD TOUR LOVE YOURSELF ~JAPAN EDITION~' in November 2018.

The crowd waved their light sticks in perfect unison, creating a spectacular sight throughout the venue. Thunderous cheers filled Tokyo Dome during 'Not Today' and 'MIC Drop.' The emotional peak of the night came during 'Body to Body,' a track from the new album ARIRANG. When the melody of the traditional Korean folk song 'Arirang' wove into the performance, the entire audience sang along in unison. HYBE Chairman Bang Si-hyuk had previously predicted that a mass sing-along of 'Arirang' at overseas venues would be the most iconic moment of this tour. The members could not hide their deeply moved expressions as they took in the voices of the crowd.

The audience sang every word of global hits 'Butter' and 'Dynamite,' their voices layering over BTS's own, sending the energy of the concert even higher. During a 'random song' segment — where the members perform older tracks on the spot — 'Crystal Snow,' from their 8th Japanese single released in 2017, and 'FOR YOU,' from their 4th Japanese single released in 2015, made surprise appearances. As Japanese-language originals made for local fans, the songs left a deep, lingering impression throughout the venue. The members moved across every part of the stage making eye contact with fans, repeatedly saying, 'It has really been a long time since we sang this. It feels so good.'

BTS delivered most of their ments (stage talk) fluently in Japanese and read out letters they had carefully written in advance to convey their sincerity. 'Even though it has been a while, you responded with the same cheers and smiles, and that actually gave us strength. We missed you,' they said. They also shared: 'I sometimes travel to Tokyo, and as I walk the streets I think about how you all live seeing these kinds of scenes every day. I am truly so happy and honored to be able to come back. And I am happy. Thank you for waiting all this time, and I sincerely love you.'

Having successfully completed their Tokyo dates, BTS will now move the tour to the United States. They are scheduled to meet American fans at Raymond James Stadium in Tampa, Florida on April 25, 26, and 28.
