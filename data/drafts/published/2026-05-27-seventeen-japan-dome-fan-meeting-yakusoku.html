SEVENTEEN drew a total of 180,000 fans across four shows at their Japan dome fan meeting, according to Pledis Entertainment on May 25. The group held their 2026 Japan fan meeting titled 'YAKUSOKU' (やくそく), completing the run at two major dome venues in Japan.

On May 23 and 24, SEVENTEEN held 'YAKUSOKU' at Kyocera Dome Osaka, meeting 80,000 fans over the two days. The fan meeting title 'YAKUSOKU' means 'promise' in Korean, carrying the meaning of a promise that SEVENTEEN and CARAT (the group's fandom name) will always be together.

Prior to the Osaka dates, the Tokyo Dome fan meeting held on May 13 and 14 drew 100,000 attendees over two days.

At the Japan fan meetings, SEVENTEEN performed 18 songs over more than three hours, including Japanese original tracks such as 'CALL CALL CALL!' as well as Japanese-language versions of Korean songs like 'Aju Nice' (어쩌나).

SEVENTEEN said, 'We felt that we could accomplish anything as long as we are with CARAT,' and added, 'Even when we cannot meet you, we are always growing.'

They continued, 'Each member is preparing many activities to meet you in various ways,' and said, 'All 13 of us will come back looking better than anyone.'
