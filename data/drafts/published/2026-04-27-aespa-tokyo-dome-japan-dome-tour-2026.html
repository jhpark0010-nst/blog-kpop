aespa packed Tokyo Dome on April 25 and 26, 2026, drawing 47,000 fans per show and a combined 94,000 over the two nights. The chorus of their signature hit 'Supernova' rang out across the iconic venue as fans sang every Korean lyric back to the group. Colorful EDM-style laser lights swept the arena while a live band added a sharp, heavy-metal edge to aespa's signature sound.

Back-to-back performances of 'Rich Man,' 'Next Level,' 'Whiplash,' 'Girls,' and 'Drama' kept the crowd in a continuous sing-along. aespa performed with handheld microphones throughout, matching the audience's energy without any sign of fatigue. The stage was shaped like a large '工' character, and a central control system synchronized fans' light sticks into a single sweeping wave of color, creating the effect of the group appearing in the middle of outer space.

The Tokyo Dome shows were part of aespa's Japan dome tour titled 'SYNK : aeXIS LINE - in JAPAN [SPECIAL EDITION DOME TOUR].' Combined with earlier dates at Kyocera Dome, where 76,000 fans attended, the tour drew a total of 170,000 people across Japan. Tokyo Dome, which holds up to 50,000 per show, is considered the benchmark venue for top-tier artists in the world's second-largest music market.

Member Winter said, 'Since this is the last day of the dome tour, I feel a little sad, so I want to pour all of that feeling into today's stage — please enjoy it together with us.' Karina said, 'From Kyocera Dome to Tokyo Dome, I took the stage with the mindset of showing the most aespa-like version of ourselves. Today we will properly show you what we do best.' Giselle said, 'Because today is the last day, I want to give a little more on stage — MY (the fandom name), please cheer us on so we can run all the way to the end.' NingNing added, 'The air inside Tokyo Dome feels even hotter than usual today — everyone, let's have a great time, fighting!'

Beyond their hit catalog, aespa performed Japanese-language songs including the anime OST tracks 'Attitude' and 'ZOOM ZOOM' for local fans. The concert also featured solo stages highlighting each member's individual personality, plus unit stages: Karina and Winter performed 'Serenade' together, while Giselle and NingNing performed 'Lollipop.' NingNing revealed that 'Lollipop' was a song she and Giselle created together in a hotel room during the 2024 tour, saying, 'I always wanted to perform it someday — I'm so happy we finally got to do it at a dome.' The show closed with encores 'Sun and Moon,' 'Live My Life,' and 'To The Girls.'

The area around Tokyo Dome filled with fans from early afternoon. Notably, a significant number of young male fans attended — an unusual sight at a typical K-pop concert. Japanese fan Suzuki Yuto, 25, said, 'aespa's music has a stronger impact compared to J-pop, which makes it really cool — that's its biggest appeal,' adding that 'Supernova' is his favorite aespa song and Winter is his favorite member. aespa is set to make their comeback on May 29 with their second full-length album 'LEMONADE.' Winter teased ahead of the final song: 'There will be a fan meeting, the second full album, and possibly some new surprises, so please look forward to it.'
