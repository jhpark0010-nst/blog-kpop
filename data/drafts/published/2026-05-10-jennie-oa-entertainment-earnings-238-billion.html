BLACKPINK's Jennie has received approximately 23.8 billion KRW (around $17 million USD) in settlement payments over the past two years through OA Entertainment (ODD ATELIER), the one-person agency she founded herself.

According to the Financial Supervisory Service's electronic disclosure system, OA Entertainment paid Jennie 9.479 billion KRW last year. Combined with the approximately 14.3 billion KRW paid out in 2024, the total settlement amount Jennie received over the two-year period comes to 23.8 billion KRW.

In the audit report, the amounts are classified under 'cost of sales,' and are estimated to reflect revenue generated from personal activities including advertising, performances, music releases, and appearances. The breakdown by individual category has not been disclosed.

Jennie founded OA Entertainment after her personal exclusive contract with YG Entertainment ended in November 2023. She continues her BLACKPINK group activities through YG Entertainment, while solo albums, advertisements, performances, and other individual activities are carried out independently. Jennie holds 100% of the shares in OA Entertainment, and her mother serves as CEO.

The company's financial performance is also notable. In its first year of operation in 2024, OA Entertainment recorded revenue of 18.9 billion KRW and an operating profit of 580 million KRW. Last year, revenue reached 23.8 billion KRW, though operating profit dipped slightly to 390 million KRW due to increased selling and administrative expenses.

Jennie has continued an active schedule through solo activities, global advertising campaigns, and performances. Since establishing her own agency, she has demonstrated strong influence and profitability, cementing her presence as a standout independent artist.
