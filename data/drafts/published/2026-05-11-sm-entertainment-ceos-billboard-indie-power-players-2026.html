SM Entertainment co-CEOs Jang Cheol-hyuk and Tak Young-jun have been named to Billboard's '2026 Indie Power Players' list. According to SM, Billboard announced the annual list on May 8 (local time). Tak Young-jun has been selected for five consecutive years since 2022, while Jang Cheol-hyuk has been selected for three consecutive years since 2024.

Billboard's Indie Power Players is a list the outlet has published since 2017, recognizing leaders of labels and distribution companies that have achieved results in the global music market outside of the three major U.S. labels — Universal Music Group, Sony Music Entertainment, and Warner Music Group.

Co-CEO Jang Cheol-hyuk commented, 'At the start of this year, we announced the SM Next 3.0 strategy and are pursuing various initiatives and changes to further elevate the creative capabilities within our organization.' He added, 'If SM continues to create and develop new trends going forward, we will be able to connect with a broader global fanbase.'

Co-CEO Tak Young-jun said, 'It has become increasingly important to consider what kind of experience artist content delivers to fans.' He continued, 'We are putting in great effort to expand beyond music to the spaces where fans consume and spend time with content, in order to deliver a consistent story and experience.'

Also included on this year's list were Shin Sun-jung, CEO of BigHit Music; So Sung-jin, Master Professional at Source Music — the label behind girl groups GFriend and LE SSERAFIM; and Choi Jae-yoon, CEO of Hello82, the label handling U.S. distribution for groups including ATEEZ and P1Harmony.
