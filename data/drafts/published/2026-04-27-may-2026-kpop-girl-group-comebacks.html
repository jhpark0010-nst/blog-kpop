May 2026 is shaping up to be a fierce battleground for K-pop girl groups. The leading girl groups from the four major agencies — HYBE, SM, JYP, and YG — have all announced comebacks at the same time, setting the stage for an all-out competition for the title of 'May Queen.'

The 4th-generation representative girl groups from HYBE, SM, JYP, and YG — LE SSERAFIM, aespa, NMIXX, and BABYMONSTER — are all gearing up for May releases. With I.O.I activities added to the mix as well, May is expected to bring the most intense head-to-head competition the music scene has seen in some time.

Under the HYBE label, ILLIT will charge ahead on the 30th with their 4th mini album 'Mamamalapinatapy,' while LE SSERAFIM will bring their fierce narrative back to the forefront on May 22 with their 2nd full-length album 'Pureflow.' LE SSERAFIM previously achieved million-seller status with their 1st full album 'UNFORGIVEN' and wrote a reverse-charting success story with 'Eve, Psyche & the Bluebeard's Wife,' though they went through a period of stagnation following live performance controversies. However, they have fully regained their momentum through the single 'Spaghetti,' and all eyes are on what kind of new impact their fierce narrative in this new album will have on the public.

aespa is also aiming for a leap forward on the 29th with their 2nd full-length album 'Lemonade.' They had been riding an unstoppable wave of success with their 1st full album 'Armageddon' and 5th mini album 'Whiplash,' but the single 'Dirty Work' and 6th mini album 'Richmand' delivered somewhat underwhelming results compared to expectations, giving the group a period to catch their breath. With this new release, aespa is expected to bring their distinctive worldview and identity back to center stage, rallying fans once again with a clear strategy of reclaiming their identity.

NMIXX has recently hit a career high with their 1st full album 'Blue Valentine' and continues on a steep upward trajectory. Having smartly expanded their mainstream appeal while staying true to their unique musical color, it remains to be seen whether their May 11 release 'Heavy Serenade' will firmly cement their position as a leading 4th-generation act. Meanwhile, ITZY — who wrote a reverse-charting success story six years after debut thanks to the 'Daechunonno' craze — is setting their sights on a second golden era with their 12th mini album 'MOTTO,' dropping on the 18th.

BABYMONSTER's strength lies in the intense performances and concepts that YG is known for, though a gap in response between domestic and international audiences still exists. After warming up with their 3rd mini album 'Chum' on the 4th, it remains to be seen whether BABYMONSTER can fully inherit YG's legacy as a 'powerhouse of girl groups' and solidify their standing through their 2nd full-length album, set for release in the second half of this year.

This May comeback showdown can truly be called a 'battle of the stars,' with the flagship groups of the four major agencies all entering the arena at once. Each carries a different narrative and set of challenges, and it remains to be seen who will emerge as the final winner. What is certain is that their spectacular comebacks will offer K-pop fans around the world an unparalleled feast for the eyes and ears.
