ZEROBASEONE (ZB1) has made their comeback with their 6th mini album 'Ascend-', released on May 18 at 6 p.m. KST. This marks the group's first release as a reformed 5-member unit, having restructured from their original 9-member lineup. 'We prepared with the same fierce determination we had at our debut,' said Sung Hanbin. 'We see this as a turning point to make a strong impression again on our fandom, the ZEROs, and the general public.'

ZEROBASEONE was formed through Mnet's audition program 'Boys Planet' in 2023 and operated as a project group for 2 years and 8 months until March of this year. Following the end of that period, members Sung Hanbin, Kim Jiwoong, Seok Matthew, Kim Taerae, and Park Gunwook remained in ZEROBASEONE, while Jang Hao, Ricky, Kim Gyuvin, and Han Yujin returned to their original agency YH Entertainment and are preparing to debut as a new group called ANDBLE.

Park Gunwook reflected on the transition: 'I was worried that activities as a five-member group might feel empty, but when we actually did it, the absence wasn't as noticeable as I expected. My mindset also changed after our last concert as nine members. I thought we could show more of each individual's charm, so I practiced harder, and the feeling grew that we could turn this crisis into an opportunity.'

ANDBLE is set to release their 1st mini album 'Sequence 01: Curiosity' on May 26, meaning the two groups' promotional periods overlap. Park Gunwook said, 'We still keep in touch, share meals, and cheer each other on.' Sung Hanbin added, 'In the K-pop market, being mentioned together could itself create good synergy. I hope we each show what we've prepared well and that positive word-of-mouth spreads for both of us.'

The new album 'Ascend-' carries the members' bold ambition to keep moving toward greater heights without stopping. Sung Hanbin explained that the hyphen (-) at the end of the album title 'represents continuity — the process of never stopping new challenges is what we think youth looks like right now.' Seok Matthew noted, 'Many people will be watching this activity with a critical eye, so we have to do even better. We personally discussed and chose both the title track and B-side tracks together, and our opinions went into other areas like choreography as well. We really worked hard on the recordings.'

The title track 'TOP 5' is a dance-pop song that reinterprets the sensibility of the 2000s in a modern way. Kim Jiwoong said, 'We looked up a lot of music and visual references from the 2000s. We talked about the appeal that comes from stripping things away rather than filling them up — we wanted to show the beauty of space rather than flashiness.' Park Gunwook spoke about his self-composed B-side track 'Customize': 'I wanted to make a song that fits the members like a tailored suit made just for them. I was grateful that the members pulled it off even better than I expected in the recording studio.' The members collectively said they chose to keep the team going 'because we wanted more time together with the members and with our fans.' Sung Hanbin added, 'I was grateful to have found members I truly connect with among so many people. More than anything, I had a strong desire to show the team ZEROBASEONE to the fans who waited for us, for a long time.' Kim Taerae said, 'I want to remain an artist that ZEROs can be proud of going forward.'
