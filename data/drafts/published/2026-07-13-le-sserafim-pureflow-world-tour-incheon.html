LE SSERAFIM perfectly embodied the message of their second full-length album on stage, opening the curtain on their second world tour in spectacular fashion. The concerts on July 11 and 12 at Inspire Arena in Incheon — held under the name '2026 LE SSERAFIM TOUR PUREFLOW IN INCHEON' — were live-streamed on the global superfan platform Weverse, reaching fans across 58 countries and regions worldwide.

The show was structured to naturally follow the new album's message of 'a festival completed through solidarity.' Dozens of dancers wearing different masks performed the opening segment, evoking the 'Creatures' that appear in the music video for the second album's lead single 'CELEBRATION.' LE SSERAFIM brought the value of solidarity embedded in the song and its music video to life on stage, allowing the audience to step into the album's narrative together and enjoy the concert.

With the venue already buzzing from the dancers' performance, LE SSERAFIM opened with 'CELEBRATION.' The exciting melodic techno and hardstyle melody prompted a massive crowd sing-along from the very first song. The members declared, 'Today is the last day of the Incheon concert. We are confident we will enjoy it to the very end. We want FEARNOT (the fandom name) to have no regrets and have a great time.'

The performances of tracks from the second full-length album were the highlight of the concert. Songs being performed live for the first time — including 'How Can We Date More,' 'Trust Exercise,' and 'Irony' — drew especially loud cheers. 'Saki (feat. Aliyah's Interlude)' delivered a musical-like performance through its playfully exchanged chorus and the members' expressive acting. The excitement peaked when all five members performed the title track 'BOOMPALA' together as a complete unit.

Existing hit songs were given new energy with added band sounds, and careful attention to smooth song transitions kept the flow unbroken throughout the approximately 120-minute runtime. During hit song segments including 'ANTIFRAGILE' and 'CRAZY,' the members ran out to the extended stage to connect closely with the audience. 'SPAGHETTI (feat. j-hope of BTS)' received an especially enriched performance with a new intro starting from lying on stage and an added dance break. LE SSERAFIM once again proved their standing as top performers among girl groups with synchronized choreography, steady live vocals, and explosive energy.

The passion of the audience was also a key element completing the concert. Fans dressed in the designated pink and red color code, filling the seats with color while cheering and chanting alongside the members. Even during the wait for the encore, the energy never cooled — fans sang along to 'BOOMPALA' and 'CELEBRATION' with a karaoke-concept video. The members shared their heartfelt feelings: 'The tour is the time when we can most intuitively feel what story we want to tell. FEARNOT are the reason we stand on stage and the reason we exist.' They added, 'In this concert, which shares its name with our full-length album, we wanted to sincerely convey the meaning and narrative of each and every song.' Meanwhile, LE SSERAFIM will head into their Japan tour starting on the 25th, and will make their first appearance at Japan's largest music festival, SUMMER SONIC 2026, performing in Osaka on August 14 and Tokyo on August 16.
