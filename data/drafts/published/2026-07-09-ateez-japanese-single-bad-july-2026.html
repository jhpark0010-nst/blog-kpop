ATEEZ has announced the release of their fifth Japanese single on July 29, 2026 — their first Japanese single comeback in 10 months. The group had previously dominated Korean online algorithms with a fancam of member Choi San performing 'Bukbu Daegong' (North Artillery Commander) on SBS Inkigayo, drawing explosive reactions from fans.

The upcoming Japanese single will include a total of 6 tracks. The title track is a Japanese-language version of 'BAD,' the lead track from their 14th mini album, which has already swept charts worldwide. The single also includes two new songs created specifically for this release: 'Seeker' and 'HIGHER,' along with instrumental versions of all tracks.

The album explores the universal theme of love through various emotional perspectives, showcasing a new side of ATEEZ's musical charm. ATEEZ has also announced a showcase to be held on July 28, the day before the single's release, raising fans' anticipation even further.

Following the Japanese single release, ATEEZ will hold a special fan meeting titled '2026 ATINY'S VOYAGE : TINY MYSTERY' from August 4 to 6 at K-Arena Yokohama, where they plan to create unforgettable memories with local fans.

The title track 'BAD' from their 14th mini album has been gaining explosive popularity across multiple charts and spreading rapidly through short-form platforms. With the Japanese version now on the way, attention is focused on whether it will captivate local fans in Japan as well. ATEEZ has been continuously breaking records on domestic and international charts with their fifth album in the 'GOLDEN HOUR' series.

Meanwhile, the fancam of Choi San from the late June SBS Inkigayo performance went viral online, with fans leaving comments such as 'his chest is unreal' and 'North Artillery Commander Choi San is amazing,' generating a massive response.
