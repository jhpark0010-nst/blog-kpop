(G)I-DLE — consisting of Miyeon, Minnie, Soyeon, Yuqi, and Shuhua — have officially fired the starting gun on their comeback with a pre-release track. On June 14, their agency Cube Entertainment dropped the music video for 'Crow' on the group's official YouTube channel. The track serves as the pre-release single from their 9th mini album, 'We made'.

The music video delivers an overwhelming atmosphere through a mega-crew performance and the members' dark, striking visuals. Soyeon opens the video standing beneath a massive flag surrounded by large-scale dancers, instantly drawing viewers in with her intense rapping and performance. Yuqi and Shuhua then add tension by exchanging rap verses while facing each other in the middle of a crowd.

Minnie creates a mysterious mood as she is framed against the shadow of crow wings cast across a wall, while Miyeon captures attention by emerging between flags beneath a golden sunset. Toward the end of the video, a flock of crows circles the members as they stand atop a formation of dancers with giant flags billowing around them, conveying the message of 'Crow' — to rise higher without wavering, no matter the circumstances.

'Crow' reinterprets the crow not as a simple symbol of bad luck, but as a symbol of the will and instinct to take flight again in any situation. The track is set for official release across major online music platforms on June 15 at 6 p.m. KST.

(G)I-DLE had previously debuted 'Crow' in February at their '2026 i-dle WORLD TOUR [Syncopation] IN SEOUL', where the song drew attention as an expression of the group's bold confidence.
