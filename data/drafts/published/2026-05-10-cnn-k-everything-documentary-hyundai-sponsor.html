Hyundai announced on May 10 that 'K-Everything,' a CNN documentary series it solely sponsors, made its world premiere on May 9, 2026. The four-part series takes an in-depth look at the driving forces behind Korean culture, which has come to lead global popular culture.

The series examines how Korean culture — rooted in tradition and innovation — has established itself as a worldwide cultural phenomenon, covering the themes of music, film, food, and beauty. Hyundai shared these details in its official announcement.

Tony Award-nominated actor, director, and producer Daniel Dae Kim serves as executive producer and host of 'K-Everything.' The first episode, focused on music, spotlights artists including Psy, Taeyang, and Jeon Somi to explore the growth engine of the K-pop industry and the influence created by fandom culture.

The second episode, 'K-Film,' features actor Lee Byung-hun, director Yeon Sang-ho, screenwriter Kim Eun-sook, and CJ Group Vice Chairwoman Lee Mi-kyung as they trace the changes and journey of the Korean film and drama industry. The third episode, 'K-Food,' features chef Corey Lee — the first Korean-American chef to earn three Michelin stars — alongside Korea's only three-Michelin-star chef Kang Min-goo and Korean cuisine specialist chef Cho Hee-sook, covering the history, philosophy, and global culinary evolution of Korean food.

The final episode on K-beauty examines how Korean standards of beauty have taken root around the world. The series, released on CNN International, is also available on OTT platforms including Coupang Play and HBO Max.

A Hyundai representative stated, 'As a company that grew with its roots in Korea, we intend to put into practice our group vision of Progress for Humanity through this series.'
