Alternative K-pop group Balming Tiger's second full-length album 'Gongbu' has been selected for NPR's 'The albums and songs of 2026 that we love the most (so far)' list. Label CAM announced the news on July 3.

'Gongbu' is an album set against the backdrop of a fictional research institute called 'Gongbu Korea,' exploring a narrative about human dreams and the unconscious.

NPR releases its mid-year list each year without rankings, featuring albums and songs recommended by its editors and critics.

NPR described the album, saying: 'Gongbu unfolds a bold world of sound and rhythm that draws you in even without knowing Korean.' The outlet added that it 'moves freely between playful and rough energy, fairy-tale-like innocence, and deep grooves, delivering unpredictable twists.'

In addition to NPR, prominent outlets including the New York Times, music media Clash Magazine, Alternative Press, Wonderland, The Greatest, and Huck have all offered praise for 'Gongbu.'

CAM stated: 'The consecutive praise from major international outlets proves that the unique musical world and artistic experimentation Balming Tiger has presented are receiving high recognition in the global music market.' Balming Tiger released 'Gongbu' in May, and in June held a solo concert in Seoul to celebrate the album's release.
