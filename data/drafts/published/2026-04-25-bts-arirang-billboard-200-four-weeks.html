BTS has broken their own Billboard record with their fifth full-length album ARIRANG. According to a chart preview article published on Billboard's official website on April 20, ARIRANG debuted at No. 1 on the Billboard 200 (dated April 4) upon its release on March 20, held the top spot for three consecutive weeks, and now lands at No. 3 on the April 25 chart — marking four straight weeks in the top tier and proving the album's broad mainstream appeal.

According to Billboard, ARIRANG logged 78,000 album units this week. That figure combines pure album sales, SEA units (streaming equivalent albums), and TEA units (track equivalent albums from digital download counts).

ARIRANG now holds BTS's highest-ever Billboard 200 ranking in a fourth chart week. The previous best was No. 8, achieved by the 2019 mini-album 'MAP OF THE SOUL : PERSONA'. With this outstanding performance on the global chart, ARIRANG is cementing itself as the group's biggest commercial success. Notably, it signals that BTS's music is reaching far beyond the fandom to a much wider pool of listeners — a sign that the 'BTS 2.0' era the group pledged with this album is becoming reality.

BTS's global popularity is carrying over into their live performances. The seven members kicked off the BTS WORLD TOUR 'ARIRANG' at Goyang Stadium on April 9 and April 11–12, performing for approximately 132,000 fans. Their Tokyo Dome shows on April 17–18 drew 110,000 attendees over two days. A standout moment came when the Tokyo Dome crowd spontaneously sang along to the Korean folk song 'Arirang' during a performance of new track 'Body to Body,' which incorporates the melody — creating a scene that will be remembered in concert history. BTS will next bring the tour to North America, with shows at Raymond James Stadium in Tampa, Florida on April 25–26 and April 28.

The BTS WORLD TOUR 'ARIRANG' spans 85 shows across 34 cities, making it the highest number of dates for a single tour by a Korean artist. As the group sets new records and creates iconic moments in each city, anticipation for the remaining shows continues to grow.

Meanwhile, BTS's title track 'SWIM' has achieved a cumulative 14 music show wins on Korean broadcast programs — a remarkable feat accomplished without any in-person TV appearances. On April 19, album track '2.0' also topped SBS Inkigayo, demonstrating the enthusiastic response to the album as a whole.
