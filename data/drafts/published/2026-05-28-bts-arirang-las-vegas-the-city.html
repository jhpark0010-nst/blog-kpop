On May 25, 2026 (local time) in Las Vegas, Nevada, even though it was the Memorial Day holiday with no BTS world tour concert scheduled, traces of BTS and ARMY (BTS fans) were visible everywhere throughout the city — a lingering afterglow from the previous night's electrifying performance.

BTS logo designs from the group's fifth studio album 'Arirang' were printed on the red milk foam of matcha lattes sold at cafes, and a meat set called the 'Arirang Combo' was selling out rapidly at a Korean BBQ restaurant. An immersive art museum screened the music video for '2.0,' a track from 'Arirang,' across four walls simultaneously, while a monorail was wrapped with the phrase 'What Is Your Arirang.' A BTS club filled an entire night playing nothing but BTS songs.

All of this was part of 'The City Arirang Las Vegas,' an urban project tied to the BTS world tour. Arte Museum Las Vegas, which is running a BTS collaboration exhibition, was packed with ARMY. Visitors flooded in to see exhibits themed around 'Arirang' album tracks including 'No.29,' 'Body to Body,' 'Swim,' '2.0,' and 'Into the Sun,' setting a new single-day attendance record since the museum opened in 2023. Inside the 'Arirang Garden' section of the BTS collaboration exhibit, fans stood in one spot watching the 15-minute video on repeat.

Matthew Park, general manager of Arte Museum, said, 'We had four to five times our usual number of visitors.' He added, 'The maximum capacity inside the space is 750 people, but visitors kept exceeding that number, so we managed the flow by letting one person in as one person left.'

Long lines of at least one to two hours formed at cafes and restaurants as well. At a Korean BBQ restaurant, ARMY waited patiently inside and outside despite warnings that the wait was at least two hours. After the long wait, diners were served the 'Arirang Combo' — raw meat with an 'Arirang flag' stuck in it, alongside tteokbokki and somyeon noodles. Though a straightforward combination, fans were willing to wait because BTS members had visited the restaurant.

With events taking place all across the city, ARMY were easy to spot on the streets too. Simply carrying or wearing BTS-related items was enough for fellow fans to quietly appear and hand over small gifts before moving on. Amy and Debra, who had come from Jacksonville, Florida, shyly approached a reporter carrying a BTS eco bag and handed over a small pouch. Inside the purple bundle — purple being the symbolic color of BTS ARMY — were candies, a hand-woven bracelet, a sticker of member Suga (Min Yoongi), and an 'Arirang' logo keychain. It is a distinctive fan culture of giving gifts and sharing joy with fellow ARMY, expecting nothing in return.

At night, BTS's influence became even more vivid. From May 23 through 28, Las Vegas lit up its major landmarks in red — the symbolic color of 'Arirang' — from 11 p.m. to midnight under the name 'Landmark Arirang Red Illumination.' A BTS advertisement was displayed on the Sphere, Las Vegas's iconic venue, and red lighting was added to large structures including the Eiffel Tower replica, a pyramid, and a Ferris wheel. After-party clubs played BTS songs all night long so ARMY could keep the energy going. It all made perfect sense in light of what V had said near the end of the previous night's concert: 'I was trying to sleep but woke up to the sound of fireworks. When I opened the hotel curtains, everything was BTS. I thought, Las Vegas is really welcoming us.'
