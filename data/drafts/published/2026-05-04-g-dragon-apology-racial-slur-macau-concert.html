G-Dragon has come under fire after wearing a stage costume bearing a racial slur during his performance at the outdoor concert 'K-SPARK IN MACAU' on May 2. His agency, Galaxy Corporation, issued an official apology on May 4 in response to the controversy.

The concert featured not only G-Dragon but also other well-known Korean stars including Daesung, P1Harmony, KISS OF LIFE, and Kiki. During his set, G-Dragon wore a sleeveless top long enough to cover his hips, and it was the text printed on that garment that sparked the controversy.

The word 'NEGER' was written on his outfit — a term that evokes the racial slurs 'Nigger' and 'Negro' and is the Dutch-language equivalent, carrying the same derogatory meaning. Whether G-Dragon was aware of the text on his costume at the time of the performance remains unknown, but given his massive global popularity, the backlash has been unavoidable.

Galaxy Corporation moved to address the situation by posting an official apology statement. The agency said, 'We sincerely apologize for the inclusion of a phrase in the performance costume that was inappropriate in its social and cultural context.' The statement continued, 'Through this incident, we have once again recognized the importance of more careful cultural sensitivity and responsible review, and we will examine and improve our overall internal review and verification procedures — including styling — to operate under more cautious standards.'

The agency closed by stating, 'We sincerely apologize once again for causing disappointment and concern through this incident.'
