BTS's comeback has driven a significant surge in users on Hybe's fan platform Weverse. According to Weverse Company, a subsidiary of Hybe, Weverse's monthly active users (MAU) in Q1 2026 reached 13.37 million — a 20% increase compared to the previous quarter.

Weverse Company explained that in connection with the release of BTS's fifth album 'ARIRANG,' approximately 30 service areas were utilized across the board for artist and fan activities, including community features, commerce, media, and live streaming.

When the BTS comeback album was announced via Weverse on January 5, daily visitors that day surged to 3.37 million — a 246% jump compared to the day before. On January 14 and 16, when BTS's world tour 'ARIRANG' and pre-orders for the same-titled album were announced, the average daily visitor count over those two days reached 5.24 million. Weverse Company noted that this was a 93% increase from the daily average of 2.71 million visitors recorded between January 1 and 13, before the announcements.

On February 9, when news of the Gwanghwamun Square comeback show was first announced, visitors increased 44% compared to the previous day. Around the time of the comeback, fans posted approximately 8 million posts, and sent 448,092 messages to members through the 'Fan Letter' service. The seven BTS members also actively communicated with fans through a total of 10 'Weverse Live' real-time broadcasts.

A video in which the members personally introduced various aspects of the new album, posted on the afternoon of March 20 — the album's release date — recorded 11.7 million views. The January 16 live broadcast, in which the members revealed for the first time why they chose 'ARIRANG' as the album title, garnered approximately 7.8 million views, with around 1.1 million comments left by fans.

In addition, Weverse offered a range of features including the commerce service 'Weverse Shop,' the world tour online streaming service 'Weverse Concert,' and the real-time playlist sharing service 'Listening Party.' Weverse Company stated, 'BTS's comeback, which showcased diverse activities and promotions spanning both online and offline, once again confirmed the versatility and service scope of Weverse in providing 360-degree support for fan activities,' adding that they will continue to expand and advance services as an essential platform for fan activities.
