BTS has achieved an impressive four consecutive weeks on the Billboard Hot 100 with four songs from their fifth studio album ARIRANG. According to Billboard's April 21, 2026 chart update, the album's title track SWIM landed at No. 10 on the main singles chart — down five spots from the previous week, but still a strong showing for the group's ongoing chart run.

Three additional ARIRANG album tracks also maintained their Hot 100 presence. Body to Body came in at No. 69, while 2.0 and Hooligan rounded out the quartet at No. 88 and No. 90, respectively. The sustained multi-track performance highlights the broad appeal of the album across different listener demographics in the United States.

Also making waves on the Hot 100 this week is Golden, the original soundtrack from the Netflix animated film K-Pop Demon Hunters, which climbed to No. 7 — marking its remarkable 43rd consecutive week on the chart. The song's enduring popularity underscores the global reach of K-pop-inspired media beyond traditional music releases.

Jennie's remix contribution to Tame Impala's Dracula continued its upward momentum, rising to No. 15 on the Hot 100 and setting a new personal peak for the track. It had previously reached No. 17 the week before, making this back-to-back record-breaking chart performance for the collaboration.

Girl group CATSEYE made a notable Hot 100 debut this week with their new single PINKY UP, entering the chart at No. 28. On the Billboard 200 album chart, BTS's ARIRANG slipped two spots to No. 3, while the K-Pop Demon Hunters OST album debuted at No. 16. CATSEYE's second mini-album Beautiful Chaos, released in June of last year, also reappeared on the chart at No. 95.
