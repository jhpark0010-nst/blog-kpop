BTS's comeback and the broader surge in K-pop popularity pushed South Korea's physical album (CD) exports to a record high in the first quarter of 2026, surpassing $100 million — approximately 170 billion Korean won — for the first time ever in a single quarter.

According to the Korea Customs Service on April 28, Q1 2026 album exports totaled $120 million (approximately 177 billion won), a 159.0% increase compared to the same period last year. This marks the highest quarterly figure ever recorded.

The Q1 result represents 41% of last year's full-year export total of $300 million (approximately 429.5 billion won), raising expectations that the annual record could also be broken this year. Album exports have set a same-quarter record for every quarter since Q3 of last year, and this is the first time the quarterly figure has crossed the $100 million mark.

By country, the United States ranked first with $36 million, accounting for 28.8% of the total, followed by Japan at $31 million (25.3%), the European Union at $21 million (16.5%), China at $18 million (14.4%), and Taiwan at $9 million (6.9%). The US recorded the highest growth rate, up 506.4% year-on-year. The EU rose 461.9%, Japan 157.4%, and China 38.2%.

By region, non-Asian markets showed particularly strong growth. The non-Asia export growth rate for Q1 was 408.3%, far exceeding the Asia growth rate of 71.9%. North America specifically rose 449.2% and Europe 397.7%.

Exports reached a total of 131 countries. Of those, 94 countries (72%) recorded their highest-ever Q1 figures, and 61 countries (47%) hit all-time quarterly records. Exports to other countries also grew 69.3% to $10 million.
