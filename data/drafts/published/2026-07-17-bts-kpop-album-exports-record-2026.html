K-pop album exports reached a record high in the first half of 2026, rising 125% year-on-year to $257.47 million (approximately 382.3 billion Korean won), according to Korea Customs Service trade statistics. Physical album sales also rebounded to approximately 55 million copies, putting the industry within reach of the 100-million annual sales milestone for the first time since 2023. The surge was driven by the comebacks of flagship global acts BTS and BLACKPINK.

By export destination, the United States ranked first with $74.11 million — an unusual result that saw it overtake Japan. China came in second at $61.17 million, followed by Japan at $45.61 million. Germany, Taiwan, Hong Kong, the Netherlands, the United Kingdom, France, and Poland rounded out the top ten export destinations.

In the first half of the year, BTS released their 5th studio album 'ARIRANG' in March — their first album in three years and nine months — topping both the Billboard Hot 100 and Billboard 200. They also launched what is described as the largest-scale world tour in K-pop history, beginning in Goyang in April. In February, BLACKPINK released their third mini-album 'DEADLINE,' which had sold close to 2 million copies as of June. Rookie acts KIKI and Hearts2Hearts drew strong responses with '404 (New Era)' and 'RUDE!' respectively, while CORTIS caused a sensation with 'REDRED.' Girl group Lisn hit with 'LOVE ATTACK,' going viral through a 'Geoje Ya-ho!' internet meme and being hailed as a 'miracle of a mid-tier idol.'

Music data journalist Kim Jin-woo noted that BTS and BLACKPINK together sold a combined approximately 6 million copies, acting as a catalyst that broke through the stagnation in the K-pop market. He added that if the current growth trend continues into the second half, the industry could achieve annual sales of 100 million copies for the first time since 2023. He also observed that while sales were once concentrated among a few groups, this year other groups have become more competitive, strengthening the 'middle tier.' In April, TXT and &TEAM each captured around 10% market share, and in June, RIIZE, ATEEZ, and BOYNEXTDOOR similarly each held roughly 10% share.

BTS's 5th album 'ARIRANG' made K-pop history on the Billboard 200 by reaching No. 1 for three consecutive weeks — a first for a K-pop act. The title track 'SWIM' gave the group their seventh No. 1 on the Hot 100. According to Luminate's Midyear Report, 'ARIRANG' topped U.S. first-half sales with 567,000 CDs and 331,000 LPs sold, ranking No. 1 in both formats. It placed 4th on the overall top 10 albums chart combining CD, LP, and streaming. Luminate CEO Rob Jonas stated in the report that 'ARIRANG' caused total U.S. CD sales to surge 16% to 16.3 million copies, and that the 'ARIRANG Effect' lifted South Korea to 3rd place in Luminate's music export power rankings.

In the U.S. first-half CD sales top 10, 'ARIRANG' led the chart, followed by ENHYPEN's 'THE SIN : VANISH' at No. 2 (286,000 copies), ATEEZ's 'GOLDEN HOUR : PART 4' at No. 3 (263,000), 'GOLDEN HOUR : PART 5' at No. 4 (212,000), CORTIS's 'GREENGREEN' at No. 5 (169,000), Stray Kids' 'DO IT' at No. 6 (141,000), and TXT's '7TH YEAR: When the Wind Briefly Stopped in the Thorns' at No. 7 (123,000). KATSEYE's 'SIS (Soft Is Strong)' placed 10th with 106,000 copies, meaning 8 of the top 10 albums were K-pop releases.

Looking ahead to the second half of 2026, BIGBANG will kick off a world tour on August 21–23 at Goyang Stadium to mark their 20th debut anniversary — their first solo concerts since 2017. At Coachella in April, they announced plans to release a new album as a full group. Stray Kids will release their new mini-album 'THIS & THAT' on August 7, targeting their ninth No. 1 on the Billboard 200. They will open their world tour 'RUN IT' at Seoul Olympic Park KSPO Dome on July 25–26 and 29 and August 1–2, and are set to perform at Brazil's Rock in Rio on September 11 as well as their own new festival 'STRAYCITY.' Red Velvet, ENHYPEN, and NCT 127 are also scheduled for second-half comebacks. A representative from a major entertainment agency said that with popular acts like Stray Kids and ENHYPEN releasing new music and holding concerts, large-scale world tours are expected to lead the K-pop market in the second half of the year.
