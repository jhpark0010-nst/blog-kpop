BTS's fifth studio album 'ARIRANG' has remained in the Top 10 of the Billboard 200 for eight consecutive weeks. According to Billboard on May 19 (local time), 'ARIRANG' landed at No. 8 this week, dropping one spot from the previous week. The album made history immediately after its release by becoming the first K-pop album to top the chart for three consecutive weeks, then followed with positions of No. 3, No. 4, No. 5, and No. 7 over the next four weeks.

The title track 'SWIM' charted at No. 34 on the Hot 100, falling four spots from the prior week. On the Billboard Global chart (excluding the U.S.), the song claimed its sixth all-time No. 1. After holding the top spot for four consecutive weeks, 'SWIM' dropped to No. 2 for two weeks before returning to No. 1 for two more consecutive weeks.

Also on the Billboard 200, CORTIS — who share a label with BTS — debuted at No. 3 with their second mini-album 'GREENGREEN'. The soundtrack album for the Netflix animated film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters' entered at No. 22, and KATSEYE's 'Beautiful Chaos' charted at No. 136.

On the Hot 100, Tame Impala's 'Dracula' — which features Jennie on the remix — held at No. 10 for the second consecutive week. The 'K-Pop Demon Hunters' OST track 'Golden' came in at No. 13, and KATSEYE's 'Pinky Up' placed at No. 76.

Following the release of the Michael Jackson biopic 'Michael', a total of 11 Michael Jackson albums or songs entered the Billboard main charts. His albums 'Thriller' and 'Number Ones' held at No. 5 and No. 6 on the Billboard 200 for the second straight week, while 'Billie Jean' rose two spots to No. 15 on the Hot 100.
