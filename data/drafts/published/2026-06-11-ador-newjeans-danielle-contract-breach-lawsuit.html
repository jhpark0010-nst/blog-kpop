ADOR explained on June 11 why it filed an exclusive contract termination lawsuit against NewJeans member Danielle specifically. The Seoul Central District Court's Civil Division 31 held the second pleading session that afternoon for the damages suit ADOR filed against Danielle, her family, and former CEO Min Hee-jin.

ADOR's legal team stated, 'The reason we requested termination of the exclusive contract only with Danielle among the NewJeans members is because Danielle took independent actions that the other members did not.' They added, 'Considering the role of Danielle's mother and the circumstances suggesting her involvement in the contract termination, we determined it was difficult to maintain a trust relationship.'

ADOR further claimed, 'Looking at the conversations exchanged by former CEO Min Hee-jin and others on the evening the first-instance ruling on the injunction to suspend the exclusive contract was issued, the content included plans for a music video shoot and contract related to Danielle's band featuring after ComplexCon, as well as album release details.' They also stated that 240 million KRW had already been invested in artist restructuring costs.

Regarding those circumstances, ADOR said, 'We had no choice but to conclude that there was absolutely no intention of accepting the injunction ruling.' They continued, 'There were also indications that Danielle's mother made remarks to the effect of pressuring collaboration. The plaintiff came to learn of these matters belatedly, and in light of their seriousness, determined it was impossible to continue the contract with Danielle and issued the termination notice.'

ADOR also addressed the defense's claim that the lawsuit would halt Danielle's entertainment activities, saying, 'The defense claims that Danielle's entertainment activities will be suspended due to this lawsuit, but Danielle is free to carry out her entertainment activities. We cannot understand why the defense says such a thing.'

ADOR also explained its reasons for seeking damages from former CEO Min Hee-jin and Danielle's mother. The company stated, 'Telegram conversation records show circumstances in which former CEO Min Hee-jin actively persuaded the NewJeans members' parents to break their exclusive contracts, including saying she would arrange things so they would not have to bear penalty fees and that she would prepare compensation.' ADOR added, 'Danielle's mother is the person among the NewJeans parents who played the largest role in participating in former CEO Min's alleged illegal conduct,' and expressed doubt as to 'whether the NewJeans members made the decision to break their contracts entirely on their own.'
