CORTIS, a rookie boy group under BigHit Music that debuted in August 2025, is rapidly making its mark with distinctive self-produced music. Their new single 'REDRED' topped Melon's Top 100 chart on May 13, making CORTIS the first boy group with less than one year since debut to reach No. 1 on Melon in the past year. Their second mini album 'GREENGREEN', released on May 4, sold 2.31 million copies in its first week according to Hanteo Chart.

That first-week sales figure is the second highest among K-pop albums released this year, trailing only BTS's fifth full-length album 'ARIRANG', which sold 4.17 million copies in its first week when it dropped in March. The group's global presence is also growing: 'REDRED' entered the Billboard 'Bubbling Under Hot 100' chart at No. 17, and the group's official SNS accounts have surpassed 11 million followers.

The music industry points to CORTIS's unique team identity as a 'Young Creator Crew' — abbreviated as 'YoungCC' in Korean fan culture — as the core reason behind their popularity. All members participate in songwriting, composing, producing, choreography creation, and video production. While there have been idols who write their own songs, such as (G)I-DLE's Jeon Soyeon or Block B's Zico, it has been rare for members to be involved across the entire album production process. All members are credited on both their debut album from September last year and this new release.

Their creative output emphasizes a raw, unfiltered sensibility. The title track 'REDRED' carries a message about what CORTIS stands against and aspires toward. The track 'ACAI' was inspired by the acai bowls the members ate every day during album preparation. Another track, 'Young Creator Crew', captures the members' honest feelings about the 'YoungCC' label that defines the team. Album photos were also shot in everyday clothes at familiar locations from their trainee days, such as the Sinsa 2 overpass (Gilmajeong Bridge), emphasizing an unadorned atmosphere.

At the showcase for the new album, CORTIS members cited 'honesty' as the secret to their popularity. According to TikTok, 'REDRED' was used as background music in over 460,000 short-form videos within 8 days of release, led by the so-called 'flappy-ear dance challenge.' On Melon, listeners aged 10 to 20 accounted for 38% of 'REDRED' streams. Analysts note that presenting a comfortable and natural image — rather than mystique or elaborate worldbuilding — has resonated with Generation Z while feeling fresh to older audiences.

Music critic Lim Jin-mo commented, 'I think they are a team that will show a new K-pop direction after BTS,' adding that 'the strength of members directly creating music and content is clear, and there is a strong possibility they will stand out not only domestically but globally, including in the U.S. market.' Critic Kim Do-heon said, 'BTS, their label seniors, also showed their color with unique music early on,' and that 'CORTIS has left a strong impression from debut as a team that breaks existing K-pop conventions.' He added, 'They use stimulating sounds, but the lyrics are cute and witty — they borrow the appeal of underground hip-hop while delivering it in an all-ages format.' British magazine Dazed also noted that CORTIS is 'relatively free from the narrative and worldbuilding devices K-pop frequently employs,' and that 'their unaffected attitude and somewhat rough but cheerful image has become a strength.'
