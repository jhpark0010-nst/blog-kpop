BTS surprised fans on June 19 at 6 p.m. KST with the surprise release of the music video for 'Merry Go Round,' a track from their fifth full-length album ARIRANG. The music video was produced through a special collaboration with global audio and music streaming platform Spotify, and is available exclusively on that platform.

The album track 'Merry Go Round' tells the story of people who quietly endure and push through the exhausting, repetitive cycles of everyday life — much like a merry-go-round that never stops spinning. The newly unveiled music video visually brings this profound message to life through striking black-and-white cinematography and a variety of symbolic objects, leaving a deep and lasting impression.

The video's narrative unfolds as the BTS members, each busy living their own separate lives, are drawn one by one to a single space as if by fate. Passing through different stretches of memory, they finally come to a stop in one place, freely sharing the countless stories they had kept in their hearts, and reaffirming the strong bond that connects them to one another.

Toward the end of the video, a dramatic twist is revealed: the space where the members gathered and talked is not simply a building, but a massive merry-go-round floating alone in the sky. The music video closes with a sweeping view of the merry-go-round spinning endlessly, as if drifting alone across a vast open sea, beautifully completing BTS's grand narrative of pressing forward in their own way amid the endless cycles of time.

Notably, the music video reinterprets the merry-go-round — a key symbolic object that appeared in the 2017 mega-hit music video for 'Spring Day' — through a modern sensibility, drawing deeply emotional reactions from ARMY (the group's fandom).

Meanwhile, BTS is set to continue their large-scale world tour across a total of 34 regions worldwide, spanning 88 shows.
