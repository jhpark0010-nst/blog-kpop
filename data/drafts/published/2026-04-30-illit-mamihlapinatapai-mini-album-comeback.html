ILLIT is releasing their 4th mini album 'MAMIHLAPINATAPAI' today, April 30, 2026, at 6PM KST. The album captures the group's most transparent and honest inner emotions at a moment when subtle feelings intersect, and features the addictive techno-genre title track 'It's Me'. The members are taking on the challenge of becoming everyone's favorite (K-pop fan term: 'ult bias') with a bold and cheeky charm never seen before.

Global music fans are already fired up. ILLIT's new album ranked No. 6 on Spotify's Countdown Chart Global (as of April 29). This chart tracks the top 10 pre-save counts — the number of Spotify users worldwide who set up notifications to be the first to hear about an album or single release — demonstrating the overwhelming interest listeners have in ILLIT. The following is a Q&A delivered by ILLIT through their agency BELIFT LAB.

Q: How do you feel about coming back with 'MAMIHLAPINATAPAI'? Minju: Just thinking about showing GLLIT (the fandom name) a different side of us already makes my heart race. We worked hard to prepare a fresh stage you've never seen before. Please look forward to ILLIT's unpredictable charm. Q: What is the meaning behind the unique album title? Wonhee: The album title 'MAMIHLAPINATAPAI' comes from the language of the Yaghan people of South America. It describes the moment when two people both want something but neither makes the first move. In this 4th mini album, it was used to express the excitement and tension felt after a first date, and the subtle emotions in between.

Q: What were your first impressions of the title track 'It's Me'? Yuna: From the very intro we were all headbanging and riding the rhythm together. The more we listened, the more addictive it got. I thought it was a song everyone could easily sing along to. Moka: 'It's Me' was a completely unexpected techno genre, so it felt fresh. At the moment when you're deeply wondering how to define your relationship with someone you like, the straightforward lyric shouting 'You're my number one, and that's me!' felt really satisfying. Wonhee: It's a different vibe from all the ILLIT songs we've shown so far, and it was so intense it surprised me. I was excited because it felt like our spectrum had expanded even further.

Q: What should listeners pay attention to in 'It's Me'? Yuna: It's characterized by an incredibly addictive hook and witty lyrics. Unique lyrics are one of the hallmarks of ILLIT's music, so I hope you pay close attention this time too. Moka: I thought of it as a 'hotpot' dish — stimulating, but once you start you can't stop. I highly recommend watching the vocals and performance that bring that sharp, punchy atmosphere to life. Iroha: It's a song that makes your body move on its own the moment you hear it. Come enjoy it with us!

Q: What parts of the performance should fans watch most closely? Minju: The killing part is a performance that combines our signature 'magical girl' hand gestures with rhythmic footwork. Watching our playful facial expressions on stage alongside the performance will give you a dopamine rush. Iroha: The performance is the most difficult we've ever done. We packed our energy in at maximum concentration. We have to perform the choreography powerfully from start to finish, and there are a lot of jumping moves in time with the beat. We had to match the angles precisely in every move, so we practiced a lot.

Q: How do you feel about your bold visual transformation? Yuna: People who've seen the concept photos will be surprised thinking 'ILLIT can do this too.' We tried a variety of stylings and each one suited us well. I felt like all the members have grown in their ability to pull off different concepts. Moka: I tried a short cut, and since I personally like bold styles, it was a lot of fun. I turned out to be better-looking than I expected. (laughs) Wonhee: Personally, I thought the bob hairstyle really suited Yuna unnie. There are a lot of new styles in the music video as well as the concept photos, so I think fans will love it.

Q: Tell us about the album tracks featuring strong member participation. Iroha: I participated in writing lyrics for the first time on 'paw, paw!' It's a song filled with affection for a beloved pet. I wrote the lyrics while thinking about a cat my family had raised for a long time since before I was born. The cat's cute little paw pads left such an impression on me that I put that into the lyrics. Minju: All the members are credited on 'Love, older you.' It's like a warm letter from ILLIT today — a little more resilient — to our younger selves during the difficult moments when we wanted to give up. We actually listened to the demo and each wrote a letter to our past selves, and that content was woven into the lyrics overall.

Q: What did you focus on most while preparing the 4th mini album? Yuna: Everything from the dancing to the vocals to the concept was a challenge, so I thought a lot about how to express it all well. All the members worked hard to be in perfect sync in order to leave a deep impression on everyone who watches our stage. Minju: I focused on the vocals. I changed my attitude section by section to maximize the variety of the songs. Watch us go from bold to haughty to alluring. Q: What do you want to hear or achieve through this promotion? Wonhee: I want to win No. 1 on a music show (a weekly K-pop music broadcast ranking program). It would mean so many people love our music, and that would make me truly happy. If we do get No. 1, I want to eat flounder dishes while performing an encore stage. Moka: I want people to say we're a team that absorbs and pulls off any color we're given. Iroha: The title track 'It's Me' is a song that shouts 'You're my number one, and that's me!' Through this promotion, we want to become everyone's favorite.

Q: What do you want to say to GLLIT who waited for the comeback? Yuna: Through our previous release 'NOT CUTE ANYMORE,' which signaled the start of our change, I felt so much love from so many people. This time with 'It's Me,' I think we can show yet another side of ourselves. I hope you look forward to it that much, and please enjoy the dopamine-filled stage. Minju: Thank you for waiting without wavering. We'll repay GLLIT, who cheers for us and sends us so much love anytime and anywhere, with an amazing stage. We'll work hard this promotion too, so let's do it together. Moka: I'm so happy we can finally present the 4th mini album. Please love this new side of ILLIT too. Wonhee: I'm already looking forward to what fun memories we'll make with GLLIT through this promotion. I hope it becomes a time when I can give GLLIT great energy and joy. We'll work hard to live up to your support and expectations. Iroha: We prepared our very best with the intention of surprising GLLIT, so I hope you have a great time enjoying it.
