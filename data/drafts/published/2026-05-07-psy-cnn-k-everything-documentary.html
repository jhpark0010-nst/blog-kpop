PSY is set to reaffirm his status as the original K-pop icon in a new program spotlighting the global popularity of K-pop today. The vehicle is 'K-Everything,' a new documentary series premiering on CNN International on May 9 (Korean Standard Time).

'K-Everything' is a program that explores the global influence and roots of Korean culture, which has been leading world cultural trends in recent years. Actor and producer Daniel Dae Kim serves as host. The series is a 4-part production that covers a wide range of Korean culture beloved around the world, including K-pop, drama, food, and beauty.

PSY will sit down with Daniel Dae Kim for a conversation on the K-pop segment, sharing his views on K-pop's popularity trends and the global super-fan phenomenon that drives the industry.

On May 6, an opener preview clip featuring PSY was released through CNN's official social channels. The clip includes footage capturing the energy of PSY's 'Heumppeok Show' (his signature outdoor water concert). It also features a candid exchange in which Daniel Dae Kim asks how long PSY enjoyed the success of 'Gangnam Style,' to which PSY replies: 'As a singer, it lasts forever, but as a songwriter, it is both a dream and a nightmare. How can I ever make something better than this song?'

'K-Everything' featuring PSY will air its first episode on CNN International at 9 p.m. on May 9. The full 4-part series will be streamed live each week for CNN International paid TV subscribers via CNN.com, CNN Connected TV, and the mobile app. In South Korea, it can also be watched on Coupang Play, and globally, in available regions, through the HBO Max streaming service.
