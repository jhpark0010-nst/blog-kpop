BTS met with Mexican President Claudia Sheinbaum on May 6 (local time) ahead of their Mexico City concerts, following an official invitation from the presidential office, according to Big Hit Music. The meeting took place as the group prepares for three concert dates — May 7 and May 9–10 — at Estadio GNP Seguros in Mexico City.

The members spent approximately 40 minutes in conversation with President Sheinbaum. When BTS and the president stepped out together onto the balcony of the National Palace, the nearby Zócalo Plaza was packed with ARMY (the group's fandom name) and local citizens.

Fans held signs reading 'BTS will always be in the heart of Mexico' and 'Welcome to Mexico,' warmly welcoming the group. Some fans were moved to tears at the sight of the members in person.

Earlier that morning, President Sheinbaum had announced at her regular press conference that BTS would be visiting the presidential office and that the palace balcony would be opened for the group to greet citizens. She also spoke about BTS's music, saying it 'always conveys a message of friendship, peace, and love.'

BTS's visit to Mexico is also generating significant economic anticipation. The Mexico City Chamber of Commerce estimated that the concerts will produce an economic ripple effect of approximately $107.5 million USD (roughly 155.7 billion Korean won).

All three concert dates are completely sold out. BTS's fifth studio album ARIRANG and its title track SWIM both reached No. 1 on the Billboard 200 album chart and the Hot 100 singles chart in the United States.
