For YG Entertainment, 2026 is a year where three major milestones converge: the label's 30th anniversary, BIGBANG's 20th debut anniversary, and BLACKPINK's 10th anniversary. In response, YG is simultaneously pushing major artist comebacks and launching new acts, firing up the engines that will carry the label into its next chapter.

BIGBANG, celebrating their 20th debut anniversary, recently took the Coachella stage as a complete group, demonstrating that their impact has not faded despite their long hiatus. Their setlist — spanning mega-hit songs to individual member solo stages — was seen as more than a nostalgia trip, proving the enduring value of K-pop legends across 20 years of music.

In August, BIGBANG will hold the 'BIGBANG 2026 WORLD TOUR' with YG, further accelerating their group activities. This marks their first world tour in nine years since 'LAST DANCE' in 2017, and is expected to carry special significance for both the artists and their fans.

BLACKPINK, now in their 10th debut year, have once again cemented their status as global top artists. With their third mini album DEADLINE, they recorded first-week sales of 1.77 million copies — the highest ever for a K-pop girl group — and set a new record for the most chart entries by a K-pop female artist on both the US Billboard and UK Official Charts. Having recently wrapped up a large-scale tour, BLACKPINK is expected to continue generating unmatched synergy through both group and solo activities.

The next-generation groups set to carry YG through the coming decade are also making bold moves this year. BABYMONSTER will release their third mini album CHOOM on May 4 and simultaneously launch a world tour spanning five continents. TREASURE is also expected to open a new chapter in their group activities on June 1 with a new mini album made up entirely of hip-hop tracks.

Adding to this, new boy and girl groups expected to debut before the end of the year will help YG build an artist lineup that spans every generation. Chief producer Yang Hyun-suk has directly stated, 'The group will have five members, and we are planning to launch a new boy group in September,' signaling full support for the new act. All eyes are on what kind of positive impact YG's new decade — built on 30 years of experience — will have on the global music market.
