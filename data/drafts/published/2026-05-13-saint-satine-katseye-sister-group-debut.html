HYBE-Geffen Records announced the formation of four-member girl group SAINT SATINE on May 12, through episode 12 of 'World Scout: The Final Piece,' aired on Japanese OTT platform ABEMA. Emily, Lexi, and Samara — all alumni of the previous audition program 'The Debut: Dream Academy,' which produced KATSEYE — were joined by Sakura as the final member, completing the new team's puzzle. Sakura was called out after beating 14,000-to-1 odds in the last stage of the audition, where only one spot remained.

The final stage was intense from the start. Sakura took the stage alongside the three already-confirmed members to perform the new song 'PARTY b4 the PARTY.' When she perfectly executed a difficult split — a move she reportedly practiced hundreds of times — a brief gasp of awe erupted from the audience. Her confident expression and relaxed groove filled the stage. HxG Head Creator Son Seong-deuk praised her, saying, 'The moment Sakura stepped forward, I got chills,' and added, 'It seems she has reached a level where she can not only show star quality but truly unleash it.'

Ayana's performance of 'WE RIDE' was equally formidable. Rather than flashy changes, what stood out was the solid skill she had built up over time. Every move and every note was unwavering, and the way she commanded the stage was impressive. HxG CEO Mitra Darab commented, 'She conveyed her swag to the audience well, and it was great to see her enjoying the stage.'

After both performances, the judges deliberated deeply. Sakura had proven explosive growth and star power; Ayana had shown polished, stable performance. The choice was not easy, and team chemistry mattered just as much as individual ability. Son Seong-deuk said, 'I have worked on many audition programs, but this is the first time the judges' opinions clashed this intensely.'

Sakura's name was finally called. Cheers erupted from the audience, and Sakura burst into tears, covering her face with both hands. 'I wanted to become an artist who shines on the world stage. I am so grateful my dream is coming true,' she said, her voice trembling but clear. Ayana was composed. 'I still don't want to give up on this dream. I will work even harder going forward,' she said. Before leaving the stage, she gently patted Sakura's head and said 'Keep going' — a brief word that left a lasting impression. The way they cheered each other on at the end of their competition was more moving than any other moment in the roughly three-month journey.

KATSEYE, the senior group, was also present at the event. Having debuted through the same HYBE-Geffen Records T&D (Training & Development) system and audition process rooted in HYBE Chairman Bang Si-hyuk's 'K-pop methodology,' KATSEYE advised their juniors: 'If you think too much, you can't enjoy the stage.' That advice was visible throughout both finalists' last performances. At the end of the broadcast, the group name for HYBE-Geffen Records' second girl group — following KATSEYE — was revealed as SAINT SATINE. Creator Son Seong-deuk explained: ''Saint' represents intense charisma and musical presence, while 'Satine' conveys a soft, elegant, and refined image.' The four members — Emily (USA), Lexi (Sweden), Sakura (Japan), and Samara (Brazil) — took the stage together once more, shouted 'The puzzle is complete,' and clasped each other's hands tightly. They are now aiming for a debut in the second half of this year as they accelerate toward the global stage.
