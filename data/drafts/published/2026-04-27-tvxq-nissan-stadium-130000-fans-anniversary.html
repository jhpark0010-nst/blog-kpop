TVXQ brought their 20th Japan debut anniversary to a spectacular close, drawing a total of 130,000 fans over two days at Yokohama Nissan Stadium on April 25 and 26, 2026. The concert, titled '東方神起 20th Anniversary LIVE IN NISSAN STADIUM ~RED OCEAN~,' turned the entire stadium a vivid red as fans filled the venue with the group's signature color.

Notably, TVXQ first performed at Nissan Stadium in 2013 as the first overseas artist to do so, then made history again in 2018 by becoming the first act to hold a three-day run at the venue in Japanese concert history. This third appearance at Nissan Stadium has now earned them the record of being the 'first and most frequent' overseas artist to perform there, cementing their unrivaled standing in Japan.

With this achievement, TVXQ — who set their own record in 2025 for the most Tokyo Dome and nationwide dome performances by an overseas artist — now also hold the 'first and most frequent' Nissan Stadium record as of 2026. Now in their 24th year since debut, they continue to make unprecedented history at Japan's most iconic venues, proving they remain a fully active, record-breaking act.

Over the course of approximately three and a half hours, TVXQ performed a total of 31 songs, including hit tracks such as 'Why? (Keep Your Head Down),' '"O"-正.反.合.,' 'Proud,' 'Survivor,' 'MAXIMUM,' and 'Reboot.' They also performed 'IDENTITY,' the OST from their 20th anniversary commemorative film, and 'Share The World -RED OCEAN Ver.-,' which they sang while riding carts shaped like the Going Merry and Thousand Sunny ships from the popular Japanese anime One Piece.

The production featured a sliding deck that expanded the vast stadium into a single unified stage, with lighting, lifts, and pyrotechnic effects enhancing the immersive experience. During 'Somebody To Love,' both members traversed the entire venue against a backdrop of dazzling fireworks, bringing the atmosphere to its peak. Fans stood throughout the show, waving T-shaped red light sticks in time with the music and chanting 'T!' in response to the group's 'We are' call. TVXQ connected with the audience through outstanding stage presence and fluent Japanese, delivering a polished concert that captured the full meaning of their 20th anniversary.

At the close of the show, TVXQ said, 'This was the best time of 2026. This third Nissan Stadium was something we achieved together with everyone here.' They added, 'It feels like a dream to be able to stand at Nissan Stadium again. If we can make you happy through our music, we will keep finding the strength to sing going forward.'
