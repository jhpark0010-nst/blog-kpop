ZEROBASEONE will give fans a first look at a new song ahead of their upcoming comeback. The group — consisting of members Sung Hanbin, Kim Jiwoong, Seok Matthew, Kim Taerae, and Park Gunwook — is set to perform their new track 'Customize' for the very first time at KCON JAPAN 2026, held on May 10 at Makuhari Messe in Chiba, Japan.

'Customize' is a track from ZEROBASEONE's sixth mini album 'Ascend-', due out on May 18. It is also the first self-composed song by member Park Gunwook. Built on a pop-rock melody, the song honestly captures the ironic state of losing oneself the deeper one becomes immersed in emotion.

ZEROBASEONE plans to raise anticipation for their comeback to its peak by showcasing the new song at KCON JAPAN 2026. The group is expected to bring the track's dramatic narrative and deep emotional arc to life through their stage performance, drawing an enthusiastic response from fans.

During a live broadcast on the afternoon of May 4, Park Gunwook shared, 'From the start, I wrote this song thinking it would be amazing if the five of us performed it together. It's a song only ZEROBASEONE can do.' He added, 'I also directed in the recording studio, and the older members all did so well. I'm curious about the reaction when it comes out — I want to let everyone hear it soon. You'll be able to meet it before long.'

The new album 'Ascend-' contains a total of 7 tracks: the title track 'TOP 5', 'Customize', 'Intro.', 'V for Vision', 'Exotic', 'Changes', and 'Zero to Hundred'. ZEROBASEONE aims to weave their strengthened identity across all seven tracks, building their distinct color and direction even more clearly, and proving their expanded musical spectrum and limitless potential.

ZEROBASEONE's 6th mini album 'Ascend-' will be released at 6 PM on May 18 across major online music platforms.
