TOMORROW X TOGETHER (TXT) has successfully pulled off their first comeback since re-signing with their label, capturing both the U.S. Billboard charts and domestic buzz. According to a chart preview article posted on Billboard's official website on April 27, the group's 8th mini album '7TH YEAR: 가시덤불에 잠시 바람이 멈췄을 때' debuted at No. 3 on the Billboard 200 (dated May 2). The album recorded 67,000 copies in pure sales and 2,000 SEA units (sales equivalent from streaming). With this entry, TXT has now placed a total of 13 releases on the Billboard 200 — 10 Korean albums and 3 Japanese albums.

The new album is a candid expression of the emotions and thoughts the five members — Yeonjun, Soobin, Beomgyu, Taehyun, and Huening Kai — have experienced over the seven years since their debut. Universal feelings such as anxiety and emptiness are woven together with experimental sounds, earning praise from listeners around the world.

The title track 'Stick With You' (하루에 하루만 더) achieved a 'grand slam' by winning five consecutive music show (music program) trophies. Starting with MBC M and MBC every1's 'Show! Champion,' the group went on to claim wins on Mnet's 'M Countdown,' KBS2's 'Music Bank,' MBC's 'Show! Music Core,' and SBS's 'Inkigayo ON THE GO.' When the fifth win was confirmed on the April 26 broadcast of 'Inkigayo ON THE GO,' all five members erupted in celebration. They shared: 'We are grateful to MOA (the fandom name) for giving us five awards through this promotions cycle. It was a meaningful and rewarding time. TOMORROW X TOGETHER is just getting started. Please keep watching us and give us your love.'

Throughout their promotional period, TXT drew attention from fans and the general public alike with their passionate, rookie-like energy and striking visuals. Their commanding stage performances added further momentum to the album's popularity. On music shows, the group delivered polished performances befitting an eighth-year group. Their tutting choreography — utilizing the joints of the hands and arms — was noted for intuitively conveying the song's emotions, while the synchronized group formations and movement design were praised for heightening immersion. The members' expressive facial acting further amplified the song's appeal. They also delivered steady, pitch-perfect live vocals at every encore stage, and fancam footage spread rapidly online immediately after each broadcast, driving widespread buzz.

Meanwhile, TXT continues to stay active across various programs. They are set to show a new side of themselves through 'TXT's Parenting Diary,' premiering on the streaming platform Wavve on May 1. They will also appear in YouTube content including 'Jjan Han Hyung Shin Dong-yeop' on May 4 and 'Leesulive' on May 15.
