Park Jin-young, along with all members of TWICE and Stray Kids, have earned the right to participate in the Grammy Awards selection process. JYP Entertainment announced on July 14 that all 18 of these artists have been named voting members of the Recording Academy, the organization that hosts the Grammy Awards.

Voting membership is a credential open to creators working in the music industry — including artists, songwriters, producers, and engineers. Voting members have the authority to participate in ballots that determine Grammy Award nominees and winners.

JYP Entertainment CEO Jung Wook and Chief Strategy Officer Shin Hyun-kook, who also serves as CEO of JYP America, were named Professional Members of the Recording Academy. Professional membership is aimed at music business professionals who support music creators. Professional members do not have Grammy voting rights but may participate in the submission process.

The 69th Grammy Awards will be held in Los Angeles, USA on February 7 (local time) next year.
