All five lawyers representing ADOR in the approximately 43 billion KRW (roughly $31 million USD) damages lawsuit against former NewJeans member Danielle and former ADOR CEO Min Hee-jin have resigned, it was revealed on April 29.

According to legal sources, the five attorneys from Kim & Chang law firm who had been handling ADOR's legal representation submitted their resignations to the court on April 24. The resignations came immediately after the conclusion of the case's first preliminary hearing session.

The two sides had clashed sharply over the pace of the trial at that first preliminary hearing, held on March 26 before Civil Division 31 of the Seoul Central District Court (presiding judge Nam In-su).

ADOR's side requested an approximately two-month extension, asking to wait for the outcome of the ongoing put-option lawsuit between HYBE and former CEO Min before proceeding with the trial.

Danielle's side pushed back, arguing that ADOR was intentionally delaying the litigation. Danielle's representatives emphasized that a prolonged lawsuit would cause serious harm to her entertainment career and demanded a swift trial.

The court rejected ADOR's request for an extension and confirmed May 14 and July 2 as the next hearing dates, signaling its intent to proceed quickly. ADOR had filed the 43 billion KRW claim for penalty fees and damages against Danielle and Min Hee-jin after terminating Danielle's exclusive contract in December of last year, citing responsibility for NewJeans' departure and delayed return. With the entire Kim & Chang team now withdrawn, attention turns to what new legal strategy ADOR will build going forward.
