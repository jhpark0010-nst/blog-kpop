BTS leader RM (real name Kim Nam-jun) has been appointed as the global ambassador for the National Museum of Korea, the museum announced on June 20, 2026. Through this role, RM will work alongside the museum to promote Korean cultural heritage to audiences around the world.

RM has long shown a distinctive interest in traditional Korean culture and fine art, and has used his influence to support related causes. In 2021 and 2022, he donated 100 million KRW each year — totaling 200 million KRW — to the Overseas Korean Cultural Heritage Foundation under the Cultural Heritage Administration, with the funds designated for the preservation, restoration, and utilization of Korean cultural heritage located abroad.

RM also maintains a personal art collection, and is scheduled to hold a special exhibition of his private collection at the San Francisco Museum of Modern Art (SFMOMA) in October of this year. This marks the first time the National Museum of Korea has ever appointed a global ambassador.

Going forward, RM will participate in various promotional activities for the National Museum of Korea and contribute to introducing Korean history and traditional culture to the world. On June 19, the day after his appointment, RM toured the 'Danwon Kim Hong-do: Painting an Era' exhibition on the second floor of the permanent gallery's calligraphy and painting hall alongside museum director Yoo Hong-jun.

The museum presented RM with a special cultural product — a reduced-scale reproduction of the Daedongyeojido, a hand-scroll edition considered the definitive map of the Joseon Dynasty. RM shared his thoughts on the appointment, saying, 'I am very pleased,' and adding, 'I will do my part so that the beauty and value of our cultural heritage can reach more people.'

Director Yoo Hong-jun said, 'It is meaningful to be able to share the beauty of our cultural heritage with the world together with RM,' and expressed, 'I expect him to play a great role in establishing the National Museum of Korea as a museum loved by people around the world.'
