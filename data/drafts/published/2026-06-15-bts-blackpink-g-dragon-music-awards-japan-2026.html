BTS and BLACKPINK were among the K-pop acts recognized at Music Awards Japan 2026 (MUSIC AWARDS JAPAN 2026), according to the awards ceremony's official website on June 15.

BTS took home the 'Best K-pop Artist' award, while BLACKPINK's 'JUMP' was named 'Best K-pop Song in Japan.' BTS member Jin's solo track 'Don't Say You Love Me' won the 'Best of Listeners' Choice: International Song Powered by Spotify' award.

Music Awards Japan is a ceremony created through the cooperation of five major local industry organizations, including the Recording Industry Association of Japan, along with government agencies. Votes from approximately 5,000 industry professionals are factored into the selections.

The original soundtrack 'Golden' from the globally popular Netflix anime 'K-pop Demon Hunters' was selected as 'Best Song Asia.' A collaboration track '2' by J-pop star Hoshino Gen and Lee Youngji was named 'Best Cross-Border Collaboration Song.' G-Dragon's 'POWER' received the 'Korean Popular Music Special Award.'

Meanwhile, J-pop star Fujii Kaze swept three awards — 'Album of the Year,' 'Best R&B/Contemporary Song,' and 'Best R&B/Contemporary Artist' — with his third album 'Prema,' which was fully produced by Korean DJ and music producer 250.

Fujii Kaze worked on all the tracks together with 250 at a studio located inside 'House of Refuse,' a multi-cultural complex on Jeju Island, last year.
