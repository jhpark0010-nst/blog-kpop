CORTIS has surpassed 700 million cumulative streams on Spotify, the world's largest music platform, their label Big Hit Music announced on May 30. The milestone covers a total of 13 tracks, including songs from their two mini-albums and 'Mention ME,' the soundtrack from the animated film 'GOAT.'

Big Hit Music noted that CORTIS reached 700 million cumulative streams at the fastest pace ever among K-pop boy groups, achieving the milestone just 10 months after their debut.

CORTIS's second mini-album 'GREENGREEN' and its title track 'REDRED' generated strong responses on TV music programs and university festivals, contributing to the group's commercial success.

The group is also set to release the music video for 'Blue Lips,' a track from their second mini-album, at midnight on May 31.

Meanwhile, BOYNEXTDOOR marked their third debut anniversary on May 30, as announced by their label KOZ Entertainment. The group shared their feelings, saying, 'The memory of stepping on stage for the first time is still vivid, so it feels surreal that it's already been three years since our debut.' They added, 'Looking back on the path we've walked, we feel a surge of passion to keep running forward.'

BOYNEXTDOOR has been holding a debut anniversary event called 'Door Janchee' since May 23. The event name combines 'DOOR' from the group's name and 'Janchee,' the Korean word for celebration. The group debuted in May 2023 with the single 'WHO!' and has since released hit songs including 'Today I LOVE YOU.' They are set to make a comeback on June 8 with their first full-length album 'HOME.'
