aespa has entered the UK Official Singles Chart Top 100 for the first time since their debut six years ago, with 'Lemonade,' the title track of their second studio album. According to the Official Charts on June 5 (local time), 'Lemonade' debuted at No. 95 on the singles chart this week.

'Lemonade' is the title track of the album of the same name and is an electronic dance track characterized by a powerful synthesizer bass sound.

Also on the same singles chart, 'Golden,' the original soundtrack from the Netflix film 'K-Pop Demon Hunters,' charted at No. 45 for its 50th consecutive week. KATSEYE, HYBE's Korean-American joint girl group, landed at No. 53 with 'Pinky Up' for their 8th consecutive week on the chart.

On the Official Albums Chart Top 100, BTS's fifth album 'ARIRANG' climbed five spots from the previous week to No. 33, marking its 11th consecutive week on the chart. 'ARIRANG' also recorded its 8th No. 1 on Spotify's Weekly Top Albums Global, making it the most No. 1 album of the year so far.

Meanwhile, Paul McCartney's new album 'THE BOYS OF DUNGEON LANE' debuted straight at No. 1 on the albums chart. 'Pop King' Michael Jackson continued the wave of popularity following his biopic film 'Michael,' placing multiple singles and albums on the charts this week. On the singles chart, 'Billie Jean' ranked No. 5, 'Beat It' at No. 9, 'Human Nature' at No. 12, and The Jackson 5's 'I Want You Back' at No. 33.

On the albums chart, Jackson's 'The Essential' came in at No. 2, 'Thriller' at No. 6, 'Bad' at No. 10, 'Off The Wall' at No. 62, and 'Dangerous' at No. 100.
