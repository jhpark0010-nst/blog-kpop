BTS has received a special award from El Paso County, Texas, which the group will visit as part of their world tour 'ARIRANG.' The El Paso County Commissioners Court announced on April 27 (local time) that it had approved a resolution to present BTS with the 'Estimado Amigo' (meaning 'Cherished Friend') award and to declare May 2–3, the dates of their concerts, as 'El Paso BTS Weekend.'

El Paso County recognized BTS's contribution to boosting local tourism and fostering community pride. The county also highly praised the social messages and public value embedded in BTS's music.

Brooke Underwood, Executive Director of the El Paso tourism authority, told local media that 'the BTS concerts are expected to generate $75 million (approximately 110.5 billion Korean won) in economic impact for the local economy over the two days.'

Local ARMY (the group's fandom name) in El Paso also launched a fundraising campaign to light up the city's landmark 'Star on the Mountain' in purple to welcome BTS.

BTS will hold solo concerts at Sun Bowl Stadium in El Paso, Texas on May 2–3, marking the first time a Korean artist has performed a standalone concert at the venue.
