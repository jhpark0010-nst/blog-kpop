Music platform Melon is joining forces with leading music platforms from Korea, China, and Japan to introduce a new standard that comprehensively showcases K-pop's global influence. On May 28, Kakao Entertainment announced the scoring criteria for the 'Global-K Chart,' which Melon, China's Tencent Music Entertainment, and Japan's Line Music will jointly launch in June. The key feature of the criteria is that it combines not only music streaming figures but also fandom activity indicators.

The Global-K Chart is an artist chart that displays K-pop artists' global influence at a glance, based on usage data from the three major music partners — Melon (Korea), Tencent Music (China), and Line Music (Japan). Under the slogan 'The Global K-POP Standard,' the chart captures the fan passion of Korea, China, and Japan — key hubs of K-pop — representing a combined population of 1.6 billion people, or approximately 20% of the world.

Melon stated, 'The Global-K Chart goes beyond simply tallying the artists whose music is listened to the most, and instead captures the various traces of fans reacting to and actually taking action around artists' activities.' Melon added, 'Since the genuine fan passion of fans in Korea, China, and Japan will be directly reflected in the chart, we hope it will serve as a meaningful stepping stone for K-pop artists to communicate more deeply and expand more broadly with their global fandoms.'

The Global-K Chart will consist of a global chart and platform-specific detailed charts. Scores are calculated by combining each platform's music streaming usage and various fandom activity indicators within the platform, based on the previous 24 hours of usage data.
