Stray Kids will launch their new world tour, 'RUN IT,' in Seoul next month, their agency JYP Entertainment announced on June 23. The group will open the tour with a total of five performances at KSPO Dome (Olympic Gymnastics Arena) in Seoul Olympic Park on July 25, 26, and 29, and August 1 and 2.

Following the Seoul dates, the members will head to Tokyo National Stadium in August, then Nagoya and Osaka in September, Fukuoka in October, Hong Kong and Taipei in December, Bangkok in January next year, and Singapore in March. Additional venues are set to be announced at a later date.

JYP Entertainment noted that through this world tour, Stray Kids have become the first overseas male artists to hold a solo concert at Tokyo National Stadium, one of Japan's largest concert venues. The group's seven Japan concerts alone are expected to draw approximately 370,000 fans.

Previously, the group successfully completed their largest-ever world tour, 'dominATE,' spanning 56 shows across 35 regions worldwide last year.

Stray Kids will also release a new mini-album, 'THIS & THAT,' on August 7. Ahead of that, the album track 'RUN IT' will be pre-released on June 24 at 1:00 p.m. On September 11 (local time), the group will appear at the Brazilian music festival Rock in Rio and at their own newly launched music festival, 'STRAYCITY,' meeting global fans at both events.

Earlier this month, on June 6, Stray Kids performed as headliners at The Governors Ball music festival in New York. In front of 45,000 fans, the members performed signature songs including 'DOMINO,' 'Chk Chk Boom,' and 'CEREMONY.'
