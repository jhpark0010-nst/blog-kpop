BTS won Artist of the Year — the top prize at the American Music Awards (AMAs) — for the second time in their career on May 25 (local time) at the MGM Grand Garden Arena in Las Vegas. The group beat out formidable nominees including Bad Bunny, Bruno Mars, Justin Bieber, Lady Gaga, and Taylor Swift to claim the trophy.

BTS took home a total of three awards on the night: Artist of the Year, Song of the Summer, and Best Male K-pop Artist. The AMAs is considered one of America's three major music awards shows alongside the Grammy Awards and the Billboard Music Awards, with winners determined by fan voting.

BTS first won Artist of the Year in November 2021 with their English-language hit 'Butter,' becoming the first Asian act to receive the honor. Having released their fifth studio album 'ARIRANG' in March following their military service hiatus, the group claimed their second top prize at a major U.S. music awards show just two months after the album's release.

Leader RM said, 'ARMY did it once again,' and added, 'We are grateful for the honor of receiving this precious award one more time after all members have completed their military service.' He continued, 'We always send our greatest thanks to ARMY around the world who have been with us for the past 13 years. Thank you so much.' Jimin said in English, 'Thank you for joining us on the concert tour and for giving us love in every city,' then added in Korean, 'ARMY, who always cheer and love us, I am truly grateful and I love you,' bowing deeply at 90 degrees.

HYBE's Korean-American joint girl group KATSEYE also took home three awards: New Artist of the Year, Breakthrough Pop Artist, and Best Music Video. They also performed their song 'PINKY UP' on stage. Member Sofia said, 'I want to give a special thank you tonight to BTS for inspiring us to showcase our culture on a global scale.' Korean member Yoonchaé said in Korean, 'I am so grateful to share this moment with the members,' then added in English, 'We will continue to express our culture with pride.'

The original soundtrack 'Golden' from the Netflix animated film 'K-pop Demon Hunters' (referred to as 'Kedeheon' / K-pop Demon Hunters) won Song of the Year, Best Vocal Performance, and Best Pop Song, while 'K-pop Demon Hunters' itself won Best Soundtrack — bringing the film's total to four trophies. Korean-American singer EJAE and Ray Amiga, who performed as the fictional girl group Huntrix in the film, attended the ceremony to accept the awards. Another Huntrix vocalist, Audrey Nuna, was not present. EJAE said, 'This song and film gained great strength thanks to the fans,' and added, 'The portal is closed. Thank you to the fans and the K-pop Demon Hunters team.' Best Female K-pop Artist went to girl group TWICE.
