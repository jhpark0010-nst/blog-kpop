BTS lit up Tampa, Florida with 190,000 fans singing along to 'Arirang' as the group launched their BTS WORLD TOUR 'ARIRANG' IN NORTH AMERICA at Raymond James Stadium on April 25–26 and 28 (local time). It was their first U.S. concert in approximately four years, and all three shows sold out well in advance.

The massive stadium was already buzzing with energy before the show began, as fans practiced wave cheers while waiting for the opening. The setlist kicked off with 'Hooligan' from the new album ARIRANG, followed by 'MIC Drop', 'FAKE LOVE', and 'NORMAL', drawing explosive cheers and mass sing-alongs from the crowd. 'Body to Body' also made a strong impression in Tampa — when the Korean folk song 'Arirang' melody embedded in the track played, the global audience sang along in unison in a symbolic moment.

The 'random song' segment — where a different track is performed at each show — also drew an enthusiastic response. Members selected songs on the spot based on audience requests and the atmosphere of the night. In Tampa, they performed 'Boy With Luv (Feat. Halsey)' and 'Pied Piper', among others. When the intro to 'Pied Piper', originally released in 2017, began — a song many fans in the crowd had requested — the venue erupted, and the members responded with a surprised and spirited performance.

BTS moved across a 360-degree stage, making eye contact with fans throughout the venue. Near the end of the show, Jin shared: 'I felt it during my solo Run Jin tour too, but Tampa is truly the best. I actively recommended to the members that we had to come here with that feeling in mind. Having done this concert, I have no regrets about that decision.' The members also said: 'We were a little worried since this was the first show of the North America tour, but thanks to all of you, those worries completely disappeared. You made the start of the North America tour the best it could be, and now we're even more excited for the shows ahead.' They continued: 'We really love you and missed you so much. I realized that this very moment is the reason I have lived until now,' and 'Tampa changed my tempo.'

Following the successful Tampa run, BTS will hold concerts at Sun Bowl Stadium in El Paso on May 2–3, becoming the first Korean artists to perform there. The North America tour spans 31 shows across 12 cities including Mexico City, Stanford, and Las Vegas, with every date sold out.

Outside the concert venues, BTS is also expanding their connection with the public in new ways. Since the 3rd, they have been releasing a short-form mini-documentary series titled 'KEEP SWIMMING with BTS' on their YouTube channel. The series features notable figures whose stories align with the message of the new album's title track 'SWIM' — 'keep moving forward.' Following director Park Chan-wook, Nora Noh, and monk Seonjae, the April 28 episode featured Maggie Kang, director of the Netflix original animation 'K-Pop Demon Hunters', who shared her attitude of pushing past fear and continuing to move forward.
