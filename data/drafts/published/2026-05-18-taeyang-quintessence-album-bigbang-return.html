BIGBANG's Taeyang released his 4th solo studio album 'QUINTESSENCE' on May 18, 2026 — his first full-length record in nine years. At a listening event held that afternoon at Cube Convention Center in Mapo-gu, Seoul, Taeyang shared his feelings ahead of the release: 'Today happens to be my birthday. I'm happy to think I can give fans a good gift.'

Taeyang reflected on the busy lead-up to the album. 'I've been hectic. I was finishing the solo album while preparing for the Coachella stage with the BIGBANG members, and I don't think I've had a single day off since Coachella,' he said. He added that 2026 marks his 20th debut anniversary, describing 'QUINTESSENCE' as 'an album focused on what is most like me and what is new at the same time.'

The album title itself reflects that philosophy. 'I drew a lot of inspiration from the word quintessence,' Taeyang explained. 'I researched and thought deeply about the process of finding one's essence — what the quintessence is, and how to capture it in my music.'

The lead single 'LIVE FAST DIE SLOW' is a song about respecting and holding firm to one's own pace amid a rapidly changing world. Taeyang described it as 'the fastest track I've ever released' and said the will to 'live at my own speed and direction' is at its core. He also noted that unlike previous albums — where he would write many tracks and select the best as the title song — this time he made every track feel like a potential title song, making the final choice especially difficult.

The 10-track album includes 'BAD,' 'MOVIE,' and 'YES,' among others. Taeyang participated in writing lyrics for 9 of the 10 songs. Featured artists include Australian pop star The Kid LAROI, Epik High's Tablo, and Allday Project's Tarzan and Uchan. Taeyang noted that Tablo contributed lyrics to three songs, and praised Allday Project, saying their energy reminded him of BIGBANG's early debut days.

On his 20th anniversary, Taeyang was quick to credit his fans. 'The strength to keep performing for 20 years comes from the fans who cheer for my stages,' he said. 'I felt that at Coachella too. Without that, it would have been impossible — looking at where things stand now, I think it's a miracle.' He closed by saying, 'Starting with this solo album, I plan to return in various forms with the BIGBANG members afterward, so I hope you'll wait for us.'
