Major Korean entertainment agencies have declared a zero-tolerance stance against malicious rumors and digital sex crimes targeting their idol groups, launching a full-scale legal offensive. On June 29, HYBE subsidiary labels Source Music, ADOR, and Belift Lab issued official statements announcing that they are mobilizing all available civil and criminal legal measures to protect their artists LE SSERAFIM, NewJeans, and ILLIT.

The three labels stated that they operate their own internal monitoring systems and are continuing legal action against malicious posts based on tips received, having already secured outcomes including suspended indictments and fines against individuals on major online platforms.

The agencies have taken a particularly uncompromising stance on deepfake crimes targeting their artists. Source Music submitted a statement to the court expressing its intent to seek the harshest possible punishment with no settlement. ADOR confirmed that a defendant who was sentenced to three years in prison last year recently filed an appeal, but the appellate court rejected the appeal in full. Belift Lab also stated that it has identified the suspect in a deepfake case and that proceedings are underway, warning that even those who use anonymous platforms such as Telegram or overseas services will be tracked down and apprehended through cooperative investigations.

Belift Lab went further, announcing that it has detected plots in secret channels to physically harm its artists, and that it will take strong legal action against any such threats. The labels have also won a series of damages lawsuits against malicious YouTubers, holding them financially accountable.

Earlier, on June 18, SM Entertainment also announced it is fighting back after securing a prison sentence against a perpetrator who violated the human rights of aespa members Karina and Winter. SM stated that a defendant who produced deepfake videos of its artists and sold them for profit was found guilty and sentenced to two years and six months in prison. The court also ordered the defendant to complete 80 hours of a sexual violence treatment program and imposed a seven-year ban on employment at institutions related to children and youth.

SM added that it has collected thousands of pieces of evidence of malicious posts and comments across online communities and social media platforms through fan tips and its own monitoring, and that it has identified multiple suspects who are currently under investigation. The agency reaffirmed that it will maintain a hardline response with no leniency or settlement for deepfake production and distribution, as well as for the creation of malicious rumors and sexually harassing content, through constant surveillance of major domestic and international platforms. The agencies emphasized that even deleted posts can be traced through their evidence collection systems, and urged fans to actively submit tips.
