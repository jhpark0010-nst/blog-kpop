South Korean prosecutors have returned an arrest warrant for HYBE Chairman Bang Si-hyuk on April 24, 2026. The Seoul Southern District Prosecutors' Office Joint Investigation Unit for Financial and Securities Crimes sent the warrant back to police, who had sought it on charges of fraudulent unfair trading under the Capital Markets Act.

Prosecutors stated, 'We determined that, at the current stage, there is insufficient substantiation regarding the grounds requiring detention, and have requested supplementary investigation.'

Bang Si-hyuk is accused of deceiving HYBE investors in 2019 by telling them there were no plans to list the company on the stock exchange, thereby inducing them to sell their shares to a private equity fund connected to him, after which HYBE proceeded with its IPO.

Police believe that under a private agreement with the fund, Bang received approximately 30% of the post-listing sale profits — around 190 billion won — along with other gains, totaling an estimated 260 billion won in illicit profits.

The Capital Markets Act prohibits obtaining financial gain through deception or using fraudulent schemes in connection with financial investment products, including unlisted shares. Violations resulting in profits of 5 billion won or more are punishable by life imprisonment or a prison term of no less than five years.
