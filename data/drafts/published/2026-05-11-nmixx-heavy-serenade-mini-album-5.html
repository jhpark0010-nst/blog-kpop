NMIXX released their 5th mini album 'Heavy Serenade' on May 11 at 6 PM KST, according to their agency JYP Entertainment. The album marks another milestone for the group as they continue to build their discography.

The title track 'Heavy Serenade' is a song expressing deep, confident love. The lyrics were written by Han Roro, a popular singer-songwriter regarded as a representative voice of the MZ generation.

The new album includes a total of six tracks: 'Heavy Serenade', 'Crescendo', 'LOUD', and more. Member Haewon shared her thoughts, saying, 'I worked hard to create a more polished result while embracing a positive sense of pressure.'

In other news, SM Entertainment announced that SHINee will release their 6th mini album 'Atmos' on June 1. This marks the group's first new music in approximately one year since May of last year. The album features a total of six tracks, including the title song 'Atmos'.

SHINee will also hold a full-group standalone concert over three days — May 29 to 31 — at KSPO Dome in Olympic Park, Songpa-gu, Seoul. Member Key will make his return through this concert, approximately five months after he suspended activities amid the so-called 'Jusaimoh' controversy.

Meanwhile, singer Lee Chan-won held his Seoul encore concert 'Changa: A Brilliant Day' at KSPO Dome on May 9 and 10, as announced by his agency Daebak Planning on May 11. Over the two days, Lee Chan-won performed tracks including songs from his second full-length album 'Chanran', released in October last year, delivering a diverse set spanning approximately 210 minutes. He closed the show with a performance of 'Rock and Roll Life', saying, 'I am so grateful to the audience who filled the venue all the way to the end.'
