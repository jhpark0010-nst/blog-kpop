BTS is bringing their large-scale city event 'The City Arirang' to two new locations: Las Vegas from May 20 to 31 (local time) and Busan from June 5 to 21. Big Hit Music announced the expansion on April 20, confirming the events will take place around BTS's 'ARIRANG' world tour concert dates in each city.

'The City Arirang' is a city-wide cultural project that projects BTS's story onto urban spaces, blending music and media art into immersive installation experiences. The concept transforms an entire city into a massive cultural hub, using landmarks and public spaces as its canvas. The event originally launched in Seoul last month to celebrate the release of BTS's fifth full-length studio album, 'ARIRANG.'

In Las Vegas, the event will make full use of the city's iconic Strip district — the famous tourist and commercial corridor. Through partnerships with major local brands, fans can expect immersive experiences throughout the area. One of the most striking visual elements will be the illumination of Las Vegas landmarks in red, the key color of the new album, turning the Strip into a vivid extension of the 'ARIRANG' world.

The Busan leg of 'The City Arirang' runs from June 5 to 21 and promises a diverse lineup of programs for fans. As with the Las Vegas edition, the Busan events are scheduled around BTS's world tour concerts in the city, giving fans an extended cultural experience beyond the concert halls themselves.

Together, the Las Vegas and Busan editions of 'The City Arirang' mark a significant expansion of BTS's comeback celebrations beyond Seoul. The project reflects Big Hit Music's ambition to turn each tour city into a living, breathing part of the BTS 'ARIRANG' universe, offering ARMY around the world a chance to engage with the album in a deeply immersive, place-specific way.
