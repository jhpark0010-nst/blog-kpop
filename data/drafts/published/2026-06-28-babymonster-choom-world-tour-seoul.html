BABYMONSTER opened their second world tour, 'CHOOM,' with three consecutive concerts at Seoul's Jamsil Indoor Stadium from June 26 to 28, 2026. A massive stage structure evoking an abandoned factory filled the arena on the evening of the 28th, with rough-textured pipelines running across the venue and red lighting that blended with the six members' performances, generating heat that rivaled the sweltering 30-degree summer weather outside.

Member Rora described the album's concept: 'CHOOM contains BABYMONSTER's strong ambition to turn the entire world into one big dance floor. In the title track CHOOM, we tried to show off a spectacular performance worthy of the song's name. Wasn't it amazing?'

The six members took the stage to the heavy sound of a live band reminiscent of a rock concert and opened with 'WE GO UP.' They then delivered a rapid-fire sequence of their new song 'CHOOM,' the first full-album title track 'DRIP,' and the debut mini-album title track 'SHEESH,' steadily building the crowd's energy. Fans responded at every hook with chants of 'CHOOM! CHOOM! CHOOM!' or 'DRIP! DRIP! DRIP!' Member Ahyeon drew particular attention by fluidly switching between rapid-fire rap and high-note shouts. Fire pillars, confetti, and thundering band performances added to the excitement.

BABYMONSTER officially debuted in April 2024 with their first mini-album 'BABYMONS7ER' and have been active with songs including 'SHEESH,' 'WE GO UP,' and 'DRIP.' As the first group YG Entertainment has released since BLACKPINK eight years ago, they have steadily built their fanbase through powerful live vocals and sharp performances. According to YG, the members actively participated in song selection, arrangement, and overall production for this tour — their first world tour in one year and five months.

After performing four songs in a row, the members paused to greet the audience. BABYMONSTER said, 'If our first world tour was a chance to show our potential, this tour is a performance where we connect more deeply with fans as a more grown group and enjoy it together,' adding, 'We will create the best unforgettable moments for our Monsteez and all the audience.' Ahyeon said, 'I missed you all so much. How have you been? Let's make a lot of fun memories together today. I love you.' Rora said, 'Since today is the last day of the Seoul concerts, can you give us even more energy than the first and second days? We trust you and will give everything we have.'

The concert also featured solo stages showcasing each member's individual color, including 'Temple' by Asa, 'Super Bass' by Pharita, and 'Problem' by Ahyeon. Rora chose Camila Cabello's 'Havana' — a song she once performed during a trainee evaluation, forgetting the lyrics partway through and crying in front of chief producer Yang Hyun-suk. Rora said, 'I think Monsteez enjoyed the solo stage since I gave it my all. Making eye contact with each Monsteez one by one while singing moved me deeply.' In the latter half of the show, BABYMONSTER performed 'FOREVER,' 'HOT SAUCE,' and their latest release 'SUGAR HONEY ICE TEA,' before closing with encores including 'SUPA DUPA LUV' and 'SHEESH.' The group will continue the tour across 18 cities and 29 shows spanning Oceania, Europe, and South America. Ahyeon said, 'It feels special to start the world tour in Seoul. We will carry the support Monsteez have given us and repay you with great stages in every city we visit.'
