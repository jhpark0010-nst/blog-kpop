TREASURE released their 4th mini album 'NEW WAV' at 6 PM KST on June 1, YG Entertainment announced. This marks the group's first new album in nine months, following their 3rd mini album 'LOVE PULSE' released in September of last year.

The new album contains four tracks, including the title song 'IF I' and 'ZOOM ZOOM'. Notably, it is the first time since their debut that TREASURE has filled an entire album exclusively with hip-hop music.

Through their agency, TREASURE's members said, 'This is an album filled with music that can be called the roots of YG and the team.' They added, 'We will show you a TREASURE that is clearly different from anything we have done before.'

In other news, TWICE member Dahyun made a donation of 30 million won to support leukemia patients in Ulsan on the occasion of her birthday. According to JYP Entertainment, Dahyun delivered the funds to the Ulsan Community Chest of Korea on May 28, her birthday.

Dahyun shared, 'On my birthday, I wanted to share the love I have received.' She also said, 'I hope my feelings can be even a small source of strength and comfort for those receiving treatment.'

Meanwhile, 24-member group tripleS released Part 1 of their full-group album 'ASSEMBLE26 'LOVE & POP'' at 6 PM KST on June 1, label MODHAUS announced. The mini album features all 24 members and marks the starting point of a new project, with a total of three albums planned to complete the narrative. The title track 'Baby Flower' is a song dedicated to girls going through growing pains.
