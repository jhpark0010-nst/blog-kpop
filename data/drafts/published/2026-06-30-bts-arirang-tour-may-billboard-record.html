BTS has set another unprecedented record on the Billboard Box Score, further cementing their status as a global top artist. According to Billboard's report published on June 29 (local time), BTS's world tour 'BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG' topped the Top Tours chart for May — following their No. 1 finish in April. May tour revenue increased 68% compared to the previous month, with attendance up 54%. During May, the group performed across four cities: El Paso, Stanford, and Las Vegas in the United States, as well as Mexico City, Mexico.

According to detailed Billboard Box Score data, BTS's 12 performances held from May 2 to May 28 generated a total tour gross of $127.8 million (approximately 197.8 billion Korean won). This marks the highest monthly tour gross ever recorded by a group since the chart was established in 2019. Notably, this figure surpasses by 35% the previous record of $95 million (approximately 147.1 billion Korean won) set by legendary British rock band The Rolling Stones in August 2019.

With this achievement, BTS has now claimed the No. 1 spot on Billboard's Top Tours chart a total of four times. Their previous chart-toppers came in May 2019 (with 'LOVE YOURSELF: SPEAK YOURSELF'), April 2022 (with 'PERMISSION TO DANCE ON STAGE'), and then April and May of this year. Across the entire global music market, only six acts have topped this chart four or more times: Bad Bunny, Beyoncé, Coldplay, Elton John, Trans-Siberian Orchestra, and BTS.

City-by-city performance figures were equally dominant. BTS's Las Vegas shows claimed the No. 1 spot in the Top Box Score category. The four Las Vegas performances held on May 23–24 and May 27–28 generated $49.5 million (approximately 76.6 billion Korean won). Stanford Stadium, Estadio GNP Seguros in Mexico City, and Sun Bowl Stadium in El Paso swept positions 2 through 4 on the chart, demonstrating the group's unrivaled ticket-selling power.

The Las Vegas stadium is particularly notable — BTS also swept both the Top Box Score and Top Tours No. 1 spots there with four shows back in April 2022. Despite performing the same number of shows at the same venue four years later, revenue grew by 38% and attendance by 23%, reaffirming the group's continued growth.

Billboard reported that cumulative revenue for 'BTS WORLD TOUR ARIRANG' since its opening on April 9 has reached approximately $204 million (approximately 315.8 billion Korean won). The outlet projected that 'with more than 50 additional shows scheduled this year, this tour looks set to easily surpass the group's own all-time record gross of $213.9 million set by BTS WORLD TOUR LOVE YOURSELF.'
